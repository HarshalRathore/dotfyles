# Data Silos Problem

The organizational challenge of data being trapped in isolated systems, departments, or formats, preventing AI applications from accessing the comprehensive data they need. With generative AI models capable of consuming vast amounts of data, data silos become a critical blocker to effective AI deployment. ^[extracted]

## The Problem

Traditional ML systems could work with relatively narrow, well-defined datasets. Generative AI applications need broad, cross-domain data access — customer data, company policies, integration data, real-time information. When this data is siloed:

- Agents cannot provide comprehensive, personalized responses
- Company policy enforcement becomes inconsistent
- Brand representation varies across touchpoints
- Time to market increases because data must be integrated per-application ^[extracted]

## The New Imperative

> No longer can we afford to be in a silo. ^[extracted]

This is not just a technical problem but a business imperative. Organizations that maintain data silos cannot build effective generative AI applications, regardless of which model they choose.

## Solution Approach

- **Data unification** — Break down silos through integration platforms and data lakes
- **Data governance** — Establish policies for data access, quality, and sharing
- **Platform approach** — Use platforms (e.g., Amazon Bedrock) that provide unified data access patterns ^[extracted]
