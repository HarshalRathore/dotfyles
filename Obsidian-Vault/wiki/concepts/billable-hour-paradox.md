# Billable Hour Paradox

The title of the Alix Partners AIEF2025 talk captures a fundamental tension in professional services: AI makes the billable hour economically unsustainable, yet the billable hour remains the dominant pricing model. The paradox is that AI simultaneously kills the economics of billable-hour billing while making billable-hour firms more productive than ever.

The mechanism: AI compresses the data-ingestion and normalization phase of consulting engagements from approximately 50% of total engagement time down to 10-20%. Under a billable-hour model, this compression directly reduces revenue for the same scope of work. Yet the compressed time enables 100% data coverage (instead of 20% sampling), better insights, and freed capacity for higher-value analysis.

The talk's title suggests the billable hour is "dead" as an economic model for AI-transformed services, but "long live" it because firms will continue to use it — likely with restructured engagement terms, fixed-fee pricing for AI-accelerated phases, or hybrid models that separate AI-driven work from human judgment work.

This paradox is specific to professional services and knowledge work more broadly.
