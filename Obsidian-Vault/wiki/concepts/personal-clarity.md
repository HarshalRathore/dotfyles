## Personal Clarity

Personal Clarity is the concept that effective product building requires a singular individual to carry deep clarity before it can propagate to teams, stakeholders, and organizations. This is not a team-level or org-level phenomenon — it starts with one person.

### Three Dimensions

Martin identifies three dimensions of personal clarity:

1. **Clarity of Vision** — Knowing what you are building and why. This is the "what" and "why" that gives you energy to push forward.
2. **Clarity of Purpose** — Understanding the deeper reason for the product's existence and its value to users.
3. **Clarity of Taste** — Having a refined sense of what good looks like, even when no one else can see it yet. Martin plans to elaborate on this concept separately in her talk.

### The Energy Model

Martin frames personal clarity not as a cognitive state but as an energy source. When you know the what and why of what you're building, "that's real energy." This energy is what allows you to push your team, push your stakeholders, and push your users — because building a product that meaningfully exists in people's lives is a "forceful" experience that requires sustained effort against resistance.

### Real-World Example

Raiza Martin's own experience building [[entities/notebooklm]] (codenamed "Tailwind") at Google exemplifies this concept. Despite "fascinatingly high" numbers of people telling her the product was stupid — users, stakeholders, teammates — her personal clarity about the tool she wanted to build (a place where you put docs and slides and chat with them to get something valuable) gave her the energy to keep driving forward.

### Organizational Implications

The concept challenges the common assumption that clarity must emerge from team alignment or organizational consensus. Instead, Martin argues that clarity starts with a singular individual who carries it relentlessly. Technology, hiring, and organizational structure are all "just transformed energy that comes from people."
