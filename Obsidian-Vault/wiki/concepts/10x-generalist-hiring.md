# 10X Generalist Hiring

The hiring principle that a lean company should either hire right or not at all — specifically, hiring 10X generalists with multiple complementary skill spikes in related fields.^[extracted]

## The Principle

Rather than hiring specialists who fill narrow roles, tiny teams need people whose complementary skills can shape and drive 10X outputs within the team.^[extracted] Examples from Olive:

- Product engineers are full-stack developers, great product thinkers, AND strong in computer network fundamentals.^[extracted]
- Marketers can code.^[extracted]
- Designers can build.^[extracted]

## Rationale

In a tiny team, every person must operate across multiple domains. A specialist who cannot operate outside their narrow role creates dependency bottlenecks that destroy the advantages of a small team.^[inferred] Complementary spikes allow each person to contribute meaningfully across product, engineering, and marketing without requiring additional headcount.^[inferred]

## Relationship to AI

AI tooling amplifies this principle: a 10X generalist with AI tools becomes a 100X contributor.^[extracted] The combination of broad human capability + AI augmentation creates output that previously required large teams.^[inferred]
