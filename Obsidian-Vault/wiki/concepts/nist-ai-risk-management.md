The NIST AI Risk Management Framework (AI RMF), released in 2023, provides a structured approach for organizations to manage risks associated with AI systems throughout their lifecycle. It was developed through a collaborative process involving industry, academia, and government stakeholders, and is a cornerstone of [[concepts/ai-governance|U.S. AI governance]] policy.

The framework is organized around four functions:

1. **Map** — Contextualize the AI system's purpose, scope, and operating environment.
2. **Measure** — Assess and characterize AI risks using appropriate metrics and tools.
3. **Manage** — Prioritize and treat identified risks through mitigation strategies.
4. **Govern** — Establish and maintain AI governance policies and oversight mechanisms.

The AI RMF is referenced in U.S. government AI governance policy, including OMB Memoranda M-25-21 and M-25-22. It provides the foundational risk taxonomy that agencies use when developing their AI implementation strategies during the 180-day rule-making period mandated by the memoranda.

For national security labs like [[entities/los-alamos-national-laboratory|Los Alamos National Laboratory]], the framework provides a structured way to assess AI risks in the context of classified systems where failures have kinetic consequences.
