## Meeting Minimalism

Meeting minimalism is the intentional strategy of keeping team calendars as empty as possible to protect deep focus time. The principle is that if you hired great people to build, you should let them build rather than making them talk about building for hours every day.

### Implementation

At [[entities/gumloop|Gumloop]], the founder's calendar is always busy because he handles customer-facing activities (flying to meet customers in person, running deep research reports before meetings). But everyone else's calendar should ideally be "totally blank" — no standing meetings, no syncs, no status updates.

### Why It Only Works at Small Scale

Meeting minimalism is only possible with small teams. In larger organizations, multiple people on a project must sync and agree on terms before starting work. This coordination requirement creates meeting density that makes deep focus time impossible. Small teams skip this — each person can move independently without team-wide alignment meetings.

### Connection to Small Team Scaling

Meeting minimalism is a direct enabler of [[concepts/small-team-scaling|small team scaling]]. It compounds the velocity advantage of small teams by ensuring that the people who make the team small are actually building, not coordinating. This practice complements [[concepts/internal-automation|internal automation]] by reducing coordination overhead even further.
