# AI Chemistry

**AI Chemistry** refers to the application of artificial intelligence to chemical simulation and discovery, where the cost of experimentation drops to near zero for molecular systems beyond 20 atoms, and assemblies at the 10,000-atom scale begin to exhibit life-like properties. ^[extracted]

## The 20-Atom Threshold

Below approximately 20 atoms, classical computational chemistry methods (density functional theory, ab initio methods) are tractable. Above this threshold, the combinatorial complexity of molecular interactions makes traditional simulation prohibitively expensive. AI models bridge this gap by learning the underlying physical rules from data, enabling accurate prediction without exhaustive computation. ^[extracted]

## Life-Like Assemblies at 10,000 Atoms

At the scale of 10,000 atoms, AI-simulated molecular assemblies begin to exhibit behaviors that resemble biological systems — self-organization, catalytic cycles, and information transfer. This represents a frontier where the boundary between synthetic chemistry and biology blurs, with implications for drug discovery, materials science, and origin-of-life research. ^[extracted]

## Implications

- **Democratized discovery**: Researchers can explore vast chemical spaces computationally before committing to wet-lab synthesis. ^[extracted]
- **Accelerated drug development**: Target binding, toxicity prediction, and lead optimization become high-throughput computational problems. ^[extracted]
- **New materials**: Properties of novel compounds can be predicted without synthesizing them first. ^[extracted]
