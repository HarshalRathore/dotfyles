# Turing Award

The **Turing Award** is the highest honor in computer science, often called the "Nobel Prize of Computing." It is awarded by the Association for Computing Machinery (ACM) for contributions of lasting importance to computing.

## Rich Sutton

**Rich Sutton**, author of The Bitter Lesson, is a Turing Award winner. He is recognized as a pioneer of reinforcement learning. Omar Khattab refers to him as "the current year's Turing Award winner" in his AIEF 2025 talk. ^[extracted]

## Significance

Sutton's Turing Award recognizes his foundational contributions to reinforcement learning — the very field that produced the empirical evidence for The Bitter Lesson. The irony (as Khattab notes) is that the award-winning work demonstrates that hard-coding domain knowledge is a liability, yet Sutton himself is honored for a highly technical contribution. ^[inferred]
