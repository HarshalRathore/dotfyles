# Gardening as Design Paradigm

A speculative direction for AI UX design, inspired by [[entities/dario-amodei|Dario Amodei]]'s observation that generative AI systems are "more grown than built." [[entities/maximillian-piras|Maximillian Piras]] suggests this framing points toward a fundamental shift in how designers approach AI interfaces: moving from procedural construction (designing every state and flow) to a gardening metaphor (setting constraints, goals, and conditions for generative systems to grow within). ^[extracted]

## The Brett Victor Connection

Piras draws on [[entities/brett-victor|Brett Victor]]'s "Future of Programming" talk, which argues that programmers should stop thinking in procedures and start thinking in goals and constraints — "guiding programs with higher layers of abstraction." Designers, Piras suggests, are well-positioned for this shift because design systems and user stories are already constraint-setting tools. ^[extracted]

## Potential Mechanisms

- **Design systems as RL constraints** — a design system could serve as a reinforcement learning boundary for generative UI, ensuring quality assurance on model-produced interfaces. ^[inferred]
- **User stories as system prompts** — the user story format (who, what, why) maps naturally to the system prompt structure that guides model behavior. Translating user stories into model prompts could make the design intent explicit to the AI. ^[inferred]

The gardening paradigm is proposed as the path beyond the [[concepts/bitter-layout|bitter layout]] — the moment when models commoditize enough that interface innovation becomes the differentiator again. ^[inferred]
