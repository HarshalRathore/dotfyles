# Reliability Is Revenue

The thesis that model reliability is the direct driver of revenue in AI applications — achieving 99.9% reliability for common tasks unlocks more economic value than marginal intelligence gains on hard tasks.

## Articulation

Federalist AI articulated this position at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025: "Reliability is revenue. For every use case you unlock and find, you're going to do a billion-dollar app in e-commerce or in B2B sales" ^[extracted].

## Core Argument

1. **Frontier models are already smart enough** — they can do PhD-level math ^[extracted]
2. **But reliability is unacceptable** — a 45% success rate is like using DoorDash where your order goes missing or you get 100 pizzas ^[extracted]
3. **The money is in boring tasks done perfectly** — booking airline tickets, sorting emails, filing taxes and invoices ^[extracted]
4. **Each reliable use case = a billion-dollar app** — in e-commerce, B2B sales, and other practical domains ^[extracted]

## Implications

- AI engineering investment should target reliability engineering over capability expansion
- Smaller, specialized models with high reliability are more economically viable than massive general models with inconsistent output
- The cost structure of AI changes: 1000x lower inference cost (CoreKey 72B) makes reliable AI economically viable at scale ^[extracted]
