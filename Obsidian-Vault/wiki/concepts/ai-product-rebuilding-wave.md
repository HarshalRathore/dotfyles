## AI Product Rebuilding Wave

The AI Product Rebuilding Wave is the prediction that every software product currently in use will undergo a complete rebuild phase, driven by the interaction possibilities that AI makes available.

### The Thesis: Everything Is Ugly

Martin's core thesis is that "everything we are using is the ugliest that it will ever be." This is because every product was created in the pre-AI era, where interactions were constrained by what was technically feasible. Pressing a button to get a "J" on screen feels strange when you can now describe what you want and get something magical back.

### The Analogy: The AI Microwave

Martin uses the analogy of a microwave: in a world where AI understands intent, we might want an "AI microwave" that knows what we need rather than requiring us to learn its interface. The point is that the richness of interactions now possible makes current products feel awkward and constrained.

### What Gets Rebuilt

Every layer of the Product Multi-Layer Cake is being rebuilt:
- **Self**: What it means to be a PM, engineer, or designer is being rewritten
- **Team**: Teams now include invisible AI participants
- **Product**: The translation from idea to technology is fundamentally different
- **User**: User expectations have been raised by AI-native products

### The Opportunity

The rebuilding wave creates enormous opportunity for product builders. The "best products out there haven't been discovered yet" — the current generation of products is just the awkward adolescence of AI-native software. The products that will define the next era are still being imagined.

### The Cost

This rebuilding is "pretty fucking hard." Each layer is being effectively rewritten, and that is not without cost. Role uncertainty, organizational disruption, and the discomfort of not knowing what jobs will look like next year are real challenges.
