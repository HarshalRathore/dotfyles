# PM Transformation in the AI Era

**PM transformation in the AI era** is the thesis that product management as a discipline must undergo a profound transformation when building products powered by LLMs and AI agents. This is not a incremental change to PM practices — it's a fundamental shift in how products are conceived, specified, and developed. ^[extracted]

## The Core Thesis

Ben Stein: "I think what's happening is the product management discipline is going to undergo a transformation, a shift in evolution, whatever you call it, that is super profound." ^[extracted]

## What's Changing

| Traditional PM | AI-Era PM |
|---|---|
| Spec-driven feature development | Affordance-based design |
| Known input space, bounded interface | Boundless surface area, free-text input |
| Behavior is deterministic | Behavior is emergent |
| PRDs describe exact flows | PRDs need new affordances for open-ended behavior |
| Build what you specify | Discover what emerges |
| Evals are optional | Evals are the primary feedback loop |

## Two Structural Forces

1. **LLM opacity** — The foundation layer is inherently unpredictable. We don't know what LLMs can do, and we can never fully know. ^[extracted]
2. **Boundless expectations** — A text input invites unlimited use cases. Give one affordance and users want it everywhere. ^[extracted]

## The Engineering World's Partial Awareness

Stein notes that the engineering world is "starting to squint and understanding maybe how the discipline is changing" through tools like IDE assistants and code generation. But "we don't really understand how product development is changing and evolving, and what are the new tools and practices." ^[extracted]
