## Open Source Book Player

At AIEF2025, [[entities/lukasz-gandecki|Łukasz Gądecki]] announced that TheBrain.pro was open-sourcing its book player — the software that renders [[concepts/multimodal-reading-experience|multimodal book experiences]] with AI-generated graphics, music, character avatars, and interactive voice Q&A.

### What It Enables

Anyone can use the open-source player to create Netflix-style interactive book experiences. The player handles:
- Rendering text with synchronized AI-generated visuals
- Playing AI-generated background music that changes per scene
- Displaying character avatars when characters appear
- Handling voice Q&A interaction
- Navigation between scenes and reading positions

### Significance

This opens the door for publishers, educators, and independent creators to produce rich book experiences without building the player from scratch. The barrier to creating immersive reading experiences drops from "requires a full AI engineering team" to "requires the player + content pipeline."

Gądecki's closing pitch: "If you want AI that feels like magic, not like chatbots, come talk to me. We build AI experiences that ship in the light and not slides."
