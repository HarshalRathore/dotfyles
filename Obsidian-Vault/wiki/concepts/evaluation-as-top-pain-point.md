# Evaluation as Top Pain Point

The 2025 State of AI Engineering Survey asked AI engineers to name the **number one most painful thing about AI engineering today**. **Evaluation topped the list** — confirming that despite significant progress in eval tooling and methodology, evaluation remains the field's central challenge.

## Why Evaluation Is Painful

- **Subjectivity**: AI outputs are often difficult to evaluate objectively, especially for creative or reasoning tasks
- **Scale**: As systems become more complex and autonomous, the number of evaluation dimensions grows
- **Human review dominance**: Despite the availability of automated eval tools, human review remains the most popular evaluation method — suggesting automated approaches are not yet sufficient
- **Cost**: Human evaluation is expensive and slow, creating bottlenecks in development cycles

## Implications for the Field

The fact that evaluation tops the pain list validates the focus of conferences like AIEF2025 on evals, monitoring, and observability. It also suggests that the next breakthrough in AI engineering may come from better evaluation infrastructure rather than better models.
