# Model Capability Awareness

The skill of understanding the limits and capabilities of AI models and agents — a core component of vibe engineering that most developers lack. ^[extracted]

## The Skill Stack

Vibe engineering is not just writing English. It requires a combination of skills:

- **Model limits** — Knowing what the model can and cannot do
- **Agent capabilities** — Understanding what the agent tool can and cannot do
- **Context management** — Knowing what context to pass and how much
- **Prompt engineering** — Writing effective technical prompts (not just "fix the app")
- **Technical knowledge** — Domain expertise to steer the models effectively
- **Ecosystem familiarity** — Being "chronically untwere" — familiar enough with the ecosystem to understand what's going on ^[extracted]

## Why It Matters

Vibe engineering only works if the engineer knows what they're doing. ^[extracted] A vibe coder with an unreliable model makes wrong decisions fast — not useful. The engineer's domain knowledge makes them uniquely qualified to evaluate AI output because the generated code reflects their own knowledge and limitations. ^[inferred]

## The "Chronically Untwere" Requirement

Kitze uses the term "chronically untwere" to describe the level of ecosystem familiarity needed. Without it, you cannot understand what's going on in the AI coding space — what tools are good, what models are capable, what patterns work. ^[extracted] This is not a technical skill per se, but a form of domain immersion that comes from sustained engagement with the ecosystem.
