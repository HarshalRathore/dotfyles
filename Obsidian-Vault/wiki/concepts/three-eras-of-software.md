# Three Eras of Software

The **three eras of software** framework divides the history of software development into three periods, each defined by its primary constraint: ^[extracted]

| Era | Constraint | Description |
|-----|-----------|-------------|
| 1. Machine-limited | Computing power | Software was constrained by available hardware |
| 2. Human-brain-limited | Human cognitive capacity | Software was constrained by what humans could design and maintain |
| 3. AI frontier | What AI can do | Software is constrained by AI capabilities |

This framework was presented at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2026. ^[extracted]

## Era 1: Machine-Limited

In the earliest era, the primary constraint was raw computing power. Software had to be efficient because hardware was expensive and scarce. This era includes the early days of computing, where every byte and cycle mattered.

## Era 2: Human-Brain-Limited

As hardware became cheaper, the constraint shifted to human cognitive capacity. Software became limited by:

- How many people could work on a project
- How much code a human could understand
- How fast humans could iterate and debug

This era includes the modern software development boom, where the bottleneck was human talent, not machine power.

## Era 3: AI Frontier

In the current era, the constraint is what AI can do. AI agents are now part of the software development process, and the question is no longer "what can humans build?" but "what can humans + AI build?" ^[inferred]

This era is characterized by:
- AI-assisted coding (Cursor, Copilot, Codex, Claude Code)
- Agent-driven development workflows
- The **10×–20× productivity** gains from AI agents
- The shift from "IDEs with slight improvements" to "chat-style engineering"

## Connection to Sovereign AI

In the AI frontier era, sovereignty over the AI pipeline becomes as important as sovereignty over the compute pipeline. **Sovereign AI** — using local models for full pipeline control — is the strategic response to this new era. ^[inferred]
