# From Rule-Based to Adaptive Technology

The paradigm shift from technology as a deterministic, rule-based system to technology as an adaptive, evolving entity capable of growth and contextual understanding. ^[extracted]

## The Rule-Based Limitation

Traditional software is shackled to explicit rules: it does the same thing repeatedly, following predetermined logic paths. This limitation is not a technical deficiency but a philosophical one — it assumes the world can be fully described by rules written by humans. ^[extracted]

## The Adaptive Alternative

Adaptive technology — powered by AI/ML — operates differently: it learns from data, evolves its behavior, and can handle situations not explicitly programmed. This is the shift from "technology as tool" to "technology as partner." ^[extracted]

## The Evaluation Imperative

Adaptive systems introduce a new challenge: because they are not deterministic, their behavior must be evaluated continuously rather than verified once at development time. This is why evals are essential — they are the mechanism for ensuring adaptive systems remain reliable as they evolve. ^[inferred]
