# Agent Negotiation

Agent negotiation is the frontier of agentic commerce: adding intelligence to decision-making at each component so that consumers and merchants have not just intents and preferences, but intelligence that can reason over them and negotiate them. ^[extracted]

## The Final Piece

Agent negotiation is described as "the frontier" — the last piece needed to make commerce truly agentic. Without it, agents can execute transactions but cannot independently reason about what to buy, at what price, or from which merchant. ^[extracted]

## Requirements

For agent negotiation to work, the system needs:

1. **Intelligent buyer agents** that can reason over preferences and translate them to specific purchases
2. **Intelligent seller agents** that can express real-time strategic goals and dynamically adjust pricing/availability
3. **Market design infrastructure** that manages the differences between buyer and seller preferences ^[extracted]

## Analogy

This mirrors financial markets where market makers and third-party institutions manage differences between buyers and sellers through intelligent intermediation rather than naive trust in disclosed information. ^[inferred]
