# Enterprise AI Productivity Paradox

The enterprise AI productivity paradox describes the gap between massive AI investment and measured business outcomes. Three data points from the Alix Partners AIEF2025 talk illustrate the paradox: 89% of CEOs plan to implement agentic AI (Deloitte), yet the National Bureau of Economic Research reports no significant impact on earnings or recorded hours, BCG finds three quarters of companies fail to scale Gen AI value, and S&P Global reports almost half are abandoning AI initiatives.^[inferred]

The proposed resolution is a distinction between **employee productivity** (individual tool-assisted output) and **enterprise productivity** (end-to-end workflow transformation across the organization). AI tools that merely make individual workers faster don't resolve the paradox — only systemic workflow redesign does.^[inferred]

This maps onto the consulting engagement model's three-phase structure: data ingestion, analysis/hypothesis generation, and recommendation/deliverable. AI compresses the first phase most dramatically (from ~50% to 10-20% of engagement time), but enterprise value accrues when AI transforms the full pipeline — enabling 100% data coverage instead of 20% sampling, and freeing human expertise for higher-value work across all phases.

The Alix Partners team's approach — building an internal GenAI platform with 20 engineers, 50 deployments, hundreds of users — exemplifies the enterprise productivity path: surgical deployment with business-partner relationships, converting skeptics into champions through demonstrated accuracy gains.
