## Forceful Product Building

Forceful Product Building is the concept that creating a product that meaningfully exists in people's lives is not a gentle or collaborative process — it requires force. Raiza Martin initially considered calling it a "violent" experience but toned it down after her team pushed back.

### The Core Idea

To truly build something that can meaningfully exist as a product — one that has a real place in people's lives — you must force it into existence. This is not about hobby projects or incremental features. It's about the sustained, forceful push required against resistance from every direction: users, stakeholders, teammates, and even your own doubts.

### The Energy Source

Forceful product building draws its energy from Personal Clarity. When you know the what and why of what you're building, that clarity becomes "real energy" that fuels the forceful push. Without this energy, the resistance is overwhelming.

### The Product Person's Role

Martin reframes the product person's job not as managing features or coordinating teams, but as finding "the nugget of opportunity" and "exploding it like a popcorn kernel." This is the singular job of anyone who embodies the force behind a product — whether you call yourself a PM, engineer, designer, or something else.

### The Chaos Context

In the AI era, every layer of product building is being rewritten simultaneously. Role uncertainty (will there still be PMs? engineers? designers?) makes the forceful push harder because the ground is constantly moving. But complementary to chaos is always opportunity.
