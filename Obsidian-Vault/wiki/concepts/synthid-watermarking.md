# SynthID Watermarking

SynthID is Google's digital watermarking technology for synthetically generated media. It provides machine-detectable provenance for AI-generated images, video, and audio, enabling verification of whether content was generated by an AI model.

## How It Works

SynthID embeds a digital watermark into generated content during the generation process itself — the watermark is intrinsic to the generated output rather than added as a post-processing step. This makes it:
- **Robust** — survives common transformations (compression, cropping, format changes)
- **Machine-detectable** — can be verified programmatically
- **Intrinsic** — embedded at generation time, not added afterward

## Applications

- **Veo 3** — SynthID watermarks on synthetic video
- **Lyria 2** — SynthID watermarks on synthetic audio assets
- **Imagine 4** — Implied watermarking on synthetic images (consistent with Google's responsible AI approach)

## Responsible AI Context

SynthID is part of Google DeepMind's broader responsible AI framework, which also includes:
- **Human-visible watermarks** — subtle visual indicators of synthetic content
- **Artist collaboration** — working with creative industry professionals (Darren Aronofsky, Ross Lovegrove) rather than operating outside it
