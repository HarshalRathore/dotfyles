# Startup Grit and Resilience

**Startup grit and resilience** is the capacity of a team to endure extreme operational chaos — rapid growth, support overload, near-shutdown anxiety — while maintaining focus on what matters. Eric Simons describes it as "the foundation underneath chaos."

## The 300-Moment

Simons compares the Bolt.new scaling experience to the movie *300*: a small group of people surrounded by tens of thousands of threats. At month two:
- 30-40K active customers
- Fewer than 20 team members
- No dedicated support or success personnel
- Simons and his chief of staff handling support tickets

Grit is what keeps the team functioning in this environment. Without it, the team would have collapsed under the pressure.

## Grit vs. Normal Resilience

Normal resilience handles predictable stress. Startup grit handles the unpredictable:
- **Unknown scale** — you don't know how big "too big" is until you're there
- **No precedent** — no one on the team has done this before
- **Simultaneous crises** — good fires (too many customers) and bad fires (broken systems)
- **Near-death anxiety** — the company was considering shutting down weeks before the launch

## The Competitor Contrast

StackBlitz's IDE competitor was acquired two weeks before Bolt.new launched. They were "on the same trajectory" and "would have been meaningful competitors" — but they lacked the grit and resilience to survive long enough. Grit is not just about working hard; it's about persisting when the outcome is genuinely uncertain.

## Building Grit

Grit comes from shared struggle:
- **Bootstrapping experience** — Simons' previous company went from zero to couches to survival
- **Seven years together** — shared history builds deep resilience
- **Shared values** — low ego, high trust, user obsession create a culture that doesn't fracture under pressure
