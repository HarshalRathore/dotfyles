# Cascading Hierarchy

The **cascading hierarchy** is the traditional startup scaling pattern where each new leadership level hires their own management layer, creating an exponential growth in headcount. ^[extracted] It is the default organizational model that [[concepts/first-principles-org-design|first-principles org design]] rejects.

## The Pattern

1. Organization grows → bring on a VP
2. VP hires directors
3. Directors hire direct reports
4. This cascades across every function

The result: a small team can become a much larger organization overnight. ^[extracted]

## Characteristics

- **Exponential headcount growth** — Each new level multiplies the team size
- **Layered management** — Multiple tiers between leadership and individual contributors
- **Function duplication** — Every department (engineering, sales, marketing) builds its own hierarchy
- **Blitzscaling acceleration** — Rapid hiring phases amplify the cascading effect

## Why It's Problematic in the AI Era

Grant Lee ([[entities/grant-lee|Grant Lee]], [[entities/gamma|Gamma]]) argues this model is outdated because:

- AI tools dramatically increase individual output, reducing the need for headcount
- Fast-moving technology requires leaders close to the work, not distant managers
- Management layers create coordination overhead that slows down teams
- Traditional hierarchy assumes output scales linearly with headcount — AI breaks this assumption

## The Alternative

Instead of cascading hierarchy, AI-era companies use:

- [[concepts/generalist-vs-specialist|generalist hiring]] — Fewer, broader team members
- [[concepts/player-coach-model|player-coach leadership]] — Leaders who also do hands-on work
- [[concepts/small-team-scaling|output-based scaling]] — Grow relative to output, not headcount
