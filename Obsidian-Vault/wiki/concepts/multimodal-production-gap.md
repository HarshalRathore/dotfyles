# Multimodal Production Gap

The **multimodal production gap** describes the significant disparity between text-based AI model adoption in production versus other modalities (image, video, audio). This gap persists even when accounting for organizations that have multimodal models in production but have not yet garnered meaningful traction.

## Key Data

From the 2025 State of AI Engineering Survey (500 respondents):

- **Text** leads all modalities in production usage by a wide margin
- **Image, video, and audio** usage all lag text by significant margins
- When broken down by adoption intent, **audio has the highest intent to adopt**: 37% of non-audio users have a plan to eventually adopt audio
- This foreshadows an expected "audio wave" in AI engineering

## Implications

The gap suggests that while text remains the dominant modality for production AI, the field is poised for multimodal expansion — particularly audio, where adoption intent is strongest. This aligns with broader trends in voice AI development and the growing maturity of multimodal model architectures.
