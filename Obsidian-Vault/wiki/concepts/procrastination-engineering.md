# Procrastination Engineering

**Procrastination engineering** is the practice of channeling procrastination into productive engineering work. Instead of avoiding a task by doomscrolling, the engineer uses the "wasted" time to build something useful — often a side project that becomes a real product.

## Origin Story

[[Christopher Chedeau|christopher-chedeau]] described this approach during Facebook's performance review cycle. Knowing he wouldn't write his reviews until the last minute, he chose to spend the intervening time building [[Excalidraw|excalidraw]] rather than passively scrolling on his phone. The result: a tool that went viral on Twitter and became widely adopted during the COVID-19 remote work shift.

## Key Insight

The procrastination engineering pattern reveals a counterintuitive truth: the time you'd normally waste on low-value activities can be redirected into high-value side projects if you acknowledge the procrastination upfront and plan for it. The catalyst doesn't have to be grand — Chedeau's entire open-source whiteboard tool was born from a service being down while he tried to write a blog post.
