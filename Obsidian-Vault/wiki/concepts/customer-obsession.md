# Customer Obsession

Customer obsession is the practice of maintaining extreme proximity to customers, treating their problems as your own, and going to extraordinary lengths to solve them — even when it means compressing months of work into hours. ^[extracted]

## Stripe's Example

[[entities/stripe|Stripe]] practiced customer obsession from its earliest days. The engineering team added all customers on [[entities/gchat|Gchat]], maintaining constant contact. ^[extracted] [[entities/patrick-collison|Patrick Collison]] would personally visit customer offices to install Stripe — so rare and intense that it became an [[concepts/urban-legend|urban legend]] within the company. ^[extracted]

The Wells Fargo integration exemplified this philosophy: rather than accepting the bank's nine-month timeline, the Stripe team completed the entire integration in 24 hours, working through the night. ^[extracted] When the initial certification failed, they didn't wait a week — they rescheduled two hours later and passed. ^[extracted]

## The Principle

Customer obsession means:
- **Proximity** — being in constant contact with users, not just periodic check-ins
- **Empathy** — treating customer problems as urgent as your own
- **Speed** — refusing to accept arbitrary timelines that don't match the actual scope of work
- **Persistence** — continuing until the problem is solved, not until the process says it's done

This approach is distinct from standard customer support; it's engineering-level problem solving driven by customer needs rather than product roadmaps. ^[inferred]
