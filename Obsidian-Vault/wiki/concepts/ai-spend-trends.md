# AI Spend Trends

Despite media narratives about an AI bubble, enterprise AI spending continues to increase decisively across surveyed organizations. ^[extracted]

## KPMG Pulse Survey Data

KPMG's quarterly pulse survey of enterprises with $1B+ revenue tracks intended AI spend over the next 12 months:

| Period | Intended Spend ($M) | Change |
|--------|---------------------|--------|
| Q4 2024 | 88 | — |
| Q1 2025 | 114 | +30% |
| Q3 2025 | 130 | +14% |

The trajectory is clear: intended spend has grown 48% in less than a year, accelerating even as bubble narratives intensify. ^[extracted]

## Deloitte Data

Deloitte's survey shows that **90%+ of organizations** intend to increase their AI spend in the next 12 months. This near-unanimity among surveyed enterprises suggests that AI investment is treated as a non-negotiable strategic priority rather than an optional experiment. ^[extracted]

## Interpretation

The spending trajectory contradicts the AI bubble narrative. Organizations are not reducing investment despite concerns — they are increasing it. This suggests that enterprise leaders view AI spending as a long-term strategic necessity, not a speculative bet that can be easily scaled back.
