## Product-Led Hiring

Product-led hiring is the strategy of recruiting from your existing customer base and user community. When your product is genuinely useful, the best candidates are people who already love it and understand how it can be used in business contexts.

### Mechanism

The core insight is that customers who discover and adopt a product develop deep product intuition. They understand its strengths, its gaps, and its business applications. When they join the company, they don't need to be "inspired to join" — they want to join because they've already experienced the product's value.

### Examples

**Instacart customer → Gumloop employee.** The Instacart representative who originally discovered [[entities/gumloop|Gumloop]] and brought it into Instacart quit his job to join Gumloop. He now handles enterprise relationships and works with large customers, bringing deep domain knowledge.

**Webflow → Gumloop Head of Education.** The Head of Education and Community at Gumloop was previously at Webflow, where he ran a Zapier course and sold automation workshops. He discovered Gumloop, got excited about it, and joined the team. His existing expertise in automation education made him an obvious hire.

### Requirements

Product-led hiring requires a genuinely great product that is accessible to the right talent pool. There is a luck component — you need users who are also exceptional people. But when it works, it produces hires who know the product better than anyone on the team and don't need onboarding on what the company does. This approach is a key enabler of [[concepts/small-team-scaling|small team scaling]] because it reduces the need for traditional recruiting.
