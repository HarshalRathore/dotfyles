# AI Architect

The AI Architect is an emerging organizational role responsible for managing, coaching, and improving a company's AI agents. [[entities/clay-bavor|Clay Bavor]] (cofounder of [[entities/sierra-ai|Sierra]]) observed the role emerging organically across Sierra's customer base and named it, drawing a parallel to the **webmaster** role from the early internet era. ^[extracted]

> "It's the AI era and AI agent version of the webmaster." — Clay Bavor ^[extracted]

## The Three Hats

The AI Architect combines three distinct disciplines: ^[extracted]

1. **Technology** — Understanding what agents can do. This doesn't require pre-training models or deep LangChain expertise, but a feel for capabilities and limitations. ^[extracted]
2. **Experience, Aesthetics, and Design** — The agent is a brand ambassador. Decisions about voice, tone, values, persona, and how the agent creates human connection. One of Sierra's customers (Chubbies) gave their agent the persona "Duncan Smothers" with irreverent, bro-y humor. ^[extracted]
3. **Business Outcomes** — Driving measurable results from customer interactions. ^[extracted]

## Organizational Origins

The role tends to emerge from customer experience (CX) teams rather than engineering teams. Bavor notes this is "the area I've been most excited about" — people who have been close to the customer in service, support, and care settings are natural AI Architects because they combine customer empathy with a hands-on understanding of the technology. ^[extracted]

## Traits of Successful AI Architects

Based on Sierra's observation of successful customers: ^[extracted]

- **Risk tolerance** — "Not letting perfect be the enemy of the good." AI agents are probabilistic software, requiring comfort with uncertainty. ^[extracted]
- **Customer problem focus** — Starting with a concrete valuable problem (even as narrow as "processing a single return"), not a broad "apply AI to something" mandate. ^[extracted]
- **First-principles team design** — Re-architecting the customer experience team around supporting the AI agent, not shoehorning old structures onto new paradigms. This includes creating new roles like conversation reviewers who coach the agent on empathy and judgment. ^[extracted]
