# Generative Video vs Image Market

As of 2025, generative video is predicted to become 100–250× larger than the image generation market. This prediction is based on compute intensity, engagement metrics, and industry applicability.^[extracted]

## The Prediction

The core prediction: the generative video market will ultimately be 100× to 250× bigger than the image generation market. This is described as "rough math" but grounded in three factors:^[extracted]

1. **Compute intensity**: Video models are approximately 20× more compute-intensive than image models.
2. **Engagement**: Video is approximately 5× more engaging than static images.
3. **Industry applicability**: Video is useful to more industries than images.

Multiplying these factors (20 × 5 = 100, with industry applicability potentially adding another 2.5×) yields the 100–250× range.^[inferred]

## Current Trajectory

Video model usage is growing rapidly even while expensive and imperfect. File.ai's revenue data serves as a market proxy: video model usage grew from near-zero in October to 18% by February and approximately 30% at the time of the talk.^[extracted]

## Historical Parallel

The video generation landscape mirrors the image generation trajectory of 2022: OpenAI's Sora was perceived as far ahead (like DALL·E 2), but Yurtseven's prior experience taught him that such leads close quickly. The expectation is that video models will similarly commoditize, driving adoption and market size dramatically.^[extracted]
