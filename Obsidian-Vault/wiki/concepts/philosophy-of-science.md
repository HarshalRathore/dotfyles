# Philosophy of Science

The **philosophy of science** — particularly [[entities/karl-popper|Karl Popper]]'s conception of scientific progress through falsification and theory competition — was invoked at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2026 as the intellectual foundation for building automated scientific discovery systems ("Eureka Machines"). ^[extracted]

## Popperian Evolution of Theories

Popper's insight: theories evolve through competition, analogous to biological natural selection: ^[extracted]

> "Just like in other types of evolution, when we choose a theory, we also choose one that is best in competition with other theories... the theory that best holds its own — it's one that, just like evolution, has a certain natural selection process. It proves itself, and there is also a sort of survival of the fittest going on in scientific theories."

The process: we propose a new theory, hypothesis, explanation, or description, then subject it to rigorous empirical testing — that is the evolutionary pressure on scientific theories. ^[extracted]

## Science as the Driver of Technology

The chain: more science → more technology → more growth → more human flourishing. ^[extracted]

## The Problem: Widening Scope, Diminishing Attention

The exponential growth of science creates a fundamental problem: there are so many niche subfields now that it is very hard to get enough people working on any particular one. The number of people focusing on any single section of science has decreased as the total scope widens. ^[extracted]

This leads to the motivation for automating scientific discovery.

## The Eureka Machine

From this Popperian foundation, the WF2026 speaker proposed a "Eureka Machine" — an AI system that automates the scientific discovery process across physics, chemistry, biology, neuroscience, medicine, economics, and astrophysics. Four pillars: ^[extracted]

1. **Existing knowledge** — Understand what humanity has already discovered
2. **Scientific measurement data** — Ingest all available data
3. **Simulations** — For things that cannot yet be measured, build simulations that can be verified and solved with AI
4. **Physical lab** — Industrial labs to run real-world experiments

On top of these pillars, an agent swarm orchestrates all sources of knowledge, data, experimentation, and rewards. ^[extracted]
