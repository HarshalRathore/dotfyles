# Lean Startup Playbook

"The New Lean Startup" is a playbook for building profitable, scalable companies with minimal headcount, articulated by Sid Bendre of Olive.^[extracted] It is organized around three pillars: operating principles, organizational structure, and AI tooling augmentation.^[extracted]

## Pillar 1: Operating Principles

Six principles that form the bedrock of leanness:

1. **Hire right or not at all** — Only hire 10X generalists with multiple complementary skill spikes.^[extracted] Product engineers are full-stack developers, great product thinkers, and strong in fundamentals like computer networks. Marketers can code; designers can build.^[extracted]
2. **Profit-first mentality** — Profit is power and focus. It provides a clear mechanism for every decision and serves as the company's North Star.^[extracted]
3. **KPI alignment** — Everyone owns a KPI. Decisions must be validated against this metric. KPI alignment removes micromanagement because everyone is focused on moving their metric week over week.^[extracted]
4. **Continuous process refinement** — For any repeating process, always ask: how would we do this better? Failures are treated as system failures, enabling feedback loops for operational and technical improvement.^[extracted]
5. **Super tools** — Consolidate workflows onto one platform. This requires reinventing how old tools are used rather than adopting new ones.^[extracted]
6. **Don't learn it twice** — Invest in technical playbooks and operational blueprints that compound benefits across new products.^[extracted]

## Pillar 2: Organizational Structure

The harvester/cultivator model (inspired by Palantir), where harvesters own products end-to-end and cultivators build the company's agentic operating system.^[extracted] See [[concepts/harvester-cultivator-model|Harvester/Cultivator Model]] for details.

## Pillar 3: AI Tooling Augmentation

AI tools are used for task automation (script writing, campaign analysis, operations, code generation, communications).^[extracted] The philosophy is that tools make a 10Xer into a 100Xer, not that tools fill gaps in weaker hires.^[extracted]
