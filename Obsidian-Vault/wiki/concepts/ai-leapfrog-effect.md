# AI Leapfrog Effect

The AI leapfrog effect is the observation, articulated by [[entities/sarah-guo|Sarah Guo]], that the most conservative, low-tech industries appear to be adopting AI fastest — faster than more tech-savvy sectors.

## Evidence

Guo cites three portfolio companies as evidence: ^[extracted]

| Company | Industry | Traction |
|---|---|---|
| [[entities/harvey-ai|Harvey]] | Legal | $70M+ ARR, AI now essential for competitiveness |
| [[entities/sierra-ai|Sierra]] | Customer service | 70% query resolution, serving SiriusXM/ADT |
| [[entities/open-evidence|Open Evidence]] | Healthcare | 1/3 of US doctors weekly, daily usage |

## Why This Happens

The transcript doesn't fully explore causality, but the pattern suggests these industries have: ^[inferred]

- **High-volume, repeatable knowledge work** that maps naturally to current AI capabilities
- **Existing business voice/communication workflows** that couldn't scale before due to labor constraints
- **Clear economic value** from automation — legal research, customer service, medical literature review
- **Less "not invented here" resistance** — these industries haven't had good software alternatives, so AI is a genuine leap rather than a replacement

## Relationship to [[concepts/cursor-for-x|Cursor for X]]

The leapfrog effect supports the [[concepts/cursor-for-x|Cursor for X]] thesis — the biggest opportunities are in domains that haven't been well-served by software, where AI enables entirely new capabilities rather than improved versions of existing digital workflows. ^[inferred]
