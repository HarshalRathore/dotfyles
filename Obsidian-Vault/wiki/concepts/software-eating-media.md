# Software Eating Media

The thesis that "software has been eating media" describes the long-term transformation of media industries through software and digital technology. YouTube exemplifies this trend: generating more revenue from ads alone than any other media company except Disney — and unlike Disney, YouTube's revenue is almost entirely media-driven (Disney has parks, cruises, and other non-media businesses).^[extracted]

## YouTube as Evidence

YouTube's ad revenue alone exceeds that of most traditional media companies. This demonstrates that software-driven media distribution can outperform legacy media companies in revenue generation. The pattern suggests that each wave of software innovation in media (internet, mobile, AI) drives industry growth rather than contraction.^[extracted]

## Ad Industry Growth Pattern

Since 2000, every ad dollar spent has increased. The industry has grown threefold, with all growth concentrated in software ads. This pattern is predicted to repeat with AI-driven advertising: the industry will grow further, with most of that growth coming from AI-generated content.^[extracted]

## Implications for Generative Media

The "software eating media" thesis provides the macro-level context for generative media: just as software transformed media distribution and advertising, generative AI is transforming media creation. The next phase is AI-driven content production, following the pattern established by software-driven distribution.^[inferred]
