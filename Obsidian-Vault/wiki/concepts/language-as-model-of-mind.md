# Language as Model of Mind

[[entities/danielle-perszyk|Danielle Perszyk]] (Amazon AGI) argues that the evolution of language and the evolution of theory of mind (models of other minds) co-evolved in a mutually reinforcing flywheel. This integration of communication and representation systems is the origin of human intelligence's generality. ^[extracted]

## The Co-Evolution Flywheel

Perszyk describes the integration: "As we fine-tuned into social cues, our models of mind became more stable. This advanced our language and our language made our models of mind even more stable." ^[extracted]

The critical breakthrough: "Our models of mind became the original placeholder concept, the first variable for being able to represent any concept. That right there is generalization." ^[extracted]

## What Makes Human Language Different

Perszyk draws a sharp distinction between human language and other communication systems: ^[extracted]

> "Other communication systems don't have models of mind. Programming languages don't negotiate meaning in real time. This is why code is so easily verifiable. And LLMs don't understand language. What do we mean they don't understand language? They don't understand that words refer to things that minds make up." ^[extracted]

The key insight: "When we ask what's in a word, the answer is quite literally a mind." Words work because both speaker and listener have models of each other's minds — they can negotiate meaning, resolve ambiguity, and infer intent. ^[extracted]

## Implications for AI

If language's power comes from its connection to shared models of mind, then current LLMs — which process language statistically without any model of whose mind produced the words — are operating without the mechanism that makes language general and flexible. This supports Perszyk's argument that the next step for agents is to develop models of human minds. ^[inferred]
