# Solvay Conference Analogy

The analogy comparing the current moment in AI engineering to the **1927 Solvay Conference** in physics — where foundational figures gathered to establish ideas that would last decades.

## The Historical Parallel

[[entities/swyx|swyx]] invoked the 1927 Solvay Conference at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 opening keynote. The Solvay Conference brought together Einstein, Marie Curie, and other household names in physics at a pivotal moment. In the period from the 1940s to 1970s, they established the standard model of physics — foundational ideas that have not been fundamentally changed in the subsequent 50+ years.

## The Thesis

The analogy makes two claims:

1. **This is the right time.** Just as the Solvay Conference represented a convergence of the brightest minds at a pivotal moment in physics, the current AI engineering community represents a convergence of the best AI engineers, researchers, and practitioners.

2. **We can establish the standard model.** The question is not whether AI engineering needs standard models (it does — ETL, MVC, CRUD, MapReduce exist in software engineering) but what those models will be.

## Application to AI Engineering

swyx used this analogy to frame AIEF2025 as a moment for establishing AI engineering's standard model. The conference gathers "the best sort of AI engineers in the world, and researchers and all that, to build and push the frontier forward."

The thesis has been stated for two and a half years and "still true, still true today."
