## Work-Trial Interviews

Work-trial interviews are a hiring method where candidates spend several days working on the team as if they had already been hired. This allows both the candidate and the company to evaluate fit through actual collaboration rather than theoretical interview questions.

### Process

Candidates are brought into the team for several days — including off-site hacking sessions (e.g., renting Airbnbs for multi-day coding sessions). By the end of the work trial, the team is "totally confident" whether the person is the right fit.

### Advantages

- **Real collaboration signal.** Working together for days reveals whether someone is genuinely exceptional and a good cultural fit, which interviews alone cannot determine.
- **Mutual evaluation.** Both sides learn about each other — the candidate sees the team's actual working style, and the team sees the candidate's actual work quality.
- **High signal-to-noise.** Despite being resource-intensive (Gumloop has done "way too many" of these), work trials produce hiring decisions with high confidence.

### Connection to Small Team Scaling

Work-trial interviews are a critical component of [[concepts/small-team-scaling|small team scaling]] because they enable the "exceptional-only" hiring bar. When every person must be absolutely exceptional, you need a hiring process that tests actual work ability, not just problem-solving in an interview setting. This approach is closely related to [[concepts/product-led-hiring|product-led hiring]], as both methods prioritize deep evaluation of fit over traditional interview performance.
