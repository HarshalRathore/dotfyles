# Player-Coach Model

The **player-coach model** is a leadership approach where managers also do hands-on, individual-contributor work — rather than managing from a distance. The metaphor comes from American football, where a quarterback or linebacker on the field makes real-time adjustments without waiting for the head coach's call. ^[extracted]

## Why It Matters in the AI Era

In traditional organizations, decisions flow top-down: the head coach calls all the plays. But in AI, "the game on the field is moving incredibly fast." ^[extractatced] Leaders who are close to the work can:

- Read and react to changes in real time
- Adjust priorities without waiting for mandates
- Make technical trade-offs with deep context
- Mentor team members with nuanced understanding of what's in flight

## Implementation at Gamma

Every member of Gamma's core leadership team is a player-coach. ^[extracted] On the engineering side, these are leaders with significant management experience who still love to code and stay in the day-to-day work. ^[extracted]

### Benefits

- **Deep context** — Player-coaches know exactly what's happening because they're doing the work
- **Effective mentorship** — They understand the nuances of their team members' challenges
- **Career pathing** — They can pave the way for team members' longer-term career aspirations
- **Lean organization** — Enables a small team (30 people) to serve 50M users without layers of management

## Relationship to Generalist Hiring

The player-coach model complements [[concepts/generalist-vs-specialist|generalist hiring]] — both reject the specialist/manager dichotomy. Player-coaches are generalists who span management and execution.

## Limitations

Grant Lee notes they don't yet know how this model scales beyond their current size. ^[extracted] It may face challenges as organizations grow significantly larger or enter highly regulated domains requiring formal management structures.
