# Techno-Optimism

**Techno-optimism** is the philosophical position, articulated by [[entities/mark-andreessen|Mark Andreessen]] in his Techno-Optimist Manifesto, that technology is the only perpetual source of economic growth and that no material problem cannot be solved through more technology. ^[extracted]

Referenced at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2026 in the context of exponential technology growth and its relationship to AI development.

## The Core Thesis

The speaker at WF2026 referenced Andreessen's manifesto approvingly: ^[extracted]

> "The only perpetual source of growth for the entire economy... it will very very likely increase the economy massively and that will benefit a lot of us... the perpetual source of growth is technology."

The argument extends: there is no material problem — not psychological problems, but material ones — that cannot be solved with even more technology. ^[extracted]

### Examples

- **Starvation** → Green Revolution
- **Darkness** → Electric light
- **Cold** → Indoor heating, air conditioning

## Technology as Evolutionary Process

Techno-optimism frames technological progress as a continuation of evolution, now consciously directed by humans. The pace is so fast that within one lifetime, a major shift can occur: ^[extracted]

> "If you were born in 1900, then three years later the first human ever sustained powered flight... about 60-ish years later in 1969, humans flew all the way to the moon... within one lifetime humanity went from no one can fly to we all fly to the moon."

The parallel for AI: moving from AI being worse at everything humans do to possibly being better at any specific task — within a 30-60 year timeframe because everything moves faster now. ^[extracted]

## Relationship to S-Curves

Techno-optimism acknowledges that most exponentials eventually become S-curves. But the optimistic view is that humanity succeeds by layering new exponential technologies on top of previous ones — hunting, farming, science, the industrial revolution — each new S-curve beginning where the last flattened. ^[extracted]
