# AI Engineering Survey Methodology

The **2025 State of AI Engineering Survey** collected responses from 500 participants at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025, providing a snapshot of the AI engineering community's composition, practices, and challenges.

## Sample Characteristics

- **500 respondents** from AIEF2025 attendees and live stream viewers
- **Largest group**: self-identified engineers (software or AI), though few held the actual title "AI engineer"
- **Title confusion**: The survey highlighted that very few attendees at an "AI Engineering" conference actually have "AI engineer" as their job title
- **Experience paradox**: Among software engineers with 10+ years of experience, nearly half have been working with AI for three years or less

## Survey Design

- **Multi-select questions** used for monitoring/evaluation practices (respondents could select multiple methods)
- **Open-ended questions** for fine-tuning techniques (captured free-form responses on LoRA, DPO, SFT)
- **Self-reported usage** of models, modalities, and tools
- **Intent measurement** for future modality adoption plans

## Limitations

- **Conference bias**: Respondents are from a single conference, likely more engaged and technically advanced than the broader AI engineering population
- **Self-selection**: Participants who attended a free conference may differ from those who did not
- **Title ambiguity**: Self-reported titles may not reflect actual role responsibilities
