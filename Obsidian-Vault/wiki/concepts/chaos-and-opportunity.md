## Chaos and Opportunity

Chaos and Opportunity is the principle that in the AI era, chaos and opportunity are complementary — each creates the conditions for the other. As every layer of product building is being rewritten simultaneously, the discomfort of uncertainty is matched by the opportunity to build new products.

### The Chaos

Martin emphasizes that "all the time" there is chaos in product building right now:
- **Role uncertainty**: Will there still be product managers? Engineers? Designers?
- **Organizational disruption**: Teams are being reconfigured with AI participants
- **Personal discomfort**: Even asking "what do you do for a living" feels weird because the answer is no longer clear

This chaos lives "inside of us at each of these layers" — it is not just an external phenomenon but an internal one.

### The Opportunity

Complementary to chaos is always opportunity. The fact that everything is being rewritten means there is room to build things that didn't exist before. The "best products out there haven't been discovered yet."

### The Product Person's Job

In a world of chaos, the product person's job is to "find the nugget of opportunity out there and explode it like a popcorn kernel." This is the singular mission that remains constant even as everything else changes.

### The Organizational Imperative

Martin notes that this mission is most effective when "every person embraces this mission" within an organization. Chaos is manageable when the entire organization is aligned around finding and exploding opportunities.
