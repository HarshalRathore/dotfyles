# Human-Agent Co-Evolution

Human-agent co-evolution is [[entities/danielle-perszyk|Danielle Perszyk]]'s (Amazon AGI) model for how humans and AI agents will reciprocally level up each other's intelligence. Drawing on the historical pattern of [[concepts/techno-social-co-evolution|techno-social co-evolution]], she argues that agents will not just serve humans but will reshape human cognition — and humans, by using agents, will generate the data needed to improve them. ^[extracted]

## The Flywheel

Perszyk describes the cycle: ^[extracted]

> "Agents will need a model of our shared environment and interfaces that support intuitive interactions with us. These will enable humans and agents to reciprocally level up one another's intelligence. To advance the models, we will need human-agent interaction data. And to motivate people to use the agents in the first place, we'll need useful products. The more useful the products become, the smarter we will all become." ^[extracted]

This mirrors the historical pattern described in [[concepts/cognitive-technologies|cognitive technologies]]: each new tool evolves within communities and becomes more useful the more it is used.

## Three Agent Roles

Perszyk identifies three roles agents play in this co-evolution: ^[extracted]

1. **Do the repetitive stuff** — Freeing human attention for higher-order thinking (see [[concepts/automation-augmentation-agency|Automation → Augmentation → Agency]])
2. **Learn from us and redistribute skills across communities** — Amplifying expertise beyond its origin
3. **Teach us new things** — When agents discover new knowledge through their exploration of the digital environment

## Agents as Collective Subconscious

Perszyk's culminating vision: "Agents can become our collective subconscious." Like how the human brain moves practiced skills from conscious attention to automatic processing, agents could handle the cognitive load of digital interaction — not as independent intelligences but as integrated extensions of human cognition. ^[inferred]
