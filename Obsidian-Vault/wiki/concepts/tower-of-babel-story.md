# Tower of Babel Story

The Tower of Babel story is one of three framing metaphors used by Diego Rodriguez (CREA) at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 to illustrate challenges in AI communication and personalization.

## The Story

Diego told the Tower of Babel story: "We wanted to reach heaven, God was like, no, created a bunch of languages, and basically misunderstanding was like, nope, you're not going to go there" ^[extracted]. He connected this to stand-up meetings where people argue over tech stacks ("It should be React. It should be JavaScript") — comparing these to God winning over human attempts at unified communication ^[extracted].

## Purpose

The metaphor frames AI as a potential solution to the Tower of Babel problem: if humans can't agree on a common language, maybe AI can bridge the gap. "Now we have AI, so maybe, okay, if it wasn't with crypto, it was with AI" — people trying to convey ideas through AI ^[extracted].

## The Three Stories

Diego Rodriguez's talk opened with three stories:
1. **Cars vs. Traffic** — predictable components (horse, wheels, engine = car) vs. unpredictable emergent behavior (traffic) ^[extracted]
2. **Tower of Babel** — language barriers and misunderstanding ^[extracted]
3. **Korea Fox Ad** — speed of AI adoption (sign-up to broadcast in two days) ^[extracted]
