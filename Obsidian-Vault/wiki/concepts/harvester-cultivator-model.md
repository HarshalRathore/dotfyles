# Harvester/Cultivator Model

An organizational structure inspired by Palantir's model of deploying four software engineers across different market segments.^[extracted] Olive describes itself as "building the consumer version of Palantir" using this two-role model.^[extracted]

## Harvesters

Harvesters are product engineers who own and live/die by their products.^[extracted] They are the Palantir Delta operators — deployed software engineers who:

- Live in the metrics
- Work on A/B experiments
- Build features end-to-end
- Work with the marketing team
- Effectively own the entire product's existence^[extracted]

**Definition:** Harvesters are people who build products that people actually want and pay for.^[extracted]

## Cultivators

Cultivators are AI software engineers whose main goal is building the company's agentic operating system.^[extracted] They:

- Pioneer automation across different business units (marketing, design, product)
- Build infrastructure that affects all users everywhere
- Help the company win in every market^[extracted]

**Definition:** Cultivators are creating the foundation that lets the company ship and scale faster in any market.^[extracted]

## The Dynamic

Harvesters produce revenue-bearing products. Cultivators produce the infrastructure and automation that make harvesters more productive.^[extracted] The cultivator work compounds across all products, while harvester work is product-specific.^[inferred]

## Relationship to Palantir

Palantir successfully scaled across multiple market segments using this model.^[extracted] Olive adapts it for consumer software with AI-native cultivators rather than traditional software engineers.^[inferred]
