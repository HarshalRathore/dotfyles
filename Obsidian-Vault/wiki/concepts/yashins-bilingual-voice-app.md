# Yashin's Bilingual Voice App

Yashin is a non-technical mother of two bilingual children who built a working bilingual language education voice app without any prior coding experience.^[extracted] She presented her project at AIEF2025 as a builder spotlight, demonstrating the democratization of voice AI development.^[extracted]

## Motivation

Yashin raises her children bilingual to connect them with her cultural roots — a goal shared by many parents of bilingual children.^[extracted] She found that raising bilingual kids is expensive, time-consuming, and often feels like a chore for both parents and children.^[extracted] She believed modern technology could make language education feel more natural and fun.^[extracted]

## Achievement

Despite having no technical background, Yashin built a working prototype of her bilingual voice app with just a little guidance from a community member at AIEF2025.^[extracted] She assembled a group of eager testers — mostly parents of bilingual children — who are excited to try her application.^[extracted]

## Significance

Yashin's project exemplifies the vision articulated by Sean DuBois: voice AI tools should be accessible enough that people with innovative ideas can build voice applications regardless of technical background.^[extracted] Her success mirrors the early mobile era — just as anyone could build an iPhone app without deep systems knowledge, voice AI frameworks are lowering the barrier to creating voice experiences.^[extracted]
