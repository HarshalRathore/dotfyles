# MVP (Minimal Viable Product)

An **MVP (Minimal Viable Product)** is the simplest version of a product that delivers core value to users and generates meaningful market feedback. It strips away everything that isn't essential to the core value proposition.

## The Race Car Analogy

Eric Simons of Bolt.new describes the Bolt.new launch product using a race car metaphor: "it was like those race cars where they strip everything out — no back seat, no side seat, just metal." This is the essence of a well-executed MVP: remove every feature, every polish, every "nice-to-have" until only the core mechanism remains.

## Bolt.new's MVP Results

Despite being an MVP stripped to bare metal:
- Grew ARR from $0.7M to $20M in 60 days
- Attracted 30-40K active customers by month two
- Produced a "clean ramp" with no jagged edges
- The product was not a pivot — it used the same core technology StackBlitz had built for seven years

The MVP wasn't a compromise — it was the right product for the market at the right time. The seven years of underlying technology meant the MVP was still deeply capable, just not over-engineered for launch.

## MVP Principles

1. **Core value first.** Every feature must serve the core value proposition. If it doesn't, it's not MVP material.
2. **Same tech, different scope.** The underlying technology doesn't change — only the scope of what's shipped changes. StackBlitz had seven years of tech; Bolt.new was the right slice of it.
3. **MVP is not a bad product.** It's a focused product. The "minimal" refers to scope, not quality.
4. **MVP validates, doesn't prove.** It tells you if the core value proposition resonates. Everything else comes after.

## When MVPs Fail

MVPs fail when:
- The core value proposition itself is wrong (not the scope)
- The market timing is off
- The MVP is so stripped that it doesn't deliver any value

Bolt.new succeeded because the core value proposition was right — seven years of development had already validated the underlying technology. The MVP was simply the right packaging.
