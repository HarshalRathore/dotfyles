## The Co-Founder Standard

For the most part, the businesses that VCs back have co-founder relationships. ^[extracted] While solo founders are not impossible, they are unique cases rather than the norm. ^[extracted]

## Why Co-Founders Matter to VCs

Having a co-founder signals several things to investors:
- The founder can attract and retain talented people
- There is shared conviction and commitment
- The founding team has complementary skills
- There is built-in support and accountability

These signals are particularly important at pre-seed and seed stages where the product and market are still evolving. ^[inferred]

## Implications for Solo Founders

If you are a solo founder looking to raise VC:
- It is possible but harder — you will need to compensate for the lack of a co-founder with other signals of conviction
- Building a strong advisory board or early team can help
- Your track record and network become even more important

## The Team-Building Signal

The speakers note that at early stages, investors are backing "the team that you can bring around you" — which includes both co-founders and early hires. ^[extracted] Your ability to recruit is itself a signal of your founding quality. ^[inferred]
