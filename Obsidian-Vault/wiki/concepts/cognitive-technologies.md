# Cognitive Technologies

Cognitive technologies are a series of foundational innovations that have progressively upgraded human intelligence over millennia. As [[entities/danielle-perszyk|Danielle Perszyk]] (Amazon AGI) describes, "Each one is a foundation for the next and each one allows us to have increasingly abstract thoughts. They become useful by evolving within communities." ^[extracted]

## The Sequence

Perszyk traces the following sequence: ^[extracted]

1. **Language** — The original cognitive technology. Language emerged from the co-evolution of communication systems and models of mind, becoming what Perszyk calls "the first variable" — a placeholder concept that enabled representing any concept. This is the origin of generalization.
2. **Writing** — Externalized language, allowing knowledge to persist beyond a single mind or conversation.
3. **Mathematics** — Abstract symbolic reasoning about quantities and relationships.
4. **Computers and the Internet** — Engelbart's vision realized: machines that augment human computation and connect minds globally.
5. **AI Agents** — The emerging cognitive technology that Perszyk argues can become "our collective subconscious."

## How They Work

Cognitive technologies function by: ^[inferred]

- **Stabilizing our thinking** — Providing external anchors for concepts that would otherwise be ephemeral
- **Reorganizing our brains** — Changing neural pathways as we adapt to the new tool
- **Controlling our hallucinations** — Directing attention to shared signals in the environment, picking out relevant signal from noise
- **Co-creating shared world models** — Allowing groups to stabilize and share representations of reality

## Nova Act as Cognitive Technology Primitive

Perszyk frames [[entities/nova-act|Nova Act]] as providing "the primitives for a cognitive technology that aligns agents' and humans' representations." Like earlier cognitive technologies, its utility depends on evolving within diverse communities of use. ^[extracted]
