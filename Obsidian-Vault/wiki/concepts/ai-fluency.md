# AI Fluency

AI fluency is the practice of building AI understanding and practical capability across the entire organization — not limiting it to the engineering or AI team. Introduced by [[entities/sprout-social|Sprout Social]] at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 as part of their [[concepts/ai-first-transformation]] people dimension. ^[extracted]

## The Principle

The talk's core insight: "You can't go AI first if the rest of the company is still AI last." ^[extracted] In an AI-first company, everyone touches AI — from marketers and designers to product managers and customer care agents. Every team needs to feel empowered to understand AI and confident enough to build with it. ^[extracted]

## Approaches to Building Fluency

Sprout Social supports AI fluency through multiple channels: ^[extracted]

- AI newsletters to keep the organization informed of developments
- AI-focused podcasts for deeper dives
- Cross-functional AI show-and-tell sessions to make learning visible
- Empowering teams to use whatever AI tools help them work smarter

## Enabling Platform Approach

To scale fluency, Sprout Social aims to be "the sole service platform to enable product and engineering teams across the organization to prototype and ship AI-powered features without needing deep involvement from the AI team." ^[extracted]

The goal is "not to turn everyone into an AI expert — it is to create a company where AI thinking and exploration is the default, not the exception." ^[extracted]

## Relationship to T-Shaped Talent

AI fluency at the organizational level complements [[concepts/t-shaped-ai-talent|T-shaped AI talent]] at the individual level. T-shaped talent brings deep AI expertise plus wide versatility in a few key individuals; AI fluency ensures the broader organization can meet them halfway. ^[inferred]
