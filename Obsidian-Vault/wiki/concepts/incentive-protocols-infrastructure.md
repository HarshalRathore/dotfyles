# Incentive Protocols for Infrastructure

The application of token-based incentive mechanisms — inspired by Bitcoin's mining rewards — to coordinate and fund physical infrastructure construction, particularly solar panel deployment in developing markets.

## Core Mechanism

Glow Labs operationalizes this concept by:

1. Taking government subsidies for energy infrastructure
2. Converting them into token rewards (Glow tokens)
3. Using those rewards to incentivize solar panel construction
4. Achieving 10x the solar capacity that the same subsidy would produce through traditional procurement ^[extracted]

## Problem Context

In the developing world:

- Rising temperatures and growing populations strain electrical grids ^[extracted]
- People cannot run air conditioners during peak heat, causing heat stroke deaths ^[extracted]
- Traditional infrastructure investment is insufficient to meet demand ^[extracted]
- Government subsidies exist but are inefficiently deployed ^[extracted]

## Bitcoin Analogy

Bitcoin incentivized the construction of tens of millions of mining machines through token rewards. Glow asks: "Why not tens of millions of solar panels?" ^[extracted]

## Markets

Glow is building incentive projects in:

- India ^[extracted]
- Mexico ^[extracted]
- Lebanon ^[extracted]
- Across the developing world ^[extracted]
