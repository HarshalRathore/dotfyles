# AI Agent Disclosure Norms

The 2025 State of AI Engineering Survey asked respondents whether AI agents should be required to disclose when they're AI and not human. **Most AI engineers answered yes** — reflecting a strong community norm favoring transparency in AI-human interactions.

## Context

This finding sits at the intersection of AI ethics, user trust, and regulatory compliance. As agents become more capable and more prevalent in customer-facing applications, the question of disclosure becomes increasingly practical rather than theoretical.

## Related Considerations

- **Trust**: Disclosure may affect user trust in AI systems
- **Regulation**: Growing regulatory pressure for AI transparency (EU AI Act, etc.)
- **User experience**: Balancing transparency with seamless interaction
- **Agent capability**: As agents become more human-like, the disclosure question becomes harder
