# KPI Alignment

The principle that every person in a lean company owns a KPI, and all decisions must be validated against this metric.^[extracted] KPI alignment removes micromanagement because everyone is focused on moving their metric week over week.^[extracted]

## The Mechanism

Each team member is assigned a specific KPI that they own.^[extracted] Before making any decision, they validate it against their KPI: "Does this move my KPI?"^[extracted] This creates a self-aligning system where individual incentives are directly tied to company outcomes.^[extracted]

## Benefits

- **Eliminates micromanagement** — When everyone knows their metric and is focused on moving it, there is no need for top-down oversight.^[extracted]
- **Decision velocity** — Decisions can be made quickly by asking a single question: does this move the KPI?^[extracted]
- **Accountability** — Clear ownership of metrics prevents diffusion of responsibility.^[inferred]
