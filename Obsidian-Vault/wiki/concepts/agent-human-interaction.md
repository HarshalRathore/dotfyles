# Agent-Human Interaction

As agents move from pilot to production in enterprises, the primary challenge is shifting from technical deployment to human interaction: how people will work with, alongside, and through agents. ^[extracted]

## The Shift

With agent adoption jumping from 11% to 42% in production deployment (KPMG Q1-Q3 2025), organizations are confronting practical questions about the human side of agents:

- **Upskilling and enablement**: How to train employees to work effectively with agents
- **Resistance management**: Decreasing resistance as people engage directly with agents
- **Sandbox experiments**: Creating safe environments for employees to interact with and learn from agents
- **Workflow redesign**: Restructuring work processes around agent capabilities

## Key Insight

The speed at which agents moved through the pilot phase has been faster than anticipated, meaning organizations are dealing with the human implications of agent deployment before they had fully planned for them. This creates both challenges (reactive rather than proactive change management) and opportunities (early adopters gain a human-readiness advantage). ^[extracted]
