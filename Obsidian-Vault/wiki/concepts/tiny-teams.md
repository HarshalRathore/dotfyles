# Tiny Teams

The "tiny teams" thesis describes a fundamental shift in how successful companies are being built in the AI era: companies are getting smaller, funding rounds are getting delayed, and profitability is being attained earlier in a company's lifetime.^[extracted]

## The Phenomenon

Companies are generating millions of ARR with teams smaller than most startups' engineering departments.^[extracted] The age of bloated teams and endless hiring rounds is ending — replaced by an era of tiny teams augmented by AI tooling.^[extracted]

## Key Drivers

- **AI tooling** — The primary driver enabling tiny teams to achieve massive scale. AI allows small teams to produce output that previously required large organizations.^[extracted]
- **Profit-first focus** — Tiny teams are inherently profit-focused because every headcount directly impacts the bottom line.^[inferred]
- **Compounding blueprints** — Reusable playbooks and templates from prior products enable rapid launches of new products with minimal incremental effort.^[extracted]

## Case Study: Olive

Olive (formerly Aleve) exemplifies the tiny teams model: a team of four scaled a portfolio of consumer AI products to $6M ARR profitably, with over 500M social media views.^[extracted] Their third product launched in stealth reached 1,000+ users in just three weeks.^[extracted]
