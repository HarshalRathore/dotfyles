# AI App Viability Through Simplicity

The **viability through simplicity** principle holds that simpler AI applications can be built, iterated, and validated faster — increasing the probability of finding product-market fit.

## The Numbers Game

Building AI apps is partly a numbers game: you try many different ideas, and some succeed while others don't. Simplicity accelerates this process by reducing the time between idea and validated result. ^[extracted]

## The Iteration Advantage

Simple architectures (single API call, minimal infrastructure) enable:

- **Faster build cycles** — Less code to write, test, and deploy
- **Faster iteration** — Easier to pivot when feedback suggests a different direction
- **Faster validation** — Can determine if an idea has merit with minimal investment

## Simplicity as a Feature

Simplicity is not a limitation of early-stage AI products — it is a deliberate feature that enables the rapid experimentation needed to discover what works. The most successful AI apps (those reaching millions of users) often started with extremely simple architectures. ^[inferred]
