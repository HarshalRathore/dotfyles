**Annotation-augmented video understanding** is a technique where minimal annotations — either manual or generated by automated tools — are overlaid on video frames to dramatically improve the accuracy of video understanding models. ^[inferred]

## The Insight

**[[entities/randall-hunt|Randall Hunt]]** of **[[entities/caylent|Caylent]]** discovered that "a tiny bit of annotation can do wonders for the video understanding models." Specifically:

- For a sports video with static camera angles, annotating the three-pointer line on the court with a blue line
- Asking the model questions like "did the player cross the big blue line?"
- Results were "way better" than asking the same question about raw, unannotated video

The improvement was so dramatic that it took only seconds to add the annotations and immediately improved model accuracy. ^[extracted]

## Why Annotations Help

Video understanding models struggle with:

1. **Spatial reasoning** — Determining precise positions, boundaries, and relationships between objects in a frame
2. **Scale and perspective** — Understanding distances and sizes from visual cues alone is error-prone
3. **Consistency across frames** — Tracking objects across temporal sequences without explicit markers

Annotations provide explicit spatial references that the model can reason about, reducing the burden on the model to infer spatial relationships from pixels alone. ^[inferred]

## Automated Annotation with SAM2

**[[entities/meta|SAM2]]** (Segment Anything Model 2) from Meta can automate the annotation process:

- SAM2 can detect and segment objects in video frames
- It can generate bounding boxes, segmentation masks, and spatial annotations
- These annotations can then be overlaid on frames before passing them to video understanding models

This creates a pipeline where SAM2 does the annotation work, and the video understanding model does the reasoning — each doing what it's good at. ^[inferred]

## Practical Applications

- **Sports analytics** — Annotating court boundaries, player positions, ball trajectory
- **Security video** — Marking restricted zones, entry points, camera blind spots
- **Manufacturing** — Marking assembly line stations, quality check points
- **Retail** — Annotating shelf boundaries, product placement zones

## Trade-offs

- **Annotation effort** — Even "tiny" annotations require some effort; SAM2 reduces this but doesn't eliminate it
- **Camera stability** — The technique works best with static camera angles; moving cameras require per-frame annotation
- **Domain specificity** — Annotations are domain-specific (court lines work for basketball but not for general video)
