# Viral Growth Mechanics

The practice of using short-form social media (primarily TikTok) as the primary user acquisition channel for AI-powered consumer products, achieving massive user conversion from viral content without paid advertising.^[extracted]

## The Mechanism

Olive's growth playbook centers on creating man-on-the-street or product-demo videos that go viral on TikTok, converting views into app downloads at scale.^[extracted] Key evidence:

- QuizBuddy: one TikTok video generated 1 million views overnight, converting to 10,000 users in under 30 hours.^[extracted]
- Back-to-school campaign: man-on-the-street videos at prestigious US colleges drove the first $1M ARR.^[extracted]
- Unstuck AI: 250M+ social media views in a single month.^[extracted]
- Total across products: 500M+ social media views.^[extracted]

## Why It Enables Leanness

Viral growth mechanics eliminate the need for paid user acquisition, which is typically one of the largest expense lines for consumer apps.^[inferred] For a tiny team, this means:

- **Zero CAC** — User acquisition cost approaches zero when growth is organic/viral.
- **Speed** — A single video can generate more users in hours than traditional marketing channels achieve in months.^[inferred]
- **Product-market fit signal** — Virality itself is strong validation that the product resonates with its audience.^[inferred]

## Relationship to AI Products

AI-powered products have an inherent "wow factor" — the visual demonstration of an LLM solving a real problem is naturally shareable on short-form video platforms.^[inferred] This creates a compounding advantage: AI products are both easy to build (with AI tools) and easy to market (via viral demos).
