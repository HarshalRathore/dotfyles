# Junior-led vs Senior-led Consulting Models

Kevin Madura at AIEF2025 described two traditional models for professional services:

**Junior-led model:** Very senior individuals direct more junior staff who do the actual work. It involves "a lot of directing" — "okay, do this" — and throwing many people at a problem. The approach relies on leverage through headcount: "throw 50 people at a problem and they kind of figure it out and probably waste some time in doing so."

**Senior-led model (Alix Partners' model):** Senior professionals with 15-20 years of experience are deeply involved in day-to-day work. They "are actually doing the work, delivering the work." This model uses less leverage (fewer junior staff per senior) but delivers results "a lot faster and more impactfully because it's the senior-led folks."

Madura's prediction: "the future is probably somewhat of a hybrid" — but AI advances shift the balance toward the senior-led model's strengths. Because AI can replicate senior knowledge and judgment, the value proposition shifts from "senior expert directs many juniors" to "AI replicates senior expertise, humans execute."

This maps directly onto the [[concepts/ai-first-firm|AI-first firm]] concept: AI enables the senior-led model's quality (deep domain expertise in the work) at the junior-led model's scale (through AI-replicated knowledge).
