# Agent Adoption Surge

Agent adoption in enterprises has accelerated dramatically in 2025, far exceeding many expectations at the start of the year. ^[extracted]

## The Numbers

According to KPMG's quarterly pulse survey of enterprises with $1B+ revenue, the percentage of companies with actual production agents (not pilots or experiments) jumped from **11% in Q1 2025 to 42% in Q3 2025** — a nearly fourfold increase in just three quarters. ^[extracted]

This represents a meaningful shift in the pattern of agent adoption. While the year started with widespread expectations of mass automation across organizational functions, the reality has been more nuanced: not a wholesale shift toward full automation, but significant and accelerating agent deployment in production environments.

## What This Means

The speed at which agents moved through the pilot/experimental phase has been faster than anticipated. Organizations are now dealing with the practical implications of having agents in production:

- **Human-agent interaction** has become a primary concern
- **Upskilling and enablement** work is shifting focus
- **Resistance to agents** is decreasing as people engage with them directly
- **Sandbox experiments** are proliferating, allowing employees to interact with agents safely

## The Caveat

Despite the agent surge, many enterprises — if not most — remain stuck in pilot and experimental phases overall. The agent surge represents a specific, accelerating trend within the broader context of enterprise AI adoption, where only 7% of organizations claim to be fully at scale. ^[extracted]
