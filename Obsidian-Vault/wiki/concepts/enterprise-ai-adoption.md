# Enterprise AI Adoption

Enterprise AI adoption in 2025 is characterized by widespread but uneven implementation, with significant divergence between organizations that are scaling and those stuck in pilot phases. ^[extracted]

## Current State

As of 2025, virtually all enterprises are using AI at least to some degree, with adoption growing increasingly deep across organizations. The most dramatic surge has been in coding and software engineering, but other departments are now also learning to communicate with code and build things with it. ^[extracted]

### Key Metrics

- **Widespread baseline**: Nearly 100% of enterprises report some AI usage
- **Deep adoption growing**: Increasing proportions are using AI extensively, not just experimentally
- **Cross-departmental spread**: Moving beyond engineering into IT operations, business units, and other functions

## The Bifurcation Pattern

A significant and growing divide exists between AI leaders and laggers within the enterprise ecosystem. ^[extracted]

**Leaders** distinguish themselves by:
- Doing more AI initiatives simultaneously, not just spot experiments
- Thinking comprehensively and systematically about AI strategy
- Pursuing revenue growth, new capabilities, and new product lines — not just productivity gains
- Treating AI as a core business strategy rather than a technology experiment

**Laggers** tend to:
- Run isolated experiments without strategic coordination
- Focus only on first-tier time savings and productivity use cases
- Remain in pilot mode without scaling mechanisms

This pattern has persisted throughout the AI adoption trajectory: the biggest organizations often make the biggest efforts, contrary to the assumption that smaller, more nimble companies would adopt faster. ^[extracted]

## Department-Level Breakout

McKinsey's State of AI study shows jagged, uneven adoption patterns across departments. IT operations is jumping ahead of other functions, suggesting that infrastructure and operations work is proving to be a natural fit for AI implementation at scale. ^[extracted]
