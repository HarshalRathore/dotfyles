# Low Ego, High Trust

**Low ego, high trust** is a team culture where members prioritize collective success over individual recognition (low ego) and have deep mutual reliance in high-pressure situations (high trust). Eric Simons identifies this as one of the core values that made Bolt.new's extreme scaling possible.

## The Two Dimensions

**Low ego** means:
- No hierarchy-driven decision-making
- People check their ego and focus on what matters
- Credit goes to the team, not individuals
- Willingness to do any task, regardless of title (Simons and his chief of staff handled support tickets)

**High trust** means:
- Deep mutual reliance without micromanagement
- People can act autonomously knowing others have their back
- No need for formal approval chains
- The ability to function under chaos without breaking down

## Why It's Non-Negotiable for Small Teams

Simons explicitly states: "If you aren't in this sort of insane situation like we were, and you have folks that are already having trouble with the ups and downs of a startup, I will tell you, it would not have been possible to do what we did with folks that didn't have just incredible grit and ability to just check their ego."

Small teams under extreme pressure amplify every interpersonal friction point. Without low ego and high trust, the team fractures. With it, the team becomes a single instrument.

## Preconditions

This culture doesn't emerge from a mission statement. It requires:
- **Shared history** — StackBlitz's team had been working together for seven years
- **Shared struggle** — having been through hard times together (Simons' previous company: broke, living on couches)
- **Shared values** — not just aligned on goals but on how to work together
- **Time** — seven years of building trust cannot be rushed

## The User Obsession Connection

Low ego and high trust enable a third core value: being "obsessed with making the user successful." When the team isn't fighting internally or protecting egos, all energy flows outward toward the user.
