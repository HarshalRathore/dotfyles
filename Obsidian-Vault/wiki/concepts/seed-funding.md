## Seed-Stage Investing

Seed funds are one of the two primary early-stage VC categories (alongside pre-seed). They are well-versed in investing in companies that are pre-revenue and sometimes pre-product. ^[extracted]

## What Seed Investors Look For

At seed stage, investors are backing:
- The founder
- The team they can build around them
- Their vision for what the company can become

Rather than current product quality or revenue metrics. ^[extracted]

## Seed vs. Later Stages

Unlike later-stage funds that require ARR growth or ARR itself, seed funds are structured to evaluate founders on potential rather than demonstrated traction. ^[extracted]

## Strategic Advice

Focus on seed funds (and pre-seed funds) when you are pre-revenue. These are the funds that understand your stage and are structured to invest at it. ^[extracted]
