# DoorDash Analogy

The DoorDash analogy is a rhetorical device used by Eugene of Federalist AI at AIEF2025 to illustrate why 45% reliability is unacceptable for AI applications in production.

## The Analogy

Eugene asked: "Would you order and use an app that only succeeds 45% of the time? Would you order DoorDash that way? Of course not. If your order goes missing or you end up having 100 pizzas, you're going to start with customer support screaming down there. It's a frustrating experience. But that's what AI agents do. When they work, they're awesome. When they don't, we are stuck cleaning up the mess" ^[extracted].

## Purpose

The analogy makes the abstract concept of model reliability concrete and emotionally resonant. It frames the reliability problem not as a technical challenge but as a consumer experience problem — no human would accept a 45% success rate for a physical service delivery, yet the AI industry tolerates it for digital agents ^[inferred].
