# Company Type Spectrum

A framework for understanding how different types of companies should approach AI team building, based on their relationship to technology as a core value proposition. Articulated by [[entities/denys-linkov|Denys Linkov]] at AIEF2025. ^[extracted]

## The Three Categories

### 1. Technology Companies

Technology IS the core value proposition. Examples: big tech companies, AI startups.

**Characteristics:**
- Technology is the product, not just an enabler
- Challenge: lack of domain knowledge when launching products
- Common issue: business-technology misalignment
- Hiring approach: typically buy data or buy expertise (vendor label data, top AI researchers)

### 2. Verticalized Solutions / Services Companies

Domain-specific AI products where technology serves a specific industry. Examples: [[entities/wisedocs|WiseDocs]], [[entities/palantir|Palantir]].

**Characteristics:**
- Deep domain expertise combined with AI capability
- Challenge: "either everything goes right or everything goes awfully poorly" — binary outcomes
- Hiring approach: balance domain knowledge with AI capability
- The "middle" of the spectrum carries the most risk

### 3. Tech-Enabled Companies

Core product is not technology but benefits dramatically from good technology. Examples: banks, retailers, small and medium businesses.

**Characteristics:**
- Technology is an enabler, not the core product
- Challenge: tech is not the core value proposition, so tech decisions differ
- Hiring approach: typically buy technology through service providers or build-vs-buy solutions
- Less risk of domain-knowledge gaps because domain expertise is the core competency

## Strategic Implications

The company type determines fundamental decisions about:
- **Build vs. buy** — tech companies buy expertise; tech-enabled companies buy technology
- **Hiring strategy** — specialists vs. generalists vs. hybrid
- **Risk profile** — the middle category (verticalized) has the highest stakes
- **Technology adoption pace** — tech companies lead; tech-enabled companies follow
