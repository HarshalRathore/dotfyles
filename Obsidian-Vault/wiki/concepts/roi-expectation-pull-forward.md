# ROI Expectation Pull-Forward

Expectations for when AI investments will deliver ROI have shifted dramatically shorter over the course of 2025, representing a significant pull-forward in enterprise timelines. ^[extracted]

## KPMG CEO Survey Data

KPMG's annual CEO survey tracks how long CEOs expect it will take to realize ROI from AI investments:

| Expectation Window | 2024 | 2025 | Change |
|-------------------|------|------|--------|
| 6 months to 1 year | 0% | 19% | +19pp |
| 1 to 3 years | 20% | 67% | +47pp |
| 3 to 5 years | 63% | 12% | -51pp |
| More than 5 years | 16% | 2% (inferred) | -14pp |

The shift is dramatic: in 2024, 63% of CEOs expected 3-5 years for ROI. In 2025, only 12% do. Meanwhile, the 1-3 year expectation jumped from 20% to 67%, and a new 6-month-to-1-year category now captures 19% of CEOs. ^[extracted]

## Interpretation

This pull-forward reflects several converging trends:

- **Faster deployment**: AI tools are being adopted and showing results more quickly than previous enterprise technologies
- **Lower barriers**: Cloud AI, APIs, and no-code/low-code platforms reduce time-to-value
- **Learning curve**: Organizations are getting better at implementing AI, shortening the learning curve
- **Competitive pressure**: Fear of falling behind accelerates investment decisions and implementation timelines

## The Tension

Despite these shorter expectations, the measurement challenge remains: 78% of organizations say traditional metrics are falling flat. This creates a gap between how quickly CEOs expect ROI and how well they can actually measure it. ^[extracted]
