# AI Impact Categories

Superintelligent's market study categorizes AI use case impact into eight broad dimensions, providing a framework for understanding the full spectrum of value that AI creates in organizations. ^[extracted]

## The Eight Categories

1. **Time savings** — Reducing the time required for tasks and processes
2. **Increased output** — Enabling more work to be done in the same time
3. **Improvement in quality** — Better outcomes, fewer errors, higher standards
4. **New capabilities** — Abilities the organization did not previously have
5. **Improved decision making** — Better information, faster decisions, more confidence
6. **Cost savings** — Direct reduction in operational expenses
7. **Increased revenue** — New revenue streams, higher conversion, better pricing
8. **Risk reduction** — Lower exposure to operational, compliance, or strategic risks

## Purpose

These categories were designed as a broad, simple heuristic for dividing the complex impact of AI into manageable dimensions. They were used to classify the ~3,500 self-reported use cases collected from 1,000+ organizations in Superintelligent's market study. ^[extracted]

## Significance

The breadth of these categories reflects a key insight: AI impact is not monolithic. Organizations that achieve the best results tend to pursue AI across multiple impact categories simultaneously, not just the obvious ones (time savings and cost reduction). Leaders think about revenue growth, new capabilities, and risk reduction alongside productivity gains. ^[extracted]
