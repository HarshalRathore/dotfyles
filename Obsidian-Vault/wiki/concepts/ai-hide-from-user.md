## Hiding AI from Users

The principle that AI should be invisible to end users — it handles the initial draft and heavy lifting, but human curation is essential for the final product. The AI "disappears" into the background, and the user experiences a polished product without knowing or caring about the AI that created it.

### The Rationale

[[entities/lukasz-gandecki|Łukasz Gądecki]] argued at AIEF2025 that many current AI products fail because they make the AI the star: ChatGPT wrappers, image generators, and half-working voice assistants all put the AI front and center. The alternative approach:

1. **AI does the initial draft** — Scene analysis, music generation, graphics creation, character detection
2. **Humans do the curation** — Judging whether the music is good, whether graphics match the mood, whether avatars fit the character
3. **The user gets the result** — A seamless experience without any awareness of AI involvement

### Why Human Touch Matters

AI cannot judge aesthetic quality:
- It cannot tell if generated music is emotionally appropriate for a scene
- It cannot determine if graphics match the intended mood
- It cannot verify if a character avatar captures the "vibe" of the person being described

Multiple simple AI building blocks combined with human curation create a magical experience that neither AI alone nor humans alone could achieve.

### Design Implication

The best AI products are those where the user never thinks "this is AI" — they just experience something wonderful. This is the opposite of products designed to showcase AI capabilities.
