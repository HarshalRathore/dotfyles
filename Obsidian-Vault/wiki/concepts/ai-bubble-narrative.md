# AI Bubble Narrative

In 2025, a narrative of an AI bubble has gained traction in media and analysis, partly driven by studies characterized as dubious (such as the MIT report). This narrative contrasts sharply with actual enterprise behavior. ^[extracted]

## The Narrative vs Reality

The AI bubble narrative suggests that AI investment is unsustainable, driven by hype rather than value. However, actual enterprise data tells a different story:

- **Spending is increasing**: KPMG data shows intended AI spend growing from $88M to $130M (48% increase)
- **Adoption is deepening**: Nearly all enterprises use AI, with growing depth of usage
- **Agents are deploying**: 42% of large enterprises have production agents (up from 11%)
- **Investment intent is near-universal**: 90%+ of organizations plan to increase AI spend (Deloitte)

## Why the Narrative Persists

The bubble narrative persists despite contradictory evidence because:

- **Media incentives**: Skepticism generates more engagement than validation
- **Study quality concerns**: Some bubble studies (e.g., MIT report) are characterized as having questionable methodology
- **Pilot bottleneck**: The fact that 93% of organizations remain in pilot mode creates a narrative of "hype without results"
- **Measurement gap**: The inability to easily measure AI ROI feeds uncertainty and skepticism

## NLW's Position

NLW opened the AIEF2025 talk by explicitly acknowledging the bubble narrative but pivoting to ground-level data from actual enterprise use cases, suggesting that the reality of AI implementation is more nuanced and more positive than the bubble narrative suggests. ^[extracted]
