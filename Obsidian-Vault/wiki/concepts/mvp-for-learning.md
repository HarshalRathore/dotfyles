# MVP for Learning

MVP for learning is a product development mindset where minimum viable products are built primarily to validate direction rather than to deliver a shippable feature. Presented by [[entities/sprout-social|Sprout Social]] at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 as part of their [[concepts/ritualized-discovery]] practice. ^[extracted]

## Philosophy

Traditional MVPs are about speed to market — ship the smallest thing that delivers value and iterate. MVP for learning shifts the purpose from "get something out" to "learn something real." ^[inferred]

- **Not every bet will land** — some features won't work as expected, and that is treated as valuable signal, not failure ^[extracted]
- **Failure is a feature, not a bug** — it drives clarity through the ambiguity inherent in AI transformation ^[extracted]
- **Validating direction, not just launching quickly** — the goal is to determine whether the team is building the right thing, not just building it fast ^[extracted]

## Relationship to Ritualized Discovery

MVP for learning is a core practice within [[concepts/ritualized-discovery|ritualized discovery]]. Where ritualized discovery establishes the structural rhythm for experimentation (hackathons, planning cycles, learning forums), MVP for learning provides the tactical mindset for individual experiments. ^[inferred]

## Contrast with Traditional MVPs

| Aspect | Traditional MVP | MVP for Learning |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Primary goal | Ship quickly, get early users | Validate direction, test assumptions |
| Attitude toward failure | Failure to avoid | Signal to learn from |
| Time horizon | Feature delivery | Strategic clarity |
| Risk posture | Minimize risk of building wrong thing | Embrace risk of being wrong to learn faster |
