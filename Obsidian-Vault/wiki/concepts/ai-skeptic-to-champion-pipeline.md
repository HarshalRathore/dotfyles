# AI Skeptic-to-Champion Pipeline

Mo Bhasin described a pattern for AI adoption within organizations: converting skeptics into champions through surgical, high-accuracy deployments rather than broad, low-impact initiatives.

The key insight: accuracy gains from AI implementations "have not come cheaply." Even unsupervised learning approaches require "the right relationships with business partners" who work hand-in-hand with the AI team to validate results. The AI team doesn't become "snake oil salesmen pushing and peddling AI" — they become trusted partners who demonstrate measurable value.

The pipeline:
1. **Identify high-friction, high-volume tasks** — categorization, data processing, document review
2. **Deploy surgically with business partnership** — close collaboration with domain experts to validate accuracy
3. **Demonstrate measurable accuracy gains** — skeptics become champions through evidence, not persuasion
4. **Scale through internal platforms** — once validated, deploy the approach organization-wide

At Alix Partners, this approach produced "enormous wins in speed and accuracy" across 50 deployments serving hundreds of users. The organizational change mechanism — converting skeptics through demonstrated results — is as important as the technical implementation.

This pattern is a practical response to the [[concepts/enterprise-ai-productivity-paradox|enterprise AI productivity paradox]]: instead of broad AI initiatives that fail to scale (as BCG reports), targeted deployments with business-partner validation produce the enterprise productivity gains that actually move business metrics.
