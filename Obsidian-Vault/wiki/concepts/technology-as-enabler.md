# Technology as Enabler

The principle that technology enables organizational success but is not itself the limiting factor. How teams are built, what problems are understood, and how technology is used determine outcomes — not the technology itself. Articulated by [[entities/denys-linkov|Denys Linkov]] at AIEF2025. ^[extracted]

## The Core Principle

> "It's not about technology, it's how we use technology." ^[extracted]

This reframes the AI conversation from "what technology do we need?" to "how do we use the technology we have?" The team-building and problem-understanding decisions should reflect this understanding. ^[extracted]

## Implications for Team Building

If technology is an enabler rather than a limiter, then:

1. **Team structure matters more than tech stack** — The right team with good technology beats the wrong team with great technology
2. **Problem understanding is primary** — Understanding the actual problems before selecting technology
3. **Usage patterns determine value** — How technology is deployed and used determines its impact
4. **Platform investment > tool procurement** — Building platforms that raise the floor for everyone is more valuable than buying point tools

This directly informs the generalist hiring approach: invest in people and platforms, not just tools. ^[inferred]

## Relationship to Company Types

The role of technology as enabler plays out differently across company types:

- **Technology companies** — Technology IS the product, so the enabler/product distinction collapses
- **Verticalized companies** — Technology enables domain-specific solutions
- **Tech-enabled companies** — Technology is explicitly an enabler of the core product

Understanding where you sit on the spectrum determines how you invest in technology vs. domain capability. ^[inferred]
