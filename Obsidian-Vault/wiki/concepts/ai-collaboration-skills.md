# AI Collaboration Skills

AI collaboration skills are the set of abilities that determine how effectively an engineer works alongside AI tools — the skills that actually predict who will build great AI products, as opposed to who can solve contrived coding puzzles. ^[extracted]

## The Five Core Skills

Beth Glenfield identifies these as the skills that matter in the AI era:

1. **Collaboration with AI** — How well does the engineer delegate tasks to AI, manage AI output, and integrate AI-generated code into larger systems?
2. **Handling ambiguity** — Can the engineer operate effectively when requirements are unclear or incomplete? ^[extracted]
3. **Communication of technical decisions** — How well does the engineer document and explain decisions in pull requests, ticket comments, and code reviews? ^[extracted]
4. **Mentoring** — Can the engineer guide others (human or AI) through complex problems? ^[extracted]
5. **Adaptability** — How well does the engineer handle changing requirements mid-sprint? ^[extracted]

## Contrast with Traditional Signals

Traditional technical interviews measure:
- Algorithm memorization
- Ability to solve isolated coding puzzles under time pressure

AI collaboration skills measure:
- Real-world teamwork patterns
- Communication quality
- Adaptability to change
- Mentorship capability ^[extracted]

Glenfield argues that the candidates companies actually want — creative problem solvers, collaborative leaders, people who can work with AI — are not being optimized for lead code performance. Instead they're building AI tools, using AI libraries, contributing to open source, and understanding business impact. ^[extracted]
