# Harold Cohen - ARIAN

Harold Cohen was an artist and computer scientist who created ARIAN, a pioneering AI art system that used computers to create drawings on massive canvases in a manner similar to human artistic creation. Cohen's work represents one of the earliest attempts at AI-generated art, predating the current generative media wave by decades.^[extracted]

## Historical Context

Cohen's ARIAN system is cited as part of the long lineage of computer-generated art that predates the current generative media era. While generative media's current wave (post-2022) is distinguished by its capabilities and accessibility, Cohen's work demonstrates that the ambition to create art through computational systems has been a pursuit since computers existed.^[extracted]

## Significance

Cohen's work provides historical grounding for the claim that "generative media" is not a new concept but rather the latest iteration of a decades-long effort to use computers for artistic creation. The current wave differs in scale, quality, and accessibility, but the fundamental ambition — machines creating art — is the same.^[inferred]
