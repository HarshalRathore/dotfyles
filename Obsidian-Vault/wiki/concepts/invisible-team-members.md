## Invisible Team Members

Invisible Team Members describes the phenomenon where every team now includes AI systems as implicit participants in their work. Behind every document, slide, and artifact that is passed around or created, there is a ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.

### The Scale

Martin describes the current state as teams of five people with five AI assistants — a 1:1 ratio of human to AI participant. This is a new normal that teams are still figuring out how to use effectively.

### The Implications

This phenomenon is part of the second layer of Martin's Product Multi-Layer Cake (the Team layer). The implications are profound:
- **Every output is augmented**: Documents, slides, and artifacts are no longer purely human creations
- **The nature of teamwork is changing**: How do you coordinate with AI participants?
- **Superpowers without understanding**: Teams have "superpowers now" but don't fully understand what that means yet

### The Personal Example

Martin shares that even when she reads something with her "own raw eyeballs," she still gives it to ChatGPT and asks "what did I miss — here's my takeaways, what's yours." This illustrates how deeply AI augmentation has penetrated even individual work practices, not just team collaboration.

### The Rebuilding Challenge

Invisible Team Members is one of the layers being rewritten in the AI Product Rebuilding Wave. The question is not whether teams will include AI participants (they already do) but how to use these superpowers effectively.
