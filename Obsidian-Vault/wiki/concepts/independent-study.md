# Independent Study

Independent study is a self-directed learning approach where individuals control the pace, content, and depth of their education outside traditional classroom structures. ^[extracted]

## Greg Brockman's Experience

[[entities/greg-brockman|Greg Brockman]] has practiced independent study throughout his life, beginning in sixth grade when his father taught him algebra. ^[extracted] In seventh grade, he and his mother approached the high school teacher to skip pre-algebra and move directly to eighth-grade math. The teacher was skeptical, but after a month of Brockman consistently answering questions correctly, he was promoted. ^[extracted]

Brockman has continued this pattern through [[entities/recur-center|Recur Center]] programs and sabbaticals, crediting self-directed learning as "a key part of how I grew up." ^[extracted]

## Why It's Effective

The transcript raises the question of what makes independent study effective versus wasteful — a distinction Brockman has lived through repeatedly. ^[extracted] His approach appears to involve:

1. **Self-verification** — testing oneself against actual problems rather than just consuming content
2. **Skipping to the right level** — not being held back by curriculum that doesn't match one's actual ability
3. **Iterative proof** — demonstrating competence through correct answers rather than asking permission

The conversation cuts off before Brockman fully articulates his framework, but the pattern suggests that effective independent study requires honest self-assessment and the willingness to prove one's level through performance rather than request. ^[inferred]
