## Product Multi-Layer Cake

The Product Multi-Layer Cake is a framework articulated by Raiza Martin to describe the four layers that compose product building in the AI era. Each layer is being simultaneously rewritten, creating both disruption and opportunity.

### The Four Layers

**1. Self** — The innermost layer. Understanding your own values, what you bring to the table, and where you come from. Martin notes that it used to be considered "technical" to be a PM who could write their own SQL queries, but now ChatGPT handles that — the question shifts from "can you do the task" to "what question are you asking." This layer is about self-awareness in a world where technical barriers to execution are collapsing.

**2. Team** — Teams are being drastically reconfigured. Every team now has "invisible participants": behind every document, slide, and artifact there is a ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini. Martin describes teams of five people with five AI assistants as a new normal we don't yet fully understand. The question is how to use these superpowers effectively.

**3. Product** — The layer where ideas are pulled from deep within the self and translated into technology. This is the traditional definition of product, but in the AI era, the translation process is fundamentally different because the technology layer has changed.

**4. User** — The outermost layer, where consumer unrest emerges. Users who experience AI-native products (ChatGPT, Cursor, Claude) develop expectations that legacy products cannot meet, creating a growing chasm between AI-native and traditional software.

### Implications

The framework implies that product building is no longer a single discipline but a multi-layered challenge where each layer requires different skills and awareness. The fact that all four layers are being rewritten simultaneously is what makes the current moment both "pretty fucking hard" and full of opportunity.
