# AI Sideshow Pattern

The **AI sideshow pattern** describes the phenomenon where AI features are added to a product because they're technically impressive, but they don't serve the core product experience — creating an "AI sideshow" that degrades the overall experience compared to not having AI at all.

## The Excalidraw Example

[[Christopher Chedeau|christopher-chedeau]] described this pattern at AIEF2025 through his team's AI integration experiments. The File AI model that transformed Excalidraw diagrams into photorealistic beach scenes was "mind-blowing" from a technical standpoint — but people don't draw realistic images on Excalidraw. The feature was impressive but irrelevant to how the product was actually used.

## The Degradation Principle

Chedeau's key warning: if you add AI that doesn't fit the product, "the whole product experience is going to be way worse than if you didn't have any AI at all." This is because:

1. AI features add complexity to the UI/UX
2. They create user expectations that may not be met
3. They distract from the core workflow
4. They signal "AI first" over "user first"

## The Test

The practical test for whether an AI feature is a sideshow or a core feature: does it serve what users actually do, or what they might do in a demo? If the latter, it's a sideshow.^{[inferred]}
