OMB Memorandum M-25-21 is an April 2025 directive from the Office of Management and Budget that codifies AI governance requirements for federal agencies, forming a pillar of [[concepts/ai-governance|U.S. AI governance]]. It replaced or superseded executive orders from the previous administration.

Key provisions:

- **Mandated strategy development:** Federal agencies have 180 days to develop AI implementation strategies, including plans for governing pilots, classifying use cases by risk level (high risk vs. low risk in their specific context), and establishing governance frameworks.
- **Go faster directive:** The memorandum explicitly directs agencies to accelerate AI adoption across government operations.
- **Mission depth requirement:** AI must go beyond productivity tools (PowerPoint summarization, email drafting) and integrate into mission-critical workflows. The memorandum recognizes that government AI workloads have real-world impacts with kinetic consequences.
- **Trust requirements:** Acknowledges that as agencies trust AI providers with increasingly sensitive data, the providers' responsibility and security posture requirements increase correspondingly.

M-25-21 was issued alongside [[concepts/omb-memorandum-m25-22|M-25-22]], and together they form the current foundation for [[concepts/ai-governance|U.S. AI governance]] policy. As of the talk date (April 2025), AI-specific governance frameworks are still being developed — agencies are in the rule-making period.
