# Paid Trial Hire

**Paid Trial Hire** is a hiring methodology where candidates complete a paid project as part of the interview process. [[entities/vik-paruchuri|Vik Paruchuri]] ([[entities/datalab|Datalab])) uses this to screen for actual shipping ability rather than talking about shipping. ^[extracted]

## Datalab's Three-Step Process

1. **Peer chat** — A short conversation where you talk through a challenge together, like talking to a peer. This tests problem-solving and communication. ^[extracted]

2. **Paid project** — A ~10-hour project for $1,000. Vik notes this "sounds like a lot but is actually a tiny amount of money to figure out if someone's a fit." The project tests actual building ability. ^[extracted]

3. **Culture fit review** — If the project is good, the team interacts as humans to assess cultural fit. Does it feel like a good fit? ^[extracted]

## Selection Rate

About 40% of interviewed candidates are hired. Vik notes that once they get someone to the beginning of the process, they have high confidence they'll be good — they don't want to waste anyone's time. ^[extracted]

## Why It Works

- **Tests actual ability** — Not theoretical answers but real work
- **Low cost relative to value** — $1,000 for 10 hours of actual work and hiring signal
- **Mutual fit assessment** — Candidate also evaluates the company
- **Filters for GSD** — Distinguishes people who ship from people who talk about shipping
