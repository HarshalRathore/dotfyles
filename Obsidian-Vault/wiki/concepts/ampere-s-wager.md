# Ampere's Wager

A thought experiment articulated by [[entities/denys-linkov|Denys Linkov]] at AIEF2025 that challenges the assumption that hiring more AI research talent is the optimal path for most organizations. Named as a successor to Pascal's Wager, with a nod to André-Marie Ampère (pioneer of electromagnetism, connecting to the "graphics card architectures" reference). ^[extracted]

## The Wager

**Would you trade your entire team — a team with domain knowledge, domain expertise, and experience working in the area — for five AI researchers from top labs?**

The wager asks you to consider:
- Your current team's domain knowledge and institutional understanding
- Five AI researchers from top labs (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google DeepMind)
- Maybe throw in some cash and first-round draft picks for those researchers too

The implicit answer Linkov pushes toward is: **no, you should not make this trade.** ^[extracted]

## Why Domain Knowledge Beats Pure Research Talent

The wager highlights several advantages of a domain-knowledgeable team over pure AI researchers:

1. **Domain understanding** — Your team knows the problems, the customers, the constraints
2. **Integration capability** — Your team can integrate AI into existing products (brownfield, not greenfield)
3. **Business alignment** — Your team understands ROI, go-to-market, and customer adoption
4. **Organizational context** — Your team knows how the organization actually works

Five AI researchers from top labs might build better models, but they likely lack the domain knowledge to apply those models effectively to your specific problems. ^[inferred]

## When the Wager Flips

The wager only favors hiring top AI researchers when:
- You are a model provider (OpenAI, Anthropic, Google) — the best tech IS the product
- You have hit a scale where the marginal improvement from better models is worth the cost
- The domain itself IS AI research

For most companies, the domain-knowledgeable generalist team delivers more value. ^[inferred]
