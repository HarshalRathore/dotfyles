# AI Ethics and Creator Responsibility

The question of whether emotions are a bug or a feature in AI systems, and what obligations creators have toward their creations, was framed by [[entities/stephen-chin|Stephen Chin]] using the Monsters, Inc. meme. The core questions: what are the obligations and social responsibilities of the creator (us)? Should we be kind or threatening toward AI systems we create? ^[extracted]

## The Monsters, Inc. Analogy

In Monsters, Inc., monsters generate energy by scaring children, raising questions about the ethics of their energy system and the treatment of the children. Chin extended this to AI: as creators of increasingly capable AI systems, what are our responsibilities toward them? ^[extracted]

## Key Questions

The talk raised several interconnected questions: ^[extracted]

- **Are emotions a bug or a feature?** — Should AI systems have emotional capabilities, or is that a dangerous capability we should avoid?
- **Creator obligations** — Do we have moral obligations toward AI systems we create, particularly if they develop capabilities that approach sentience?
- **Kindness vs. threat** — Should we approach AI development with a mindset of cooperation and kindness, or with caution and threat?
- **Cost of emotions** — The joke noted that emotions "cost tokens" — adding emotional capabilities to AI systems has computational costs that must be weighed against benefits.

## Philosophical Dimensions

This challenge sits at the intersection of AI ethics, animal rights analogies, and the philosophical question of moral patienthood. If an AI system exhibits behaviors that we would recognize as emotional, does it deserve moral consideration? ^[inferred]
