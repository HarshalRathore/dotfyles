# First-Principles Org Design

**First-principles org design** is the practice of applying first-principles thinking to team building and organizational structure — the same rigor that successful founders apply to product design. ^[extracted] It starts by questioning fundamental assumptions about how teams should be organized, rather than inheriting traditional patterns.

## The Traditional Assumption

The default assumption in startup scaling is the cascading hierarchy:

1. Bring on a VP
2. VP hires directors
3. Directors hire direct reports
4. This cascades across every function

This pattern can transform a small team into a large organization overnight during "blitzscaling" phases. ^[extracted]

## The First-Principles Question

Grant Lee ([[entities/grant-lee|Grant Lee]], [[entities/gamma|Gamma]]) asks: what if we applied the same first-principles thinking to team building that we apply to product building? ^[extracted] The answer in the AI era: companies can achieve massive scale with dramatically fewer people because AI changes the economics of individual output.

## Core Principles

1. **Generalists over specialists** — People who span multiple disciplines create more value per head
2. **Player-coach leaders** — Managers who also do hands-on work enable real-time adaptation
3. **Output-based scaling** — Grow relative to output and user count, not linearly with headcount

## Why Now?

The AI era fundamentally changes the assumptions underlying traditional org design:

- AI tools augment individual output dramatically
- Fast-moving technology requires leaders close to the work
- Traditional management layers become coordination bottlenecks
- One person can now do what previously required a team
