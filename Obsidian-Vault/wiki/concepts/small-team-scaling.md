## Small Team Scaling

Small team scaling is the strategy of growing a startup with fewer than 10 people while maintaining high velocity and product quality. The core premise, articulated by [[entities/max-brodeur-urbas|Max Brodeur-Urbas]] of [[entities/gumloop|Gumloop]], is that small teams move faster because they avoid the coordination overhead that slows down larger organizations.

### Key Principles

**Exceptional-only hiring.** Every person on a small team must be absolutely exceptional. The bar is so high that hiring decisions should feel like a "no-brainer." If you aren't extremely excited about a candidate, don't hire them. This means hundreds of interviews and work trials to find the right people, but it prevents the velocity drag of underperforming team members.

**Coordination avoidance.** In larger teams, multiple people on a project must sync and agree on terms before starting work, which leads to slowness everywhere. Small teams skip this — each person can move independently.

**Investor friction.** Small teams often confuse investors who expect rapid headcount growth after funding rounds. The trade-off is accepting investor confusion in exchange for maintaining exceptional individual contributors who outperform larger teams.

### Evidence

Max and his co-founder observed that working at Amazon and Microsoft was slower due to team size and coordination requirements. They intentionally chose to scale under 10 people, raised money to hire the most exceptional individuals, and proved that small AI-native teams could compete with larger organizations.

Gumloop raised a Series A as a team of two and grew to nine people while building a product used by large enterprises.

### Gamma: Scaling Beyond 10 People

Grant Lee ([[entities/grant-lee|Grant Lee]], [[entities/gamma|Gamma]]) extends the small-team thesis beyond the 10-person ceiling. Gamma serves **50 million users** on a team of only **30 people** — roughly one-tenth the size a similar company would have needed a few years ago. ^[extracted]

Gamma achieves this scale through three complementary strategies:

1. **Generalist hiring** — Cross-discipline team members who span design, coding, and research
2. **Player-coach leadership** — Every core leader both manages and does hands-on work
3. **Content-first product** — AI-powered tooling that dramatically reduces the effort per user served

This represents a different regime of small-team scaling: not just under 10 people, but proving that 30 people can serve tens of millions of users when augmented by AI tooling and lean organizational design. ^[inferred]
