# Generalist Engineer

A **generalist engineer** is someone who can work across the full stack and understand all aspects of a company. They are the core building block of [[concepts/small-ai-teams|small AI teams]]. ^[extracted]

## Definition

Jeremy Howard ([[entities/answer-ai|Answer AI]]) and [[entities/vik-paruchuri|Vik Paruchuri]] ([[entities/datalab|Datalab]]) advocate hiring fewer than 15 generalists as the foundation of small AI companies. ^[extracted]

A generalist engineer:

- Works across the full stack (frontend, backend, data, ML, ops)
- Talks to customers
- Understands all aspects of the company
- Can flex to solve key issues wherever they arise

## Seniority = Maturity, Not Experience

Vik Paruchuri redefines "senior" not as years of experience but as **maturity**: ^[extracted]

> The ability to look at a problem, figure out how to solve it, do what it takes, and iterate with the customer to solve it.

This means:

- Self-direction — no need for management
- Customer empathy — understanding what users actually need
- End-to-end ownership — from discovery to deployment
- Maturity in collaboration — low ego, high trust

## The Three Core Roles at Datalab

Datalab operates with three overlapping roles: ^[extracted]

1. **Research Engineer** — Research, model training, architecture
2. **Full-Stack Engineer** — Building product, APIs, infrastructure
3. **Go-to-Market** — Sales, marketing, support collapsed into a generalist role

Everybody talks to customers. Everybody builds product in some way. The roles overlap significantly.

## Screening for Generalists

Datalab's interview process screens for: ^[extracted]

- **Low ego** — People who care about the work, teammates, and customers, not politics or personal advancement
- **GSD (Get Shit Done)** — People who ship, not talk about shipping
- **Patience** — Best hires come from taking time to find the right person, not filling roles quickly
- **Maturity** — Can work without being managed

The paid trial project (~10 hours, $1,000) is the primary screening mechanism for actual shipping ability. ^[extracted]

## Sourcing Generalists

Vik's experience: open-source work and Twitter are great ways to attract generalist candidates. Doing good work in public attracts people who care about the mission and want to build in the same way. ^[extracted]
