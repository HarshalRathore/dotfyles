# Employee vs Enterprise Productivity

Mo Bhasin at AIEF2025 drew a critical distinction between two types of AI-driven productivity, arguing this explains the enterprise AI productivity paradox:

**Employee productivity** is individual-level improvement — making a single worker faster, more accurate, or more capable through AI tooling. This is what most AI implementations measure and optimize for.

**Enterprise productivity** is system-level improvement — transforming end-to-end workflows, data coverage, and organizational output. This is what actually moves business metrics like earnings and recorded hours.

The gap between the two explains the NBER/BCG/S&P data: companies investing in AI see employee productivity gains (faster document review, better individual analysis) but don't see enterprise productivity gains (no earnings impact, no recorded hours change) because the underlying workflow architecture hasn't changed.

The Alix Partners example illustrates enterprise productivity: by enabling 100% data coverage instead of 20% sampling, AI doesn't just make individual analysts faster — it transforms the quality and scope of the entire engagement. The output is "that much better" because it's based on complete data rather than prioritized sampling.^[inferred]
