# AI Engineering Title Confusion

The 2025 State of AI Engineering Survey revealed a striking observation: **very few attendees at an AI Engineering conference actually hold the title "AI engineer."** This reflects widespread confusion about job titles in a field that is evolving faster than HR departments can define it.

## Key Findings

- When Barr Yaron asked attendees to raise their hands if their title is "AI engineer," the response was "extremely sparse"
- The largest survey group self-identified as engineers (software or AI), but titles varied widely
- The conference community is described as "broad, technical, and growing"
- The "AI engineer" label is expected to "gain even more ground" as the field matures

## Historical Context

- Google Trends shows "AI engineering" as a search term **barely registered before late 2022**
- The term spiked after ChatGPT launched (November 2022)
- The term is still relatively new — explaining the title confusion

## Experience Paradox

Among software engineers with 10+ years of experience:
- **Nearly half** have been working with AI for three years or less
- **One in ten** started working with AI just in the past year
- The most seasoned software developers are often AI newcomers

## Implications

- Organizations need to define what "AI engineer" actually means in practice
- Hiring and role definitions are lagging behind actual work being done
- The title "AI engineer" may become more common as the field formalizes
- Cross-disciplinary teams (experienced software + new AI skills) are the norm, not the exception
