# Voice Marketplace

A voice marketplace is a platform where voice actors and creators can license their voices for use in AI-generated speech, turning voice actors from competitors replaced by AI into providers amplified by AI. ^[extracted] Cartesia operates one of the first prominent voice marketplaces, explicitly positioning it as a tool to amplify voice actors rather than replace them. ^[extracted]

## Philosophy: Amplification Over Replacement

Arjun Desai (Cartesia) explicitly rejected the narrative that AI will replace voice actors. ^[extracted] Instead, Cartesia's stated goal is to give voice actors a platform where "their essence, their personality can actually be exuded and licensed by other people that want to use the platform." ^[extracted] This reframes voice actors as brand assets rather than labor to be automated away. ^[extracted]

## How It Works

Voice actors onboard to the platform, providing voice samples that are used to create voice models. ^[extracted] These models can then be licensed by enterprises and developers for use in voice AI applications — customer support agents, narration, gaming, and other use cases. ^[extracted] Voice actors are compensated through licensing arrangements rather than per-session payment. ^[extracted]

## Use Cases

The voice marketplace serves multiple use cases:

- **Narration** — a significant portion of marketplace use cases are narration-focused
- **Customer support** — enterprise voice agents with branded personalities
- **Gaming** — dynamic NPC dialogue with specific voice personalities
- **Healthcare** — patient-facing voice interfaces with empathetic tone

## Implications for the Voice Industry

The voice marketplace model creates a new economic relationship between voice actors and AI:

1. **Voice actors become voice providers** — their voice becomes a licensable asset
2. **Reach amplification** — a single voice actor can be licensed across many applications simultaneously
3. **Creative control** — voice actors retain agency over how their voice is used through licensing terms
4. **New revenue stream** — licensing income supplements or replaces traditional voice acting work
