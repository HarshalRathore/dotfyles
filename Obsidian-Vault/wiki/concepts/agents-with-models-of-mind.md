# Agents with Models of Mind

[[entities/danielle-perszyk|Danielle Perszyk]] (Amazon AGI) argues that agents will eventually need **models of human minds** — the ability to infer and reason about human goals, beliefs, and intentions — to achieve true reliability and generality. ^[extracted]

## From Computer Use to Mind Reading

Perszyk presents a progression: ^[inferred]

1. **Current agents** — LLM wrappers that use tools and execute instructions but lack a shared environment to ground interactions.
2. **Computer-use agents (Nova Act)** — Agents grounded in a shared visual (browser) environment, capable of interacting with pixels and UIs like humans. This grounds their perception but not their understanding of goals.
3. **Agents with models of mind** — The next step: agents that can infer what a human wants, not just what they literally say.

> "Reliability isn't just about clicking in the same place every time. It's about understanding the larger goal." ^[extracted]

## The Evolutionary Precedent

Perszyk connects this to the evolution of human intelligence. The key adaptation that made human intelligence general and flexible was language, which co-evolved with **models of mind** — the ability to infer the contents of other minds. Language "both a cause and an effect of modeling our minds." ^[extracted]

The breakthrough moment: "our models of mind became the original placeholder concept, the first variable for being able to represent any concept. That right there is generalization." ^[extracted]

## Preconditions for Emergence

Perszyk argues we cannot directly build agents with models of mind — we must "set the preconditions for them to emerge": ^[extracted]

1. **A model of a shared environment** — Grounded, visual, interactive (the browser)
2. **Interfaces that support intuitive interactions** — Natural communication channels
3. **Human-agent interaction data** — The raw material for learning models of mind
4. **Useful products** — That motivate people to use agents, generating the data
