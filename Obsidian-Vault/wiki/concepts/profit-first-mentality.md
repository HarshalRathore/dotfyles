# Profit-First Mentality

The operating principle that profit is power and profit is focus — serving as the primary mechanism for decision-making and the company's North Star.^[extracted]

## The Principle

Profit-first mentality means being relentless about prioritizing profits.^[extracted] Every decision is evaluated against its impact on profitability. This replaces vague mission statements or growth-at-all-costs mentalities with a concrete, measurable signal.^[extracted]

## Why It Enables Leanness

- **Natural headcount discipline** — In a profit-first company, every hire must justify itself through direct contribution to revenue or cost reduction. This prevents the bloat that plagues VC-funded startups.^[inferred]
- **Clear decision signal** — Profit provides a binary signal for whether a decision is right, replacing subjective debates about "strategic value."^[inferred]
- **Earlier profitability** — Olive achieved profitability within nine months of operations, directly attributable to the profit-first approach.^[extracted]

## Relationship to KPI Alignment

Profit-first mentality works in tandem with KPI alignment: everyone owns a KPI, and those KPIs are ultimately aligned to the profit North Star.^[extracted] Together, these principles remove the need for micromanagement.^[extracted]
