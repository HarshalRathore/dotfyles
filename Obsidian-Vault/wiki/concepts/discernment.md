# Discernment

**Discernment** is the rarest and highest form of agency. It is the ability to decide whether a problem is worth investing in at all — and having the judgment to walk away when it isn't. ^[extracted]

At the AI Engineer World's Fair 2026, discernment was positioned at the top of the **agency ladder**, above flag, execute, diagnose, propose, recommend, and resolve. ^[extracted]

## Core Insight

When agents make more paths possible, agency is not about chasing every single path. It is about deciding **which paths deserve your ownership and attention**. ^[extracted]

Discernment is meta-agency: the agency to choose what agency to apply. It is the judgment call that determines whether effort is worth the return.

## Why It's Rare

Discernment requires:

1. **Context** — understanding the full landscape of the problem space
2. **Experience** — recognizing patterns of what has and hasn't worked
3. **Honesty** — being willing to admit a problem isn't worth solving
4. **Courage** — walking away from something that looks promising but isn't

## Operating Model

In a human-AI collaborative system: ^[inferred]

- **Agents** handle investigation, implementation, testing, and reporting (the inner loop)
- **Humans** handle discernment and authority (the outer loop)

This creates a clean separation: agents execute at scale; humans discern what matters.
