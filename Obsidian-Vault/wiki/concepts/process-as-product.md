# Process as Product

Process as product is the practice of treating internal organizational processes as products that must earn their existence by producing measurable outcomes. Introduced by [[entities/sprout-social|Sprout Social]] at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 as part of their [[concepts/ai-first-transformation]] ways of working. ^[extracted]

## The Problem with Legacy Processes

Many organizational processes were designed for "predictable, linear roadmaps with deterministic risks" — a world where teams generally knew what they were building months in advance. ^[extracted] In an AI-first world where timelines shift and capabilities evolve monthly, these processes don't hold up. ^[extracted]

However, the talk cautions against the opposite extreme: introducing too many new processes all at once backfires, creating overhead and drag instead of helping. ^[extracted]

## The Evaluation Framework

Sprout Social evaluates every process against three outcome-based questions: ^[extracted]

1. **Does it create clarity of direction?** — Does the process help teams know what to build and why?
2. **Does it unblock teams?** — Does the process remove friction or does it create more?
3. **Does it help make better decisions faster?** — Does the process accelerate or slow decision-making?

If the answer to any of these is no, the process must be rethought, retooled, or cut entirely. ^[extracted]

## Process Is Not a Dirty Word

The talk emphasizes that "process isn't a dirty word — when it's purposeful, it gives teams the clarity and flow and becomes a process that accelerates." ^[extracted] The goal is not to eliminate process but to ensure every existing process earns its place in the workflow.
