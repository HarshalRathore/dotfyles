# Ritualized Discovery

Ritualized discovery is the practice of transforming AI experimentation from ad-hoc, reactive innovation into a systematic, repeatable process baked into a company's planning rhythm. Introduced by [[entities/sprout-social|Sprout Social]] at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 as part of their [[concepts/ai-first-transformation]] framework. ^[extracted]

## The Shift

Historically, innovation in SaaS companies happened reactively: someone had an idea, teams tested it in isolation, maybe ran a spike, and moved on. This approach was ad-hoc and rarely became a sustained strategic driver. ^[extracted]

In an AI-first world, where the landscape shifts monthly and the stakes are high, this fragmented approach doesn't hold up. Ritualized discovery means: ^[extracted]

- Building dedicated time into planning cycles for experimentation ^[extracted]
- Running hackathons and learning forums in visible, actionable formats ^[extracted]
- Making discovery outcomes visible and connected to company strategy ^[extracted]

## MVPs for Learning

A key part of ritualized discovery is adopting [[concepts/mvp-for-learning|MVPs for learning]] — shipping not just to launch quickly but to validate direction. The mindset assumes not every bet will land; failure becomes a feature, not a bug — it's what drives clarity through ambiguity. ^[extracted]

## How It Differs from Traditional Innovation

| Aspect | Ad-Hoc Discovery | Ritualized Discovery |
|--------|-----------------|---------------------|
| Cadence | Reactive, event-driven | Scheduled, repeatable |
| Scope | Isolated spikes | Strategic alignment |
| Learning | Siloed | Visible, actionable |
| Failure handling | Discouraged, hidden | Expected, treated as signal |
