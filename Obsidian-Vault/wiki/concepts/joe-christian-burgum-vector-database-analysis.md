# Joe Christian Burgum: Rise and Fall of Vector Databases

Joe Christian Burgum's analysis describes the trajectory of the vector database infrastructure category: a gold rush following ChatGPT's launch, followed by industry recognition that vector search alone is insufficient for sophisticated retrieval.

## The Narrative

1. **Gold Rush** — ChatGPT's launch created massive demand for vector databases as the primary retrieval mechanism for RAG systems
2. **Reality Check** — The industry is starting to recognize that vector search alone is insufficient for enterprise-scale retrieval
3. **Multi-Strategy Future** — Sophisticated retrieval requires multiple strategies beyond simple vector similarity ^[extracted]

## Significance

Sam Julien notes this analysis is "music to our ears at Writer" because they had been advocating for graph-based RAG for years before the industry caught up. The market trajectory validates the customer-first approach: solve the real problem (accurate retrieval) rather than betting on a single technology. ^[extracted]
