# Ship First Product

**Ship first product** is the principle of getting the first working version of a product to market as quickly as possible, even if it's stripped down to bare essentials. The core insight: a shipped MVP generates real market feedback that no amount of planning can replicate.

## The Bolt.new Launch

Bolt.new launched as an MVP that Eric Simons describes as a race car: "just metal, no back seat, no side seat." It had no extras, no polish, no back-seat features — just the core mechanism. Despite this minimal state:
- Grew ARR from $0.7M to $20M in 60 days
- Attracted 30-40K active customers by month two
- Produced a "clean ramp" of growth

The product was not a pivot — it used the same core technology StackBlitz had been developing for seven years. The "minimal" referred to launch scope, not underlying capability.

## Why Shipping First Matters

1. **Real feedback beats hypothetical planning.** Users interacting with a shipped product provide data that no amount of internal discussion can generate.
2. **Revenue validates faster than hypotheses.** $20M ARR in 60 days is a market signal no survey could match.
3. **Momentum compounds.** A shipped product creates momentum — users, revenue, team morale — that fuels further iteration.
4. **Speed beats perfection.** The race car doesn't need back seats to win the race.

## The Seven-Year Context

StackBlitz spent seven years developing the core technology before shipping Bolt.new. This is not contradictory to "ship first" — the seven years were about building the engine, not about delaying the launch. The "ship first" principle applies to the *launch decision*, not the *development timeline*.
