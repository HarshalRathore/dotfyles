# Pilot-to-Scale Bottleneck

Despite enthusiastic experimentation, the vast majority of enterprises remain stuck in pilot and experimental phases of AI adoption, unable to move to full-scale production deployment. ^[extracted]

## The Scale Gap

McKinsey's State of AI study reveals the depth of this bottleneck:

- **7%** of organizations claim to be fully at scale with AI and agents
- **62%** are still experimenting or piloting
- The remaining ~31% are in various intermediate states

This means roughly **93% of organizations** have not achieved full-scale AI deployment. ^[extracted]

## Why the Bottleneck Exists

The transition from pilot to scale requires capabilities that most organizations haven't developed:

- **Strategic coordination**: Moving from isolated experiments to a coherent AI strategy
- **Infrastructure**: Production-grade systems that can handle real-world load
- **Organizational change**: Managing the human side of agent deployment and workflow redesign
- **Measurement**: ROI frameworks that capture the full spectrum of AI impact
- **Talent**: Teams with the skills to build, deploy, and maintain AI systems at scale

## The Agent Exception

Interestingly, agents have moved through the pilot phase faster than expected. While most enterprise AI remains in pilot mode, agent adoption jumped from 11% to 42% in production deployment in just three quarters (KPMG data). This suggests that agents may be a special case — simpler to deploy incrementally than other AI applications — but even agent adoption exists within the broader pilot bottleneck context. ^[extracted]
