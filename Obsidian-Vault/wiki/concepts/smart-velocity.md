# Smart Velocity

Smart velocity is the principle that speed in AI product development is only valuable when paired with clarity of direction. Introduced by [[entities/sprout-social|Sprout Social]] at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 as a core component of their [[concepts/ai-first-transformation]] ways of working. ^[extracted]

## The Problem Speed Alone

The talk identifies a fundamental tension: "If you move fast without clarity of direction, you end up with chaos. And if you have clarity of direction with no momentum, you stagnate." ^[extracted]

In an AI world where capability evolution is measured in months, the instinct is to prioritize pure speed. But speed without direction produces waste at an accelerated rate. ^[inferred]

## What Smart Velocity Looks Like

Smart velocity means moving fast with purpose: ^[extracted]

- **Prototyping quickly** — but always against a clearly understood problem ^[extracted]
- **Building MVPs** — but ships are driven by validated direction, not speed targets ^[extracted]
- **Momentum with adaptability** — not just moving faster, but adjusting course as learning accumulates ^[inferred]
- **"Building the muscle to move fast with purpose"** — the capability to accelerate execution without losing strategic alignment ^[extracted]

## Relationship to Execution as Moat

Smart velocity is connected to the [[concepts/execution-as-moat|execution as moat]] thesis: if execution speed is the only durable advantage in AI, then direction-quality matters as much as pace. Raw speed without strategic clarity burns resources without building defensibility. ^[inferred]
