# AI App Ideation Methods

Effective ideation for AI applications relies on systematic capture of opportunities rather than waiting for inspiration.

## Source-Driven Ideation

The most reliable method is **listening to user requests** on social platforms like Twitter. When someone tweets "can someone build an app to do X," that is a validated demand signal — someone already wants this. This approach led to apps like:

- A glasses-picking tool (built from a Twitter request)
- A git commit message writer (built from a friend's tweet)

## The Running Idea List

Most people have great ideas at random times but forget them. The key differentiator is **writing them down**. Maintain a running list of ideas, especially a shortlist of your top five apps to build next. This creates a prioritized queue that you can draw from when you have capacity. ^[extracted]

## Model-Driven Interruption

New open-source model releases can interrupt your idea queue. When a breakthrough model drops (e.g., a new image model), the best builders scramble to build an app with it immediately. This requires having ideas ready to map onto new capabilities. ^[extracted]

## Naming as a Strategic Step

Hassan emphasizes that naming is another critical phase of the ideation-to-build pipeline — a good name can significantly impact adoption and discoverability. ^[extracted]

## Implication

Ideation is not a one-time event but an ongoing practice. The builders who ship the most are those who capture ideas continuously and maintain a prioritized backlog. ^[inferred]
