# Techno-Social Co-Evolution

Techno-social co-evolution describes the feedback loop between human cognition and the technologies we create. As [[entities/danielle-perszyk|Danielle Perszyk]] describes it: "We invent new technologies that then shape us." ^[extracted]

## The Cognitive Flywheel

The pattern follows a repeating cycle: ^[extracted]

1. **More powerful brains/computers** — Our cognitive capacity grows
2. **We connect them together** — Through language, writing, networks, social structures
3. **Enables more sophisticated learning** — Social information processing, distributed cognition
4. **Makes our brains/computers even more powerful** — Feedback loop accelerates

Perszyk traces this pattern across evolutionary and technological history: from the development of social cognition in early hominids (~6 million years ago) to the invention of computers to the current era of AI agents. ^[extracted]

## Offloading and Augmentation

A core mechanism of techno-social co-evolution is cognitive offloading. We distribute our cognition across the environment — first to other people's minds (through language), then to external tools. [[entities/douglas-engelbart|Douglas Engelbart]] recognized this when he said "the development of artificial intelligence has been going on for centuries" through the steady evolution of human augmentation tools. ^[extracted]

## Implications for AI Agents

Perszyk argues that current AI development is simply the latest chapter in this co-evolutionary story. Just as computers reshaped our brains and changed how we think, AI agents will reshape us in turn — but the outcome depends on how we build them. If designed thoughtfully, agents become what she calls "our collective subconscious" — doing repetitive work, redistributing skills, and discovering new knowledge. ^[inferred]
