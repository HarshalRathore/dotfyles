# Sequence of Moats

The sequence of moats is a competitive strategy concept articulated by [[entities/jamin-ball|Jamin Ball]], partner at Altimeter Capital, and cited by [[entities/brian-balfour|Brian Balfour]] at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025. It describes a fundamental shift in how competitive advantage works in the AI era.

## Core Idea

> "The real moat is just a sequence of smaller moats stacked together. Each one buys time. What you do with that time, how fast you execute, how quickly you evolve, determines whether you stay ahead." — Jamin Ball ^[extracted]

## The Shrinking Moat Timeline

- **Traditional moats**: 6 to 12 months of competitive advantage
- **AI-era moats**: 2 to 3 weeks ^[extracted]

This compression means no single feature, dataset, or capability provides durable protection. Instead, teams must plan for continuous re-investment and re-invention.

## Relationship to the Lego Blocks Framework

The sequence of moats is the temporal dimension of the [[concepts/ai-lego-blocks-framework|AI Lego Blocks Framework]]: ^[inferred]

1. **Initial assembly**: Build a differentiated product by combining proprietary data, functionality, and off-the-shelf AI capabilities
2. **First moat**: That assembly buys 2-3 weeks of lead time
3. **Sequence**: Leverage the initial data and functionality into new unique assemblies before competitors catch up
4. **Acceleration**: Each new moat must build on the previous one to compound the advantage

## Practical Implications

- Teams must prioritize execution speed over perfection ^[inferred]
- Architecture decisions should anticipate rapid iteration rather than long-term stability ^[inferred]
- Data moats compound over time — each iteration generates more unique data that feeds the next [[concepts/ai-data-output-flywheel|flywheel]] ^[inferred]
- Product teams need to be sequencing new capabilities in parallel with shipping current ones ^[inferred]
