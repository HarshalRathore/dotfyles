# Human-Computer Interaction

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) is the study of how humans interact with computers, encompassing interface design, usability, and the evolution of interaction paradigms. In the context of AI, HCI is being fundamentally reimagined beyond traditional screen-and-keyboard interfaces. ^[inferred]

## AI and HCI Evolution

Benjamin Dunphy, at the AI Education Summit announcement, drew a parallel between the historical evolution of computers (from large, cold corporate machines to personal companions) and the future of AI education. He argued that AI's growing capabilities should free us from the constraints of traditional HCI paradigms, enabling more natural forms of learning and interaction. ^[extracted]

The core thesis: as AI systems become more capable, the interface between humans and AI should evolve toward more natural, less constrained interaction models — potentially enabling even mediocre teachers to become world-class instructors through AI augmentation. ^[extracted]
