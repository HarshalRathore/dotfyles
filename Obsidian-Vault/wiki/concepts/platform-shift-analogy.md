# Platform Shift Analogy for Voice AI

Voice computing is to text computing what mobile computing was to desktop computing — a fundamental platform shift that changes how humans interact with computers.^[extracted] This analogy, articulated by Sean DuBois of OpenAI at AIEF2025, frames voice as the next major interface paradigm for the generative AI era.^[extracted]

## The Late 2007 Analogy

DuBois compares the current state of voice computing to late 2007, the era of the first iPhone.^[extracted] Just as no one in 2007 had yet invented pull-to-refresh or swiping gestures, the fundamental interaction patterns for voice AI have not yet been discovered.^[extracted] The infrastructure is being built now — WebRTC, voice agents, open-source frameworks — but the killer interfaces are still to come.^[extracted]

## Desktop-to-Mobile Parallel

The desktop-to-mobile transition freed computing from being tethered to a desk. Voice computing frees computing from needing hands.^[extracted] When your hands are occupied but your voice is available, voice becomes the natural interface. This parallels how mobile computing made computers available in situations where a laptop was impractical.^[extracted]

## The "Next Bicycle for the Mind"

DuBois calls voice "the next bicycle for the mind" — a metaphor extending Steve Jobs' description of the personal computer. Voice computing amplifies human cognition by adding a hands-free interaction modality, just as a bicycle amplifies human locomotion.^[extracted]

## Decentralized Computing Model

Voice AI enables a new computing model: keep all computing power remote in the cloud while having small, always-available devices locally.^[extracted] This combines cloud-scale processing power with always-on, hands-free interaction — a pattern that mirrors how mobile devices access cloud services while remaining lightweight and portable.^[extracted]
