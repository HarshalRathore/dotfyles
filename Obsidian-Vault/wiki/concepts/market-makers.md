# Market Makers

Market makers are third-party institutions that manage the differences between buyers and sellers in a commerce system. ^[extracted]

## Financial Markets Analogy

In financial markets, market makers and third-party institutions manage the complex, changing, and often conflicting preferences between buyers and sellers. They provide liquidity, price discovery, and trust in markets where participants have a disincentive to honestly report their preferences. ^[extracted]

## Application to Agentic Commerce

[[entities/adam-behrens|Adam Behrens]] argues that agentic commerce needs to move toward a similar model. The current approach of naively trusting the information given to agents is insufficient. ^[extracted]

Just as finance developed market makers to handle the gap between buyer and seller information, agentic commerce will need intermediaries that can:

- Reason over buyer and seller intents
- Negotiate between conflicting preferences
- Provide price discovery across fragmented product data
- Manage trust when participants don't fully disclose their preferences ^[inferred]

## The Frontier

The frontier is adding intelligence to decision-making at each component — consumers and merchants need not just intents and preferences, but intelligence that can reason over them and negotiate them. ^[extracted]
