## Product Taste

Product Taste is the third dimension of Personal Clarity (alongside vision and purpose), identified by Raiza Martin as essential for forceful product building. While Martin signals she will elaborate on this concept separately in her talk, the surrounding context provides useful framing.

### The Context

Product Taste sits within Martin's framework of personal clarity — the three things that give a product builder the energy to push forward against resistance. Taste, in this context, is not about aesthetics alone but about having a refined sense of what good interaction design looks like, especially in the AI era.

### Taste in the AI Era

The AI era creates a unique challenge for taste: the interaction possibilities have expanded dramatically, but most products still use pre-AI interaction models. Product Taste is the ability to see past the current "ugly" state of software and imagine what good could look like when interactions are AI-native — where you describe what you want and get something magical back.

### Taste vs. Consensus

Taste is inherently individual. It is one of the reasons Martin argues that clarity starts with a singular individual rather than emerging from team consensus. You cannot consensus your way to good taste — you need someone with the taste to see what others cannot yet see.

### Relationship to Consumer Unrest

Product Taste is what allows a product builder to recognize when consumer unrest is justified. When users experience AI-native products and then encounter legacy software, the friction they feel is a signal that the product builder's taste has been calibrated to a higher standard.
