## What VCs Actually Invest In at Early Stages

At pre-seed and even seed stages, investors are not betting on a finished product. They are betting on three things: the founder, the team the founder can recruit, and the founder's vision for what the company can become. ^[extracted]

This is fundamentally different from later-stage investing, where metrics like ARR, growth rate, and product-market fit become the primary evaluation criteria. ^[extracted]

## The Product Evolution Reality

A good investor knows that the product you pitch on day one will look dramatically different in 7–10 years. ^[extracted] The likelihood that the initial product is the final, sustainable version is low. ^[extracted] Therefore, over-emphasizing having a "perfect, beautiful product on day one" is a strategic mistake. ^[extracted]

## The Right Focus

Founders should over-emphasize:
- Their own core abilities and judgment
- The team they can build around them
- Their unique insight into the market

Rather than being hyper-obsessed with building the perfect product before approaching investors. ^[inferred]

## Implication for AI Engineers

For AI engineers considering startups: your technical capability and market insight matter more than having a polished demo. Investors at early stages are looking for founders who can see something others don't and can build the team to execute on that vision. ^[inferred]
