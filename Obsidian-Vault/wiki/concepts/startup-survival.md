# Startup Survival

**Startup survival** is the principle that the primary determinant of startup success is not product quality or market timing, but simply staying around long enough to hit product-market fit. The competitor who runs out of runway first loses, regardless of how good their product is.

## The Core Law

Eric Simons of Bolt.new illustrates this with a concrete example: one of StackBlitz's main competitors in the IDE space was acquired and "stripped for parts" two weeks before Bolt.new launched. That competitor was "on the same trajectory" and "would have been meaningful competitors" — but they didn't have enough runway to reach the other side.^{inferred}

This is the fundamental law of startup survival: **duration beats quality**. A good product with no runway loses to a great product with runway.

## The Seven-Year Test

StackBlitz itself survived seven years before Bolt.new launched. During those seven years:
- ARR grew from zero to $0.7M
- The team stayed aligned and lean
- They kept iterating on the same core technology
- They nearly shut down, then launched Bolt.new as their "last effort"

Seven years of survival is not a failure — it's the cost of finding PMF. The question is not "how fast did we find it" but "did we survive long enough to find it."

## Survival Strategies

1. **Keep burn rate low.** Humans are the most expensive line item. Fewer people = more runway.
2. **Stay aligned.** A small, aligned team outlasts a large, fractured one.
3. **Build grit.** The capacity to endure uncertainty and keep going.
4. **Take shots on goal.** More iterations = higher probability of hitting PMF.
5. **Accept near-death.** Simons was prepared to shut down the company. This acceptance freed him to take the Bolt.new bet without desperation.

## The Bootstrapping Advantage

Simons and his co-founder learned survival skills through bootstrapping a previous company — being broke, living on couches, learning "how far a dollar can stretch." This experience made StackBlitz inherently lean and survival-oriented from day one.
