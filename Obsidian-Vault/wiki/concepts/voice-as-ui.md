# Voice as the Next UI Paradigm

Voice is going to be the core building block of the next generation of user interfaces for the generative AI era.^[extracted] This vision, articulated by Sean DuBois of OpenAI at AIEF2025, positions voice computing as the next major platform shift — analogous to the transition from desktop to mobile computing in the 2000s.^[extracted]

## The Desktop-to-Mobile Analogy

DuBois draws a direct parallel between the current state of voice computing and late 2007 (the era of the first iPhone).^[extracted] Just as no one in 2007 had invented pull-to-refresh, the fundamental interaction patterns for voice AI have not yet been discovered.^[extracted] The infrastructure is being built now; the killer interfaces are still to come.^[extracted]

## Voice as "The Next Bicycle for the Mind"

The "bicycle for the mind" metaphor (originally from Steve Jobs describing the personal computer) is repurposed for voice: voice computing frees us from needing our hands to interact with computers.^[extracted] When your hands are occupied but your voice is available, voice computing becomes the natural interface.^[extracted]

## Current Voice Computing Patterns

People already spend hours talking to computers for:
- Driving development environments with voice
- Brainstorming sessions
- Personal assistant interactions
- Coaching and therapy
- Research assistance^[extracted]

These patterns are early iterations — the real form factors are still being discovered.^[extracted]

## Decentralized Computing

Voice AI enables a new computing model: keep all computing power remote in the cloud while having small, always-available devices locally.^[extracted] This combines the processing power of cloud AI with the convenience of always-on, hands-free interaction.^[extracted]
