# Meter Chart and LLM Task Capability

Kevin Madura referenced a chart from an organization called Meter that evaluates LLM capability by measuring the length of tasks that LLMs can complete with at least 50% success rate. The chart shows a steep takeoff rate in LLM task completion capability.

Madura's interpretation: the steep curve is "mostly because it's a verifiable domain" — software development has clear correctness criteria (tests pass/fail), making LLM progress measurable and compounding. Model capabilities are "a little bit jagged" — they perform very well in verifiable domains but less so in messy, non-verifiable domains like general knowledge work.

The key insight for professional services: the Meter chart is a "rough proxy for the coming disruption for professional services and knowledge work more broadly." However, Madura cautioned that "the takeoff will not be as steep as software engineering" because of "the messiness of the real world" in knowledge work — there are fewer clear correctness criteria, more ambiguity, and more subjective judgment required.

This framing helps explain why AI adoption in consulting feels different from AI adoption in software development: the disruption is real but uneven, with some engagement types (due diligence, document review) seeing dramatic AI impact while others (strategic advisory, relationship management) see more modest gains.
