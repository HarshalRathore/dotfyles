## Multimodal Reading Experience

A multimodal reading experience combines text with other sensory inputs — AI-generated graphics, AI-generated music, character avatars, and interactive voice Q&A — to transform reading into something closer to watching a movie.

### Components

1. **Text** — The original book content, preserved
2. **Graphics** — AI-generated visuals for scenes and settings
3. **Music** — AI-generated background music that matches the emotional tone of each scene
4. **Character avatars** — Visual representations of characters that appear when they're in a scene
5. **Voice Q&A** — Interactive voice interaction where readers can ask questions about what's happening

### The Philosophy

Gądecki noted that nothing in this approach is fundamentally new — you could already ask a friend about a book, or read with background music. But AI makes it possible at scale and on-demand. The key insight is that these simple elements, when connected together, create a magical experience.

Music is particularly powerful: it "influences emotions hugely" and when it "flows with the book," it creates a reading experience unlike anything previously possible. Before AI, producing graphics and music for every book would cost ~$100,000 per book — AI reduces this to near-zero marginal cost.

### Example: The Snow Queen

One early example showed the scene where the sorcerer's parentheses fly away with a reality-distorting mirror. The experience included narration, music matching the scene's tone, and visuals — all AI-generated but human-curated.
