# DALL-E Inflection Point

January 2022, when Sam Altman began tweeting DALL·E 2 images, marked the inflection point for generative media. The public demonstration of AI image generation at a quality that surprised even industry observers triggered a rapid cascade of open-source releases, competitive responses, and commoditization that transformed the field within months.^[extracted]

## The Moment

Gorkem Yurtseven describes sitting on his floor at the end of COVID, watching Altman tweet and reply to users with DALL·E 2 images. Despite looking "kind of bad quality" in retrospect, it was "the most incredible technology ever" at the time — one of the biggest "WTF moments" of his life. He recognized OpenAI's lead and believed it would be extremely hard for others to catch up.^[extracted]

## The Cascade

Within weeks of the DALL·E 2 tweets:

- **MidJourney** released its initial model in beta as a Discord bot (April 2022)
- **Stable Diffusion** open-sourced its model, enabling home GPU inference
- A flood of new image models followed (StableXL, Flux, and others)

The playing field evened out remarkably quickly, driving the marginal cost of creation toward zero.^[extracted]

## Historical Pattern

This pattern — a frontier demonstration followed by rapid open-source replication and commoditization — has repeated in AI. Yurtseven's experience with the DALL·E 2 moment led him to predict the same pattern would hold for Sora, and the prediction proved accurate.^[inferred]

## Prior Waves

The DALL·E 2 moment was not the first AI art wave. Prior efforts included:

- Harold Cohen's ARIAN drawing system (1960s–2000s)
- GAN breakthroughs
- Google's Deep Dream
- PRISM (viral selfie avatar app)

But none of these approached the capabilities or application breadth of the post-DALL·E 2 era.^[extracted]
