# Data Coverage Transformation

Kevin Madura at AIEF2025 described a fundamental shift in data analysis enabled by AI: the transition from partial sampling to complete corpus coverage.

The traditional constraint: human throughput limits how much data can be reviewed. For example, reviewing 5,000 contracts at 30 minutes each would require enormous human resources. In practice, firms prioritize the "top 20%" of data — the most valuable or likely-to-be-relevant pieces — and accept that 80% of the corpus goes unanalyzed.

AI removes this throughput constraint. With AI, you can "look at 100% of the corpus of data" — all vendor contracts, all customer records, all documents — and derive insights across the entire dataset. The value isn't just speed; it's completeness. The output is "that much better" because it's based on 100% of the data rather than 20%.

This maps directly to the [[concepts/consulting-engagement-phases|consulting engagement phase model]]: Phase 1 (data ingestion) is where AI delivers the most dramatic compression, but the downstream impact on analysis quality and recommendation quality is the real enterprise value.

The implication for professional services: the competitive advantage shifts from firms that can afford large teams for data processing to firms that can design AI systems for complete data coverage and insight extraction.
