# WF2026 — AI Engineer World's Fair 2026

The AI Engineer World's Fair 2026 (WF2026) is the second edition of the AI Engineer World's Fair conference. It drew 7,000 attendees and featured content across 18 tracks, expo sessions, and four stages with over 100 expo partners. Day two was emceed by [[entities/tejas-kumar|Tejas Kumar]], Developer Advocate at [[entities/ibm|IBM]].

The conference theme emphasized **reliability over velocity** — wiring agents with intent upstream, verification baked into development workflows, and the growing gap between model capability and production trust.

## Day Two Talks

### Anthropic — Fable Model Field Guide

[[references/aief2025-fable-model-field-guide-tariq-shihapar|Tariq Shihapar]] (Anthropic MTS) presented a field guide to working with the new Mythos class of models, particularly **Fable**, describing it as "the map opening up" — like transitioning from a tutorial level to an open-world RPG. Key themes: capability overhang, system prompt evolution, finding unknowns, and "being unreasonable" about trade-offs.

### Sonar — Code Verification and ACDC

[[references/aief2025-code-verification-acdc-tariq-shawkat|Tariq Shawkat]] (CEO, [[entities/sonar-labs|Sonar]]) presented the Agent-Centric Development Cycle (ACDC) framework for verification-powered agentic loops. Covered zero-trust multi-layered verification, the perception agent harness, and data showing AI coding productivity gains dissipating after 3 months without verification.

### Amazon AGI Lab — Perception Agents

[[references/aief2025-perception-agents-jabarth|Jabarth]] (Amazon AGI Lab) presented on perception agents — agents that perceive, plan, and act in real-time on rendered screens rather than APIs. Covered the annotation tool and verification tool as open-source pieces of the perception agent harness, and the "verification wall" for knowledge work.
