## AI Engineer World's Fair 2025

The AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 (AIEF2025) is a conference bringing together AI engineers, product leaders, and builders to discuss the state of AI engineering and product development.

### Talk Catalog

The conference featured numerous talks including:

- Raiza Martin, Huxe (ex NotebookLM) — "Everything is ugly, so go build something that isn't"
- James Lowe, i.AI — "Why your product needs an AI product manager"
- Rick Blalock, Agentuity — "Conquering Agent Chaos"
- Tom Smoker, WhyHow — "Knowledge Graphs in Litigation Agents" — Knowledge graphs and multi-agent systems for class action/mass tort case discovery
- Stephen Chin & Andreas Kollegger, Neo4j — "Top Ten Challenges to Reach AGI" — AGI challenges framed through sci-fi movie memes (Memento, Skynet, Matrix, Monsters Inc., Terminator, Star Wars, Star Trek, Deep Thought)
- And dozens more covering agent architectures, evals, RAG, voice AI, and product strategy

### Topics Covered

The conference covered a broad range of AI engineering topics:
- Agent architectures and orchestration
- AI evaluation frameworks
- RAG and retrieval systems
- Knowledge graphs in domain-specific applications
- Voice AI and real-time conversational interfaces
- Product strategy in the AI era
- Enterprise AI deployments
- Developer tooling and productivity

### AI Engineer World's Fair 2026 (WF2026)

The 2026 edition drew 7,000 attendees and featured 18 tracks. Day two was emceed by [[entities/tejas-kumar|Tejas Kumar]] (IBM). Key talks:

- [[references/aief2025-fable-model-field-guide-tariq-shihapar|Tariq Shihapar, Anthropic]] — Fable/Mythos model field guide, capability overhang, system prompt evolution
- [[references/aief2025-code-verification-acdc-tariq-shawkat|Tariq Shawkat, Sonar]] — ACDC framework for verification-powered agentic loops
- [[references/aief2025-perception-agents-jabarth|Jabarth, Amazon AGI Lab]] — Perception agents and the verification wall for knowledge work
