## Overview

Dani Grant of [[entities/jam-vc|Jam]] and Chelcie Taylor of [[entities/notable-vc|Notable]] presented at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 on the AI engineer's guide to raising VC capital. The talk covered fundamentals of early-stage fundraising: timing, prerequisites, outreach strategies, and founder psychology. ^[extracted]

## Key Claims

### Prerequisites for Raising VC

- **Revenue**: You do not need revenue to raise VC. Pre-seed and seed funds are well-versed in investing in companies pre-revenue. ^[extracted]
- **Product**: You do not need a product to raise VC. Investors at early stages are backing the founder, team, and vision — not a finished product. ^[extracted]
- **Full-time commitment**: Mixed and firm-dependent. Many VCs are comfortable with side-project founders, but going full-time is a strong conviction signal. ^[extracted]
- **Co-founder**: Most VCs-backed companies have co-founders. Solo founders are the exception, not the rule. ^[extracted]

### Timing

- Raise as early as possible — specifically, when you need to scale. ^[extracted]
- It is easier to raise when everything is potential rather than after building a product that needs to "wow" investors. ^[extracted]
- "Raise before you're raising" — start conversations early. ^[extracted]

### Outreach Strategy

- Warm introductions are best, but cold emails do get read. VCs are deal hunters. ^[extracted]
- Cold emails that get answered are usually prompted by a warm signal (e.g., responding to investor content). ^[extracted]
- The best cold emails have: specific asks, warm signals, and tangibility. ^[extracted]
- The job of the email is not to make the sale — it is to compel a response. ^[extracted]
- "Ask for advice, get money" — bringing investors in early for advice creates relationships that become fundraising opportunities. ^[extracted]

### What Investors Evaluate at Early Stages

- The founder's abilities and judgment
- The team they can recruit
- Their unique insight into the market
- Their vision for the company

Not the product, which will look dramatically different in 7–10 years. ^[extracted]

## Speaker Backgrounds

- **Dani Grant** (Jam): Venture investor who reads cold emails as part of her deal-hunting job. Shares that her own fund raised pre-product with her co-founder still employed. ^[extracted]
- **Chelcie Taylor** (Notable): Venture investor who shared real cold email examples from her inbox. Her fund raised pre-product with her co-founder still at his day job. ^[extracted]

## VC Fund Stage Spectrum

The speakers distinguish between:
- Pre-seed/seed funds: invest in people, team, vision — comfortable with pre-revenue
- Later-stage/multi-stage funds: look for ARR growth or ARR full stop ^[extracted]
