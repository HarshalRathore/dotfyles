# Survive the AI Knife Fight: Building Products That Win

> **Talk:** Survive the AI Knife Fight: Building Products That Win
> **Speaker:** [[entities/brian-balfour|Brian Balfour]]
> **Organization:** [[entities/reforge|Reforge]]
> **Event:** AI Engineer World's Fair 2025
> **Source:** [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1MVh05GDydE)

## Summary

A product strategy talk addressing the central question facing AI companies: "What do I build and why will it win?" Balfour argues that tactical and technical advances in AI engineering mean nothing unless teams answer this question correctly. In a landscape where incumbents, new platforms, and well-funded startups compete simultaneously, and where technology shifts on a monthly basis, the path to winning lies not in the AI itself but in how teams assemble off-the-shelf capabilities with their unique assets.

## Key Framework: AI Lego Blocks

Balfour introduces the [[concepts/ai-lego-blocks-framework|AI Lego Blocks Framework]], which positions competitive advantage as emerging from the assembly of three categories of "Lego blocks":

1. **AI Capabilities** (commodity — available to everyone)
2. **Proprietary Data** (unique context for AI outputs)
3. **Product Functionality** (workflows, rules, integrations, algorithms)

The key: neither building custom AI infrastructure (reinventing the wheel) nor merely adding chatbot features (copying) suffices. The answer lies in the middle — assembling blocks in unique configurations.

## The Traps

- **Trap 1**: Building custom models and infrastructure when off-the-shelf suffices ^[extracted]
- **Trap 2**: Just copying basic AI features like chatbots ^[extracted]
- **The right path**: Assembling differentiated AI features by integrating the best available AI capabilities with your product's data and functionality ^[extracted]

## Case Study: Granola

The talk uses [[entities/granola|Granola]] as a detailed case study of the framework in action — an AI note-taking product that entered a crowded market but found a seam by identifying an unmet customer need and using off-the-shelf AI capabilities combined with unique data and functionality.

## The Competitive Landscape

Balfour characterizes the AI product environment as a "Gettysburg map" with: ^[extracted]

- Fast, huge moving incumbents (Microsoft, Google, Meta)
- New horizontal platforms (ChatGPT, Anthropic) eating major use cases
- Foundational technology shifts on a monthly basis
- Hordes of well-funded startups in every category

## Sequencing as Strategy

Initial differentiation is not enough. Balfour emphasizes that teams must sequence their advantage continuously — each assembly of Lego blocks buys limited time before competitors catch up. The [[concepts/sequence-of-moats|sequence of moats]] concept captures this dynamic.
