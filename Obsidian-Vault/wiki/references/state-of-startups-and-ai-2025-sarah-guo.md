# State of Startups and AI 2025 — Sarah Guo, Conviction

> [!tldr] Sarah Guo argues we are in the "dial-up era" of AI — massive value creation ahead, but building durable AI products is harder than expected. The winning playbook: apply the [[concepts/cursor-for-x|Cursor-for-X]] recipe to every vertical, build [[concepts/copilot-first-strategy|copilots first]] and ride the capability wave, and accept that [[concepts/execution-as-moat|execution is the only defensibility]].

## Key Claims

- **Reasoning is a new scaling vector** — unlocking transparent high-stakes decisions, sequential problems, and systematic search. This benefits knowledge work. ^[extracted]
- **Agent startups are proliferating** — Conviction's Embed grant program saw 50% more agent startup applications year-over-year. ^[extracted]
- **Voice is the first modality to hit business workflows** — because it maps onto existing business voice use cases (medical consults, lead gen) that couldn't scale before. ^[extracted]
- **Code was first because engineers built for engineers** — they understood the workflow intimately. That "build for yourself" pattern is the playbook for every other industry. ^[extracted]
- **Cursor is a thick wrapper, not a thin wrapper** — orchestrating multiple models (diff, merge, embeddings), packaging context, skillful prompting, and a UX that makes it safe to say yes. "$14-15B wrapper." ^[extracted]
- **Last year's model is a commodity** — GPT-4 went from $30 to $2 per million tokens in 18 months. Distilled versions at $0.10. Plan for a [[concepts/multi-model-world|multi-model world]]. ^[extracted]
- **The AI leapfrog effect** — the most conservative, low-tech industries are adopting AI fastest (legal, customer service, healthcare). ^[extracted]
- **Execution is the moat** — Cursor didn't invent code completion, models, or their product surface. They out-executed on every dimension. Counter-example: Jasper raised $125M with first-mover brand, but ChatGPT crushed its first iteration. ^[extracted]
- **Co-pilots are underrated** — human tolerance for failure drops dramatically with latency. Build great augmentation (the Iron Man suit) and extend toward autonomy as capability improves. ^[extracted]
- **Transformative companies come later** — Instagram came 4 years after the iPhone, Uber 5, DoorDash 6. The truly transformative companies reimagine experiences, they aren't first to recognize the change. ^[extracted]

## Structure

1. **Capabilities** — reasoning unlocks new use cases; multimodality progressing (voice, video, image); model market getting more competitive, not less
2. **Application layer** — Cursor as the case study; the "Cursor for X" playbook; code was first because of structured text, deterministic validation, and engineers building for themselves
3. **The AI leapfrog effect** — conservative industries (legal via Harvey, customer service via Sierra, medical via Open Evidence) are adopting fastest
4. **Execution is the moat** — Cursor vs Jasper as paired case studies in defensibility
5. **Advice** — build copilots first and extend to autonomy; the dial-up era means transformative companies are yet to come
