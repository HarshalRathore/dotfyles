# AIEF2025 — Rethinking Team Building: how a 30-person Startup serves 50 Million Users

**Speaker:** Grant Lee, Co-founder and CEO of [[entities/gamma|Gamma]]
**Event:** AI Engineer World's Fair 2025
**Duration:** ~18 minutes
**Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q8zoXAbmJdI

## Summary

Grant Lee presents Gamma's organizational design approach — how a team of 30 serves 50 million users by rejecting traditional startup scaling patterns. The talk covers three pillars: hiring generalists, the player-coach leadership model, and lean scaling strategies.

## Key Themes

### 1. Organizational Innovation

Grant argues that founders should apply first-principles thinking to team building the same way they apply it to product building. ^[extracted] Traditional org design — VP → directors → direct reports cascading across every function — is outdated in the AI era. ^[extracted]

### 2. The Rise of the Generalist

Gamma's head of design (also the first hire) exemplifies the ideal generalist: a visual designer who can code, does deep UX research, talks to users, and mentors the team. ^[extracted] Strong generalists both like to learn and like to teach — the ability to articulate and persuade others about new skills is a key interview criterion. ^[extracted]

### 3. Player-Coach Leadership

Adapted from American football, the player-coach model puts leaders on the field who both manage and do hands-on work. ^[extracted] In the AI era, the "game on the field" moves incredibly fast, and player-coaches can read, react, and adjust without waiting for top-down mandates. ^[extracted] Every one of Gamma's core leadership team is a player-coach. ^[extracted]

### 4. Lean Scaling

At Gamma's scale, the team is roughly one-tenth the size a similar company would have needed a few years ago. ^[extracted] This doesn't mean no hiring — it means radically fewer hires relative to output and user count. ^[extracted]

## Three Pillars of Gamma's Org Design

1. **Who to hire** — Generalists over specialists, people who learn and teach
2. **How to manage** — Player-coach leaders with deep context and technical expertise
3. **How to scale** — Lean teams that grow relative to output, not linearly with headcount
