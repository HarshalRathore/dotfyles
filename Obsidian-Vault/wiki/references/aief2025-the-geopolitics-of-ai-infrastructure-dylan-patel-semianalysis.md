# The Geopolitics of AI Infrastructure — Dylan Patel, SemiAnalysis

**Speaker**: Dylan Patel, Founder & Analyst, SemiAnalysis  
**Event**: AI Engineer World's Fair 2025  
**Video**: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Zz4QjZsYWK0

## Summary

Dylan Patel covers three major themes in the geopolitics of AI infrastructure:

1. **China's semiconductor advancement** — Huawei's Ascend chips, supply chain circumvention of US sanctions, and SMIC's domestic manufacturing progress
2. **The failure of export controls** — How Huawei accesses TSMC chips, HBM memory, and packaging equipment despite comprehensive US sanctions
3. **The Middle East AI boom** — G42's 5-gigawatt data center campus, the GPU allocation deal, and the blurring of inference/training infrastructure

## Key Points

### Huawei's Ascend Chips

- Ascend 910B and 910C chips manufactured by [[entities/tsmc|TSMC]] through intermediaries
- Cloud Matrix 384: 384 chips across 12 racks with optical interconnects
- Successfully deployed an architecture that [[entities/nvidia|NVIDIA]] failed to productionize (DGX H100 Ranger)

### Export Control Circumvention

- ~2.9M TSMC chips accessed through Sofgo (cryptocurrency mining shell company)
- HBM reaches China via Samsung → Coasia (Taiwan) → Faraday (dummy chip) → extraction → Huawei Ascend
- ~13 million HBM stacks stacked by Huawei, continuing to receive shipments
- US fined TSMC ~$1B for ~$500M in revenue (described as "slap on the wrist")

### NVIDIA's Perspective

- H20 ban blocked ~1 million GPUs from China
- CFO stated $50B in GPU sales to China would have occurred without restrictions
- $5B write-down of H20 inventory

### Middle East / Stargate

- G42 deal: 500,000 GPUs/year, 20% kept, 80% to US companies
- 5-gigawatt data center campus in UAE (25x larger than typical AI data centers)
- [[entities/microsoft|Microsoft]] is G42's investor and customer
- [[entities/openai|OpenAI]] gets Middle East GPU cluster (unstated component)
- UAE matching investments extend to US (Kentucky, New York)

### Inference-Training Convergence

- Inference clusters in the US run RLVR at night during low utilization
- Lines blurring between inference and training infrastructure
