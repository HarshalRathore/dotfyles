# AIEF2025 - Why We Don't Need More Data Centers - Dr. Jasper Zhang, Hyperbolic

**Speaker**: Dr. Jasper Zhang, CEO and co-founder of [[entities/hyperbolic|Hyperbolic]] ^[extracted]

**Event**: AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 (AIEF2025) ^[extracted]

**Video**: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=M6Vbaig1TsM ^[extracted]

## Summary

Dr. Jasper Zhang presents the thesis that building more data centers alone cannot solve the AI compute shortage. Instead, a GPU marketplace and aggregation layer ([[entities/hyperdos|HyperDOS]]) that makes better use of existing idle capacity is the more efficient solution. ^[extracted]

## Key Claims

1. **Data center build-out is insufficient**: By 2030, demand will grow from 55 GW to 219 GW (22% annual growth). Each data center costs $1B+, electrical grid connections have 7-year waitlists, and a 15+ GW supply deficit will persist even with on-time delivery. ^[extracted]

2. **GPU utilization is extremely low**: Enterprises idle 80% of their GPUs (Deloitte). 100+ GPU clouds exist in a fragmented market. ^[extracted]

3. **Marketplace solves the matching problem**: Aggregating supply and demand through a uniform platform reduces cost by 50–75%, eliminates supplier vetting friction, and enables dynamic allocation. ^[extracted]

4. **Cost savings increase productivity**: A startup can reduce GPU costs from $43.8M to $6.9M (6x saving) through dynamic reservation, burst allocation, and reselling idle GPUs. ^[extracted]

5. **Scaling laws amplify savings**: More compute for the same budget means better models, not just cheaper operations. Startups can move beyond closed models to training their own. ^[extracted]

6. **Sustainability**: Reusing idle compute is more sustainable than building new data centers that consume 4% of US electricity and generate massive CO2. ^[extracted]

7. **Evolution to workload platform**: GPU marketplaces will evolve into all-in-one platforms for AI workloads — training, online inference, offline inference, and model hosting. ^[extracted]
