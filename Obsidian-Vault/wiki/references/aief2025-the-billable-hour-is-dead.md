# The Billable Hour is Dead; Long Live the Billable Hour

**Speakers:** Kevin Madura + Mo Bhasin, [[entities/alix-partners|Alix Partners]]
**Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Wv1tAxKYLeE
**Conference:** AI Engineer World's Fair 2025

## TL;DR

Kevin Madura and Mo Bhasin from [[entities/alix-partners|Alix Partners]] presented on how AI is reshaping professional services and knowledge work, drawing from their experience building an internal GenAI platform (20 engineers, 50 deployments, hundreds of users over 2 years). They framed the enterprise AI productivity paradox, described the three-phase consulting engagement model, demonstrated AI-powered categorization using structured outputs and tool calls, and argued that AI-first firms that replicate senior knowledge will dominate the future of professional services.

## Problem / Motivation

The enterprise AI productivity paradox: 89% of CEOs plan to implement agentic AI (Deloitte), yet NBER reports no significant impact on earnings, BCG says three quarters of companies fail to scale Gen AI value, and S&P Global reports almost half are abandoning AI initiatives. The question: how is everyone spending on AI but no one seeing value?

## Method / Architecture

The talk covered three main areas:

1. **AI reshaping knowledge work** — The Meter chart as a proxy for disruption, junior-led vs senior-led consulting models, the AI-first firm concept (Dwarkesh Patel's "50 copies of the CEO")
2. **Three real-world use cases** — Data ingestion compression (50% → 10-20% of engagement time), 100% data coverage vs 20% sampling, AI-powered categorization using structured outputs + tool calls
3. **What doesn't work and where things are going** — Accuracy requires business-partner relationships, unsupervised learning is "not unchecked," converting skeptics into champions

## Key Insights

- AI compresses Phase 1 of consulting engagements (data ingestion) from ~50% to 10-20% of time
- The value of AI isn't speed — it's complete data coverage enabling better insights across the full corpus
- Employee productivity ≠ enterprise productivity; the gap explains the AI investment paradox
- Structured outputs + tool calling (web queries) enables accurate classification without training data
- The future is a hybrid model where AI replicates senior knowledge and humans handle execution
- Model capabilities are "jagged" — strong in verifiable domains (software), weaker in messy knowledge work

## Limitations

This is a conference talk from professional services practitioners, not an academic paper. The claims about AI impact are based on internal experience at Alix Partners rather than controlled studies. The Meter chart data is from an organization the speakers cited but didn't produce.
