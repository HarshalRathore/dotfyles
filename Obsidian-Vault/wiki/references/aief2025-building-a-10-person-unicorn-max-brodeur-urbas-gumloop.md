## Building a 10 Person Unicorn — Max Brodeur-Urbas, Gumloop

### TL;DR

Max Brodeur-Urbas, founder of [[entities/gumloop|Gumloop]] (YC Winter 2024), shares how his team scaled to nine people after raising a Series A as a team of two. The talk covers three pillars: being super picky in hiring, product-led hiring from the customer base, and making intentional time to work together. Core operational practices include near-zero meetings, deep focus time, and automating every internal process.

### Background

Max's journey to Gumloop involved building several projects: video game moderation software, ML models for detecting children's age in video games (for VR age separation), and bot detection. His side project on top of his side project was Agent Hub, a UI for AutoGPT that he thought would be "GitHub for agents." He built it because people in the AutoGPT Discord were excited about AI but didn't know how to clone repos or set things up locally.

The pivot came when he noticed that people describing what they wanted agents to do were actually describing complex workflows — people who knew Python, API calls, and LLM queries could automate their entire request without hoping an agent would figure it out.

### Three Hiring Pillars

1. **Be super picky.** Every person must be absolutely exceptional. If a hiring decision isn't a "no-brainer," don't hire them. Max consulted the co-founder of [[entities/instacart|Instacart]] (who invested in Gumloop) who emphasized: "if you aren't extremely excited about someone, you shouldn't even consider hiring them."

2. **Product-led hiring.** Two of Gumloop's customers quit their jobs to join because they already loved the product. The Instacart customer who brought Gumloop into Instacart now handles enterprise relationships. The Head of Education came from Webflow with a Zapier course and automation workshops.

3. **Make time to work together.** Work-trial interviews where candidates spend several days on the team, including off-site hacking sessions (e.g., renting Airbnbs for multi-day sessions in Yosemite). This is the only way to know if you actually want to work with someone.

### Three Operational Practices

1. **Almost no meetings.** Let people build. The founder handles customer-facing activities but everyone else's calendar should be "totally blank." Deep focus time is essential for engineers hired to build exceptional products.

2. **Inspire, don't micromanage.** The founder's role shifted from being involved in every feature to inspiring what features should be built based on customer conversations, then letting exceptional people execute.

3. **Automate everything.** Gumloop runs its entire internal operations on its own product. Before every meeting, deep research reports are auto-generated. When interesting users sign up, automated notifications with pre-drafted emails go to the founder's inbox.

### Disclaimer

Max explicitly disclaims expertise: "I don't actually know what I'm talking about. I'm trying to figure out if we're just getting lucky over and over or if our approaches are actually working, but take everything I say with a grain of salt."
