# Bolt.new: How we scaled $0-20m ARR in 60 days, with 15 people — Eric Simons, Bolt

Eric Simons, co-founder of Bolt.new (built by StackBlitz), presented at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 on how a team of fewer than 20 people scaled ARR from $0.7M to $20M in just 60 days after launching Bolt.new in October of the previous year. The company had been operating for seven years before this launch, during which they struggled to find a commercially viable venture-scale offering despite building on the same core technology.

## Key Points

**The ARR ramp was unnaturally clean.** Starting at $0.7M ARR over seven years, Bolt.new's launch produced a near-perfect linear growth curve to $20M ARR in 60 days — doubling that figure subsequently. The product shipped was essentially an MVP stripped down to bare metal, yet the team's ability to scale it under extreme demand was described as "unbelievably impressive."

**Small teams with high context per head are the competitive advantage.** Simons and his co-founder (a childhood friend they've built websites with since age 13) operate on a decade-old mantra: a small number of people with more context per head. This gives everyone agency — they can build without permission chains, move fast, and make immediate impact. The philosophy traces back to their experience bootstrapping a previous company from zero, living on couches, and learning how inefficient most startups are at finding product-market-fit.

**Runway matters more than speed.** Humans are the most expensive line item for a company. Lower burn rate = more time to take "shots on goal." One competitor in their previous IDE space was acquired and "stripped for parts" two weeks before Bolt launched — they were on the same trajectory but ran out of runway first. The lesson: stay around long enough to hit product-market fit, even if it takes longer than expected.

**The 300-moment: surviving extreme scale.** At month two, ~30-40K active customers with a team of <20 people felt like the movie *300*. No one on the team had success or support in their title — Simons' chief of staff and he largely responded to support tickets themselves. What made it work was incredible camaraderie, seven years of working together, shared values, and alignment.

**Low ego, high trust, obsessed with user success.** The team philosophy: low ego, high trust, obsessed with making the user successful, and underneath the chaos — grit and resilience. Without these traits, the extreme situation would have been impossible.

**Firefighting prioritization framework.** When everything is on fire (sometimes good fires = tons of customers, sometimes bad fires = broken things), the framework is: you have one fire truck in a town on fire — prioritize high-impact areas, infrastructure, and key people. Some fires will burn and that's okay. Focusing on 10% of things often gets the lion's share of results. A larger team creates pressure to do everything; a small team forces clearer thinking.

## Notable Quotes

- "If you can add $100,000 of ARR by the end of the year, that would be game changing" — expectations before Bolt launch
- "You really want a small number of people with more context per head" — core company mantra
- "Getting product-market-fit is like an enterprise sales pipeline" — metaphor for startup discovery
- "Focusing on 10% of the things often gets you the lion's share of the result that actually matters" — prioritization insight
