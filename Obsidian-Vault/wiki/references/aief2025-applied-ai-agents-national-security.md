Mark Machotton, Enterprise AI Architect at [[entities/los-alamos-national-laboratory|Los Alamos National Laboratory]], delivered a talk at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 covering LANL's approach to agentic AI for national security science.

**Key topics:**

- **70 years of applied AI:** LANL has been doing applied statistics and ML since 1956 (Los Alamos Chess on Maniac I), predating modern AI. Monte Carlo methods developed then are still in use today.
- **Agentic AI for science:** Demonstrated an AI agent that reads scientific papers, generates hypotheses, and designs an inertial confinement fusion (ICF) capsule for [[entities/livermore-national-laboratory|Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory]]. The agent executes code on LANL's HPC assets, running thermodynamic and hydrodynamic simulations — not just returning text.
- **Venato supercomputer:** 2,500+ nodes of NVIDIA Grace Hopper superchips built with [[entities/nvidia|NVIDIA]] and [[entities/hpe|HPE]]. Brings frontier models (including [[entities/openai|OpenAI]]'s) onto classified networks.
- **Partnerships:** Academic (UC family of schools), industry (NVIDIA, HPE, OpenAI). LANL positions itself as "a safe place to do dangerous things" for frontier labs.
- **Governance landscape:** OMB Memoranda M-25-21 and M-25-22 (April 2025) codify AI governance for government agencies. FedRAMP Rev4 has 1,000+ security controls. DoD's Security Requirements Guide (SRG) adds two more impact levels on top of FedRAMP. CNSSI 1253 adds another layer. AI governance strategies are still in a 180-day rule-making period.
- **Data classification:** LANL handles open/public, controlled unclassified, classified, and DOE-restricted data. Nuclear weapon physics is "born classified and stays classified forever."
- **Mission depth:** AI must go beyond PowerPoints and email summaries — it must integrate into mission-critical workflows (payroll, procurement, cybersecurity, nuclear stockpile stewardship).
