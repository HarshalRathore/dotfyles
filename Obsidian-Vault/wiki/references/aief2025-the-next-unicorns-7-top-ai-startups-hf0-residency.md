# The Next Unicorns: 7 Top AI Startups from the HF0 Residency

Diego Rodriguez, co-founder and CTO of CREA, presents seven AI startups from the HF0 Residency at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025. The talk opens with three framing stories about unpredictability (traffic vs. cars), language barriers (Tower of Babel), and speed of AI adoption (Korea Fox ad broadcast in two days), before introducing each startup.

## Opening Stories

Diego Rodriguez frames the talk with three stories:

1. **Cars vs. Traffic** — "If you think about it, cars are easy to predict. You get the horse, you have the wheels, you swap the horse, and you put an engine, which was known at the time, and that's a car. But you know what's really hard to predict? Traffic." The point: AI can generate millions of images per day, but finding and managing personalized content at scale is the real challenge ^[extracted].

2. **Tower of Babel** — "We wanted to reach heaven, God was like, no, created a bunch of languages, and basically misunderstanding was like, nope, you're not going to go there." He connected this to stand-up meetings where people argue over tech stacks ("It should be React. It should be JavaScript") — comparing these to God winning over human attempts at unified communication ^[extracted]. "Now we have AI, so maybe, okay, if it wasn't with crypto, it was with AI" — people trying to convey ideas ^[extracted].

3. **Korea Fox Ad** — A friend from Netflix asked how she finds personalized content for each town in India. Then Diego learned Korea was already broadcasting an ad with Fox to millions of people. "We went from sign up to conversion to payment to broadcast in two days" ^[extracted].

## Key Themes

- **Voice as interface**: Open Home positions natural conversation as the primary interaction model, replacing command-based language ^[extracted].
- **Reliability over capability**: Federalist AI's core thesis — models are already smart enough; the bottleneck is reliability for boring tasks ^[extracted].
- **Incentive protocols**: Glow applies Bitcoin-like incentive mechanisms to solar panel construction in developing markets ^[extracted].
- **Inference cost innovation**: CoreKey 72B achieves 1000x lower inference cost through non-transformer architecture ^[extracted].
- **Voice model expressiveness**: OpenAudio's S1 introduces instruction-based voice control, surpassing 11 Labs on TTS Arena ^[extracted].
- **LLM marketplace consolidation**: OpenRouter abstracts multi-provider inference into a single API with best-pricing routing ^[extracted].

## Speaker Background

Diego Rodriguez frames himself as someone who "will try to hire you" — the talk doubles as a recruitment pitch. He mentions CREA has an email created for today that will "prioritize applications" ^[extracted].
