# AIEF2025 - #define AI Engineer - Greg Brockman, OpenAI (ft. Jensen Huang)

**Speaker:** Greg Brockman (with Jensen Huang)
**Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=avWhreBUYF0
**Event:** AI Engineer World's Fair 2025

## Summary

An interview-style talk at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 featuring [[entities/greg-brockman|Greg Brockman]] discussing his journey from aspiring mathematician to co-founder of [[entities/openai|OpenAI]] and first CTO of [[entities/stripe|Stripe]]. [[entities/jensen-huang|Jensen Huang]] appears as a featured co-host. The conversation covers Brockman's accidental path into programming, Stripe's early engineering culture, and his philosophy on speed, constraints, and independent study.

## Key Topics

### Accidental Path to Programming
Brockman originally wanted to be a mathematician, inspired by [[concepts/first-principles-thinking|Galois and Gauss]] and their work on century-spanning problems. He went to [[entities/w3schools|W3Schools]] to learn PHP after a friend discouraged him from publishing a chemistry textbook. His first project was a table-sorting widget — the moment it worked felt like "magic." ^[extracted]

### Building Over Theorizing
Brockman contrasts mathematics (where you write a proof and three people care) with programming (where everyone benefits without understanding the code). This philosophy led him to abandon the 100-year time horizon in favor of building. ^[extracted]

### Stripe's Early Culture
As the first CTO of Stripe (growing from 4 to 250 people), Brockman describes extreme customer proximity — adding all customers on Gchat, [[entities/patrick-collison|Patrick Collison]] personally installing Stripe at customer offices. The Wells Fargo integration was completed in 24 hours instead of the claimed nine months. ^[extracted]

### First Principles on Speed
Brockman's nuanced view: it's not about ignoring all constraints, but about finding where there is unnecessary overhead from constraints that no longer apply. This philosophy becomes especially relevant with AI tools like [[entities/codex|Codex]] accelerating productivity. ^[extracted]

### Independent Study
Brockman's lifelong practice of self-directed learning, from skipping pre-algebra in seventh grade through [[entities/recur-center|Recur Center]] programs and sabbaticals. ^[extracted]
