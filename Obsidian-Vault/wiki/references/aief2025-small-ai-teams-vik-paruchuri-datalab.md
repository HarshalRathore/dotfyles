# Small AI Teams with Huge Impact — Vik Paruchuri, Datalab

**Speaker**: [[entities/vik-paruchuri|Vik Paruchuri]], CEO of [[entities/datalab|Datalab]]
**Event**: AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 (AIEF2025)
**Video**: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=K-iYKDMFKhE

## Summary

Vik Paruchuri shares how he built [[entities/datalab|Datalab]] into a seven-figure ARR company with a team of 3-4 people, achieving 40k GitHub stars and training state-of-the-art AI models. His core thesis: head count does not equal productivity.

## Key Claims

- Datalab reached seven-figure ARR with a team of 4, growing revenue 5x since January 2025
- Customers include tier-one AI labs, universities, Fortune 500, and AI startups including [[entities/gamma|Gamma]]
- Built [[entities/marker|Marker]] and [[entities/surya|Surya OCR]] open-source projects
- Surya OCR3: 500M parameters, 90 languages, 99% accuracy, unique features (character-level bounding boxes, PDF text grounding)
- Previous company [[entities/dataquest|DataQuest]] became more productive after layoffs (30→15→7)
- Small teams of generalists outperform large teams of specialists
- AI tools multiply productivity by handling "easy low leverage pieces"

## Five Pillars of Small AI Teams

1. Hire fewer than 15 generalists
2. Fill edges with AI and internal tooling
3. Use simple, boring technology (no Kubernetes for 3-person companies)
4. Work in person (not remote)
5. High trust, continuous discussions, minimal bureaucracy

## Hiring Process

Three steps: (1) peer chat, (2) paid ~10-hour project ($1,000), (3) culture fit review. ~40% hire rate from interviewed candidates.

## Tech Stack

Server-rendered HTML with HTMX and Alpine.js (no React). Clean, modular, well-documented code optimized for AI-assisted development.
