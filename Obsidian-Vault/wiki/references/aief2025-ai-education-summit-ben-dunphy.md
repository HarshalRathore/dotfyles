# AI Education Summit Announcement

**Speaker:** Benjamin Dunphy
**Event:** AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 (AIEF2025) — Day 2
**Video:** https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=U-fMsbY-kHY_test

## Summary

Benjamin Dunphy, co-founder of AI Engineer World's Fair 2025, announced the AI Education Summit as a new initiative. He shared his personal journey with AI (from his "AGI achieved" moment to his son's birth) and framed AI education as critical for preparing children, parents, and educators for an AI-driven world.

## Key Points

### Personal AI Journey

Benjamin described his "oh shit moment" with AI as prompting ChatGPT to recursively decompose the universe into core principles and subclassifications. He was "blown away" by the responses — finding them both fascinating and wrong (e.g., classifying viruses as a subcategory of quarks). He immediately texted his brother-in-law "AGI has been achieved." ^[extracted]

A few months later, his son was born. Benjamin draws a parallel: with computers, his mind feels expanded; with his son, his heart expands. This personal reflection shaped his view on AI education. ^[extracted]

### The Education Gap

Benjamin frames the core problem: a significant gap between rapid AI advancement and the preparedness of children, parents, and educators to navigate AI effectively and ethically. His solution is a global community dedicated to AI education with three pillars:
1. Knowledge
2. Skills
3. Ethical framework

### HCI Evolution

He draws a historical parallel: computers evolved from large, cold corporate machines to personal companions. Similarly, AI education must evolve beyond screen-and-keyboard interfaces and classroom-style learning. AI's potential can free us from these constraints, enabling even mediocre teachers to become world-class instructors. ^[extracted]

### First Event Details

- **Format:** Free online event
- **Keynote:** Stefania Druga (former Google research scientist, three-time AI speaker)
- **Focus:** Practical knowledge for the future of AI education
- **Audience:** Parents, educators, and anyone interested in AI's evolution for learning

Stefania's previous AIEF2024 talk demonstrated a student learning to code by programming "the very thing that is teaching them to code" — a recursive self-improvement loop in educational AI. ^[extracted]
