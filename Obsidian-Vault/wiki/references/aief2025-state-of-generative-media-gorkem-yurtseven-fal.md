# The State of Generative Media - Gorkem Yurtseven, FAL

Gorkem Yurtseven of File.ai (FAL) delivered a talk at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 tracing the history and trajectory of generative media — defined as generative video, audio, and image creation powered by an inference engine, with partnerships spanning both open-source and closed-source model providers.

## Key Claims

The talk opens with a historical arc: from Harold Cohen's ARIAN drawing system through GAN breakthroughs, Deep Dream, and the PRISM avatar app, culminating in the January 2022 inflection point when Sam Altman tweeted DALL·E 2 images. Within weeks, the playing field evened out — MidJourney released its Discord bot beta, Stable Diffusion open-sourced its model enabling home GPU inference, and a flood of new image models followed (StableXL, Flux). This rapid commoditization drove the marginal cost of creation toward zero.^[extracted]

Yurtseven draws a critical distinction between the marginal cost of *creation* (approaching zero) and the marginal cost of *creativity* (still valuable). Storytelling and creative direction remain important; the technology removes friction from production, not from ideas.^[extracted]

## Industry Impact Predictions

The talk identifies three industries primed for generative media transformation:

**Advertising** — predicted as the first industry impacted at scale. Yurtseven argues ads are the perfect use case because (1) the industry can absorb unlimited content volume, (2) ads can be hyper-personalized (10,000 demographic variants generated quickly, or on-the-fly personalized to individual users), and (3) the ad industry has grown threefold since 2000 with all growth concentrated in software ads. He predicts AI-driven ads will similarly drive industry expansion.^[extracted]

**E-commerce** — described as having one of the clearest product-market fits in generative media, with virtual try-on technology already adopted by multiple retailers and startups over the past year. Online shopping's inherently visual nature makes it naturally suited to AI interactivity.^[extracted]

**Video** — predicted to become 100–250× larger than the image generation market. Yurtseven's rough math: video is 20× more compute-intensive, 5× more engaging, and useful to more industries. He cites his own company's revenue data as a market proxy — video model usage grew from near-zero in October to 18% by February and ~30% at the time of the talk.^[extracted]

## Case Study: A24 Civil War Campaign

File.ai ran an interactive live-marketing campaign for A24's *Civil War* movie, allowing users to upload selfies that were transformed into toy soldiers displayed in Times Square. This exemplifies the kind of personalized, interactive experiences that generative media enables.^[extracted]

## Historical Context

The talk frames generative media as the latest wave in a long lineage: computer graphics since the dawn of computing, Harold Cohen's ARIAN system creating art on massive canvases, GAN-era breakthroughs, Deep Dream, PRISM selfies — but notes that none of these previous waves approached the capabilities or application breadth of today's generative media landscape.^[extracted]
