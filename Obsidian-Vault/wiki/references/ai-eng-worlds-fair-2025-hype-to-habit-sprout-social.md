# From Hype to Habit: How We're Building an AI-First SaaS Company—While Still Shipping the Roadmap

> **Talk:** From Hype to Habit: How We're Building an AI-First SaaS Company—While Still Shipping the Roadmap
> **Speakers:** [[entities/rossella|Rossella]] & [[entities/debsha|Debsha]]
> **Organization:** [[entities/sprout-social|Sprout Social]]
> **Event:** AI Engineer World's Fair 2025
> **Source:** [YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=3YGRcgZJ3yc)

## Summary

A candid, real-time account of Sprout Social's AI transformation journey. The talk argues that AI transformation is "not binary — it's not like flipping a switch" and structured as a multi-dimensional evolution organized around three pillars: **Strategy**, **Ways of Working**, and **People**. The speakers share what's working, what's not, and a practical framework to guide companies through their own AI transformation. ^[extracted]

## The Framework

### Strategy: From AI-Enhanced to AI-First

The first critical shift is about how to determine what to build. In an AI-enhanced approach, the question is "where can we add intelligence to an existing experience?" In an AI-first approach, the question becomes "what new experiences can we deliver that weren't even possible before?" ^[extracted]

### Ways of Working: From Deterministic Roadmaps to Ambiguity-Embracing Discovery

Key operational shifts include:
- Moving from deterministic roadmaps to ambiguity-embracing planning where "the destination itself can evolve" ^[extracted]
- [[concepts/ritualized-discovery|Ritualized discovery]] — treating experimentation as a repeatable, deliberate process ^[extracted]
- [[concepts/process-as-product|Process as product]] — evaluating processes against outcomes ^[extracted]
- [[concepts/mvp-for-learning|MVPs for learning]] — building to validate direction, not just to ship ^[extracted]
- [[concepts/smart-velocity|Smart velocity]] — moving fast with purpose, not speed for its own sake ^[extracted]

### People: From Specialists to Pathfinders

"Becoming an AI-first company isn't just tech transformation — it's mainly a culture transformation." ^[extracted] Key people shifts:
- [[concepts/t-shaped-ai-talent|T-shaped talent]] — deep expertise plus wide versatility ^[extracted]
- [[concepts/ai-fluency|AI fluency]] across the entire organization, not just the AI team ^[extracted]
- **Pathfinders** — people who can navigate ambiguity and forge paths where none exist ^[extracted]

## The Central Tension

"You basically need the discipline of an enterprise and the curiosity and nimbleness of a startup — but you need both to be running in parallel." ^[extracted] The talk frames this as "steering a ship and launching a rocket at the same time." ^[extracted]

## What Doesn't Change

- AI features must solve real customer problems — rooted in needs, not novelty ^[extracted]
- User experience, performance, reliability, and trust remain non-negotiable ^[extracted]
- Human creativity, judgment, and care remain central to leadership ^[extracted]

## Key Quotes

> "You can't go AI first if the rest of the company is still AI last." ^[extracted]

> "If you move fast without clarity of direction, you end up with chaos. And if you have clarity of direction with no momentum, you stagnate." ^[extracted]

> "The most transformative inventions in human history only became truly revolutionary when they became part of our day-to-day life." ^[extracted]

## Case Illustration: WooFull

The talk uses a fictional health-tracking app for puppies ("WooFull") to illustrate the difference between AI-enhanced features and AI-first experiences. In an AI-enhanced world, a user saying "I'm feeling kind of blah" gets a narrow response from a single feature team (meal tracking or digestive insights). In an AI-first world, the system unifies across workflows to produce a holistic answer. ^[extracted]
