# Larry Tesler

HCI pioneer and computer scientist who made foundational contributions to interactive computing. He invented the copy-paste paradigm and was a vocal advocate for modeless interfaces, famously arguing "don't mode me in." ^[extracted]

## Influence on AI UX Critique

[[entities/maximillian-piras|Maximillian Piras]] invokes Tesler's anti-mode principle to argue that the model picker dropdown in AI applications functions as a mode selector — changing how identical inputs produce different outputs. Piras frames the [[concepts/model-picker-as-mode|model picker as a violation of Tesler's principle]], arguing that users should not need to understand model-specific capability profiles. ^[extracted]
