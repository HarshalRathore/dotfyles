# Nathan Wan

Nathan Wan is the Head of AI at [[entities/ensemble-health|Ensemble Health Partners]], a company that manages the revenue cycle for hundreds of hospitals and health systems in the U.S. He leads a team of approximately 14,000 people and provides end-to-end revenue cycle management (RCM) services. ^[extracted]

## Career

Wan started his career in tech at [[entities/google|Google]], where he built software for operational teams and worked on speech recognition and language modeling over 10 years ago. One notable project was an early attempt at ambient clinical documentation — using AI to reduce the administrative burden on doctors who spend hours after patient visits writing and documenting notes. Though that early effort was not successful, the concept has since become commercially viable through multiple companies. ^[extracted]

He then moved to biotech startups, first in diagnostics (building ML models to detect cancer from blood biomarkers) and then in therapeutics (analyzing complex microbiome communities for drug discovery compounds). These experiences gave him deep expertise in applying ML to healthcare problems, though he notes these are among the "toughest and hardest to solve" areas. ^[extracted]

## AI in Healthcare Revenue Cycle

At AI Engineer World's Fair 2025, Wan presented on AI's opportunity in healthcare's financial side — specifically the revenue cycle management space. His core thesis: while most AI-in-healthcare attention goes to diagnostics, imaging, and drug discovery, the administrative/financial side of healthcare (billing, insurance, claims) is equally impactful and "ripe for AI disruption." He estimates that 20% of U.S. GDP is attributed to the healthcare system, with a large proportion going to administration. ^[inferred]

He argues that most claim denials are not medical disagreements but technical errors in registration or missing data — errors that AI could prevent at the source if organizations have longitudinal visibility across the entire revenue cycle. ^[extracted]
