# Claude 3.7 Sonnet

**Claude 3.7 Sonnet** is Anthropic's first reasoning model, released in February 2025. Simon Willison described it as a "great model" and noted its creative approach to the pelican-on-bicycle SVG benchmark.

## First Reasoning Model from Anthropic

Claude 3.7 Sonnet marked Anthropic's entry into the reasoning model space, joining DeepSeek R1 (January 2025) and OpenAI's O1 series as part of the industry's shift toward models that "think" before answering. ^[extracted]

## Pelican-on-Bicycle Response

Willison highlighted Claude 3.7 Sonnet's SVG output as particularly notable for its creative problem-solving: ^[extracted]

> "Pelicans can't ride bicycles. And Claude was like, well, what about if you put a bicycle on top of a bicycle? And it kind of works."

This response demonstrated both the model's reasoning capability (understanding the physical impossibility) and its creativity (proposing a novel solution that partially satisfies the prompt). Willison described the output as "kind of works" — a positive assessment for the benchmark.

## Significance

Claude 3.7 Sonnet's release was significant because: ^[inferred]

1. **Anthropic enters reasoning** — After DeepSeek R1 and OpenAI O1, Anthropic released their own reasoning model, intensifying the reasoning model arms race
2. **Creative problem-solving** — The model's ability to propose novel solutions (bicycle on bicycle) demonstrated capabilities beyond simple pattern matching
3. **February 2025 timing** — Released in the same month as GPT-4.5 and O1 Pro, creating a dense competitive landscape
