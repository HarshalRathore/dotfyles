# Jitendra Malik

**Jitendra Malik** is a pioneering computer vision professor at **UC Berkeley** and one of the original figures to define the concept of **world models**. He was referenced during a panel at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2026 as the "OG computer vision professor" whose foundational work on world models remains influential in the AI community. ^[extracted]

## World Models Definition

Malik's definition of world models dates back to at least 2019 (and conceptually to the 1990s), where he described world models as fundamentally model-based representations — essentially the model in model-based reinforcement learning. During the WF2026 panel, a Google researcher noted that Malik "has a lot of work to say about world models" and encouraged the audience to check out his definition, positioning it as the canonical reference in the field. ^[extracted]

The discussion arose in the context of whether "world model" has become too broadly applied — "everything is a world model now, it's not that useful" — with Malik's definition offered as the precise, original framing that cuts through the ambiguity. His definition ties world models to model-based RL: a structured representation of how the world works that an agent can use for planning and simulation. ^[extracted]

## Research Legacy

Malik's work spans computer vision, computational neuroscience, and AI. He is known for foundational contributions to object recognition, image segmentation, and the connection between vision and physical reasoning. His definition of world models was cited in the WF2026 panel discussion about whether language is the right intermediate representation for AI systems, and whether video models should be thought of as "complementary foundation models" that provide space-time understanding alongside text-based reasoning. ^[extracted]
