# Clay Bavor

Clay Bavor is the cofounder of [[entities/sierra-ai|Sierra]], an AI platform for building customer-facing AI agents. He previously spent 18 years at Google as a VP, where he led the AR/VR division (including Google Lens) and was instrumental in Google's hardware and immersive computing efforts. ^[extracted]

## Role at Sierra

As cofounder, Bavor articulates the vision that "every company in the future is going to have an agent, its own branded customer-facing agent" — describing it as "what comes after the website, what comes after the mobile app." ^[extracted]

## Key Contributions

- **Agent Development Life Cycle (ADLC)** — Bavor's team invented a new software development lifecycle for probabilistic AI, replacing traditional deterministic testing with [[concepts/user-simulation-testing|user simulation testing harnesses]], monitoring, and [[concepts/closed-loop-agent-improvement|closed-loop improvement]]. ^[extracted]
- **The AI Architect Role** — Observed emerging organically across Sierra's customers: a role combining technology understanding, brand/experience design, and business outcomes. ^[extracted]
- **The Agent Iceberg** — The metaphor for hidden complexity: the visible tip (model choice, framework, embeddings) versus the vast underwater mass (regression testing, model migration, voice handling, speaker separation). ^[extracted]
- **First Derivative Principle** — Argues that "understanding where things are going is even more important than understanding where things are today" and advocates tracking model capability trajectories. ^[extracted]
- **Shapeshifter Interface Vision** — Predicts future agents will "summon text, voice, video, imagery, user interface, and more" and that wearables/glasses will be the ultimate vehicle for trusted personal AI. ^[extracted]
