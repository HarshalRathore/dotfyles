# George Cameron

**George Cameron** is the co-founder of [[entities/artificial-analysis|Artificial Analysis]], an independent AI benchmarking company. He presented "Trends Across the AI Frontier" at AIEF2025, bringing quantitative analysis to the trade-offs between intelligence, cost, speed, and openness in modern AI models.

## AIEF2025 Talk: Trends Across the AI Frontier

George's talk at AIEF2025 examined four distinct frontiers in AI beyond raw intelligence:

1. **Reasoning models** — the intelligence-versus-verbosity trade-off, with models like O4 Mini High using 72× more output tokens than GPT 4.1
2. **Open weights frontier** — the closing gap between open-weight and proprietary intelligence, particularly DeepSeek R1's proximity to leading models
3. **Cost frontier** — order-of-magnitude cost differences, with O3 costing ~$2,000 vs GPT 4.1 Nano at 500× cheaper
4. **Speed frontier** — how latency impacts what applications can be built, with reasoning models introducing 10× latency multipliers

His key message: you should not always use the leading most intelligent model, and the trade-offs have real, measurable implications for application design. ^[extracted]
