Mark Myshatyn is the Enterprise AI Architect at [[entities/los-alamos-national-laboratory|Los Alamos National Laboratory]] (LANL). He presented "Government Agents: AI Agents Meet Tough Regulations" at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025, covering how a 70-year applied AI/ML laboratory approaches agentic AI under strict regulatory constraints.

Myshatyn oversees LANL's AI strategy across their 40-square-mile campus with 13 nuclear facilities and ~20,000 researchers. His office covers both mission AI (pushing the science of AI faster, writing their own models) and operational AI (payroll, procurement, cybersecurity workflows). He emphasizes LANL's dual approach of building internally while partnering with frontier labs and academia, including a partnership with the UC family of schools and collaborations with OpenAI on chem/bio safety work.

He is a vocal advocate for bringing AI deeper into government mission work rather than limiting it to productivity tools like PowerPoint assistants or email summarizers, citing the real-world kinetic and geopolitical consequences of AI failures in the national security domain.
