# Dan Shipper

Dan Shipper is the founder and operator of [[entities/every-company|Every]], a capital-efficient AI-native company that runs four software products with just 15 people. He spoke at AIEF2025 on building an AI-native company and introduced the concept of "compounding engineering."

## Background

Shipper runs Every, which has:

- Six business units
- Four software products (Quora, Monologue, Spiral, and others)
- 7,000+ paying subscribers and 100,000+ free subscribers
- Double-digit MRR growth monthly for six consecutive months
- Only ~$1M in total funding

## Key Contributions

### Compounding Engineering

Shipper coined the term "compounding engineering" to describe an engineering methodology where each feature makes the next feature easier to build, as opposed to traditional engineering where each feature adds complexity. He articulated a four-step loop: plan, delegate, assess, codify.

### AI-Native Company Thesis

Shipper argues that the difference between 90% and 100% AI adoption in an engineering org is a 10x difference — even 10% traditional engineering (typing into a code editor) forces the whole organization to lean back into slower, traditional methods.

## Philosophy

- Code is cheap enough to prototype risky ideas
- Demo culture enables weirder, more creative work
- The programming level of the stack is moving from languages up to English
- Tacit knowledge should be codified into explicit, shareable prompts and tools
