# Jeremy Silva

Jeremy Silva is the product lead at [[entities/freeplay|Freeplay]], a company that helps teams build AI products. He comes from a "data science machine learning background" and has been "building language models since the dark ages, pre-GPT3." ^[extracted]

## AI Engineer World's Fair 2025

Jeremy has been a prominent voice at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 with two talks:

### Build Dynamic Products, and Stop the AI Sideshow

Co-presented with [[entities/eliza-cabrera|Eliza Cabrera]] ([[entities/workday|Workday]]). This talk introduced:
- The [[concepts/ai-sideshow]] anti-pattern — centralized AI strategy running as a sidecar to core product strategy ^[extracted]
- The [[concepts/dynamic-products|crawl-walk-run framework]] — progressive maturity model for AI integration ^[extracted]
- The three causes of sideshows: risk quarantining, technology-first approach, top-down solution pushing ^[extracted]
- The cure: integrate AI risk into planning, start with customer problems, enable bottoms-up discovery ^[extracted]

### The Build-Operate Divide

Co-presented with [[entities/chris-hernandez|Chris Hernandez]] of [[entities/chime|Chime]], which introduced the [[concepts/build-operate-divide]] framework and related operational concepts. ^[extracted] Freeplay's experience "working across dozens of different enterprise teams" informed the observations.

## Key Contributions

- Coined the [[concepts/ai-sideshow]] framing — a practical diagnostic for when AI strategy has gone off track ^[extracted]
- Developed (with Cabrera) the crawl-walk-run AI integration maturity model ^[extracted]
- Articulated why "AI products that need not announce themselves as AI" are the hallmark of successful integration ^[extracted]
