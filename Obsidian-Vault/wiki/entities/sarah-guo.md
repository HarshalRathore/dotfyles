# Sarah Guo

Sarah Guo is the founder of Conviction, an AI-native venture fund. She previously spent about 10 years as an investor at Greylock, where her partner Mike Vernal previously served at Sequoia. She started Conviction roughly two and a half years before the public launch of ChatGPT, making her an early AI-focused investor.

## Investment Focus

Guo invests across the AI stack, with a thesis that "this time is different" — that AI represents the largest technology revolution of our era, creating space for a startup VC alongside new startup opportunities. ^[extracted]

### Portfolio Companies

- [[entities/cursor-ai|Cursor]] — AI code editor, $1M to $100M ARR in 12 months
- Cognition — AI dev tools (Devin)
- [[entities/mistral|Mistral]] — Open-weight model provider
- Thinking Machines — AI model lab
- [[entities/harvey-ai|Harvey]] — Legal AI platform, $70M+ ARR
- Open — AI platform (mentioned)

## Key Observations

Guo notes she has never seen uptake from users like the last 2-3 years of AI, and that companies are going from zero to $50-100M run rate faster than any previous technology revolution. She advises operators to "pay very little attention to what the marketing world is saying" and focus on whether products work long-term. ^[extracted]
