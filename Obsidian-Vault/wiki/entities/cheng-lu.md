# Cheng Lu

Cheng Lu is an engineer at [[entities/midjourney|MidJourney]] known for articulating a key distinction in AI prediction and evaluation. He gave talks that Diego Rodriguez (Krea.ai) describes as "great" and worth watching.

## The Cars vs Traffic Distinction

Cheng Lu's most cited insight is the distinction between predicting components versus predicting emergent behavior: "Predicting the car back when everything was horses, it's not that hard... we have a thing that goes like this, we have horses that make energy, so you swap the thing for the engine, that's essentially a car." The hard prediction is not the car — it's the traffic. ^[extracted]

This insight reframes what AI researchers and engineers should be focusing on: not the predictable components of AI systems (models, architectures, APIs), but the emergent, unpredictable behaviors (user interactions, system dynamics, perception-level quality). ^[inferred]
