# Shafik Quoraishee

Game developer at [[entities/nyt-games|NYT Games]], part of the [[entities/new-york-times|New York Times]] games team. Works on popular NYT games including [[concepts/connections-game|Connections]]. ^[extracted]

## Background

Prior to game development, Quoraishee worked in media for most of his career. His technical background spans three domains: ^[extracted]

- **Machine learning** — foundational for his Connections solver work
- **Mobile development** — relevant to NYT's mobile game apps
- **Data science** — informs his analytical approach to game design and AI benchmarking

## AIEF2025 Talk

Presented "New York Times' Connections: A Case Study on NLP in Word Games" at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025, where he: ^[extracted]

- Framed Connections as a reproducible benchmark for testing LLM capabilities
- Modeled the game as an augmented graph coloring problem
- Built a custom Connections solver using semantic similarity edges
- Drew parallels between human System 1/System 2 thinking and AI reasoning

His work is independent research and experimentation, not based on NYT's internal research. ^[extracted]

## Key Quotes

> "The purpose of this work is investigatory, not authoritative. So it's mostly me trying to kind of look into the realm of AI problem solving in the game space through our games, and it's mostly like a fun exercise." ^[extracted]
