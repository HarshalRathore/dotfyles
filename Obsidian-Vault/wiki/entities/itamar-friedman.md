# Itamar Friedman

Itamar Friedman is the CEO and co-founder of [[entities/qodo|Qodo]], a company focused on AI-powered code review and testing. He presented "Vibe Coding with Confidence" at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 (AIEF2025).

## AIEF2025 Talk: Vibe Coding with Confidence

At AIEF2025, Friedman presented a thesis on the evolution of AI developer tools across three generations: Gen 1.0 (autocomplete), Gen 2.0 (chat and agentic chat), and Gen 3.0 (CLI-based end-to-end workflows). He argued that the CLI — not IDE plugins or chat interfaces — will become the new interface leader for AI-assisted software development, enabling reliable, end-to-end agentic workflows across the entire SDLC. ^[extracted]

His core argument is that "vibe coding" has captured the imagination of developers but lacks the confidence needed for enterprise use. The next game-changing shift, he argues, is moving from chat-based AI to CLI-based agents that can execute end-to-end workflows — giving developers the speed of vibe coding with the confidence of structured, tested, reviewed output. ^[extracted]

Friedman also introduced Qodo's framing of AI coding tools as either "blue team" (code generation) or "red team" (reviewing, testing, quality assurance). Qodo focuses on the red team — acting as a tech lead perspective on code quality — while acknowledging that the industry has been heavily focused on the blue team. ^[extracted]

During the talk, Friedman announced the soft launch of a Qodo CLI tool, inviting the audience to test it and provide feedback. ^[extracted]

## Key Positions

- **CLI as the future interface** for AI coding tools, enabling cross-SDLC workflows and agent-to-agent coordination
- **Enterprise vs. noob distinction**: what "end-to-end" means differs radically between casual developers (prompt → software) and enterprise teams (reliable, versatile workflows across the SDLC)
- **Holistic AI across the SDLC**: like Wiz's approach to cloud security, the next breakthrough will be a unified solution spanning planning, coding, testing, and review — not point tools
- **Shift-left AI**: moving review and testing left in the development timeline, so developers get feedback as they write code rather than after pushing
