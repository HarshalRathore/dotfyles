# James Lowe

Head of AI Engineering at the **Incubator for AI** (i.AI), a small team of experts within the UK government created by 10 Downing Street to deliver public good using AI through experimentation and product building. ^[extracted]

## Background

James Lowe speaks from the perspective of a technical leader operating at the intersection of AI engineering and product management within a government context. The UK government delivers for over 70 million citizens and spends over a trillion pounds, making the scope of i.AI's work exceptionally wide — from frontline services up to the Prime Minister's meetings. ^[extracted]

## Key Contributions

Lowe delivered the talk "Why your product needs an AI product manager, and why it should be you" at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025, arguing that AI expertise is essential for product management roles and that the AI product manager mindset should be adopted by product managers, AI engineers, and founders alike. ^[extracted]

He shared two concrete case studies from i.AI:

- **Consult / Theme Finder** — a government consultation analysis project where prioritizing AI capability evaluation early led to a product 1,000× faster and 400× cheaper than human analysis, with the evaluation process revealing where human-in-the-loop input was most valuable. ^[extracted]
- **Minute** — an AI transcription tool for government meetings where they explored features broadly using AI coding assistance, then stripped back to focus on what actually worked with users. ^[extracted]

## Philosophy

Lowe endorses the view (citing Andrew Ng) that AI coding and AI features make prototyping cheaper and the previously impossible possible, increasing demand for people who can decide what to build. He also cites Brett Taylor's view that there is "a lot of power in combining product and engineering into as few people as possible" and that "few great things have been created by committee." ^[inferred]
