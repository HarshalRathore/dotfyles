# Mark Bissell

Mark Bissell is a Member of Technical Staff at [[entities/goodfire-ai|Goodfire AI]], working on bringing [[concepts/mechanistic-interpretability|mechanistic interpretability]] from research labs into practical engineering use cases. He presented "Why You Should Care About AI Interpretability" at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025. ^[extracted]

## Background

Before joining Goodfire, Bissell spent three years on Palantir's healthcare team, where he experienced first-hand the reliability requirements for mission-critical AI systems and the challenges of deploying non-deterministic LLMs to production. This background informs his perspective on why interpretability matters for AI engineers. ^[extracted]

## Key Ideas

- Frames the reliability gap in AI development — "whack-a-mole prompt edits," unscalable LLM-as-judge approaches, and unpredictable fine-tuning — as the core problem that [[concepts/neural-programming|neural programming]] solves
- Demonstrates [[concepts/feature-steering|feature steering]] (adjusting internal features by 60% to enforce PII confidentiality) and dynamic prompting (injecting prompts based on feature activation detection)
- Argues that interpretability is the most important and interesting problem in AI — "the hallmark of an engineer is that we like to understand how systems work"
