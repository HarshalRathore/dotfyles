# Sharif Shameem

Founder of [[entities/lexica|Lexica]], a company working on generative image models. A prolific demo builder since the early days of GPT-3 (2020), his work helped establish the practice of using interactive demos to explore latent AI model capabilities. His GPT-3 browser-based JSX compiler demo was an early precursor to what is now called "vibe coding."

## Key Contributions

- **Early GPT-3 demos (2020).** Built one of the first interactive natural-language-to-code interfaces by hooking GPT-3 to a browser-based JSX compiler. ^[extracted]
- **Custom HTML parser for model browsing (2021).** Wrote an HTML parser to compress web pages into GPT-3's 4,000-token context window, enabling the model to browse and interact with websites — an early exploration of model agency. ^[extracted]
- **Multivac (2021).** A visual interface for decomposing large problems into context-window-sized sub-problems, an early form of structured reasoning assistance with LLMs. ^[extracted]
- **Articulated AI engineering as excavation.** Distinguished AI engineering from traditional goal-oriented engineering as a discovery process for latent model capabilities. ^[extracted]

## Key Ideas

- Curiosity is the subconscious pattern recognition that pulls ideas from the future into the present. ^[inferred]
- Demos are the primary toolkit for exploring what AI models can do. ^[extracted]
- AI engineers have a moral obligation to follow their curiosity and share what they discover, honoring computing pioneers like J.C.R. Licklider. ^[extracted]
