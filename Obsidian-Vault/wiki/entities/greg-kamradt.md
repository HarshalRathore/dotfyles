# Greg Kamradt

Greg Kamradt is the president of the [[entities/arc-prize-foundation|ARC Prize Foundation]], a non-profit organization founded by [[entities/francois-chollet|Francois Chollet]] and Mike Knup whose mission is to serve as a "North Star guide towards Open AGI." ^[extracted]

## Role at ARC Prize Foundation

Kamradt leads the foundation's efforts to build benchmarks for measuring artificial general intelligence. In December 2024, he was invited by [[entities/openai|OpenAI]] to join their live stream to co-announce the O3 preview results on ARC-AGI. ^[extracted]

## Key Contributions

- **ARC-AGI-3 announcement** — Presented ARC-AGI-3, the first interactive reasoning benchmark from the ARC series, at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025. ^[extracted]
- **Human baseline data collection** — Led in-person testing of over 400 humans in San Diego to validate ARC-AGI tasks. ^[extracted]
- **Advocacy for interactive benchmarks** — Argues that measuring human-like intelligence requires interactive, exploration-based evaluation, not single-turn tasks. ^[extracted]

## Views on Intelligence

Kamradt advocates for using humans as the target for AI benchmarking, citing John McCarthy's definition of AI (making machines do novel tasks) and Chollet's definition of intelligence as skill acquisition efficiency. He argues that energy consumption would be the ideal denominator for measuring intelligence, with action count and cost as practical proxies. ^[extracted]
