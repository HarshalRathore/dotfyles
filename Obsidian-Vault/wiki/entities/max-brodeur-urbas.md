## Max Brodeur-Urbas

Max Brodeur-Urbas is the founder of [[entities/gumloop|Gumloop]], a workflow automation tool that has been adopted by large enterprise customers. He was part of the Y Combinator Winter 2024 batch and raised a Series A round when his team consisted of only two people.

Before Gumloop, Max built a series of software projects including video game moderation software, ML models for detecting children's age in video games for VR age separation, and bot detection systems. His side project on top of his side project became Gumloop: a UI for AutoGPT (initially called Agent Hub, which he envisioned as "GitHub for agents") that he built after noticing people in the AutoGPT Discord were excited about AI but didn't know how to clone repos or set things up locally.

The key pivot insight came when he noticed that people describing what they wanted AI agents to do were actually describing complex workflows — if they knew Python, API calls, and LLM queries, they could automate their entire request without hoping an agent would figure it out. This led to Gumloop's core product: configuring agents as a series of configurable nodes in a workflow, rather than describing desired outcomes in natural language.

Max and his co-founder intentionally kept the team small (under 10 people) after observing that teams at Amazon and Microsoft were slower due to coordination overhead. They adopted practices including extremely selective hiring, [[concepts/product-led-hiring|product-led hiring]] from their customer base, [[concepts/work-trial-interviews|work-trial interviews]], minimal meetings, deep focus time, and [[concepts/internal-automation|internal automation]].
