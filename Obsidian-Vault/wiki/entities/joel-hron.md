# Joel Hron

**Joel Hron** is CTO of [[entities/thomson-reuters|Thomson Reuters]]. He spoke at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 on building trustworthy agents for high-stakes domains.

## Background at Thomson Reuters

Hron led Thomson Reuters' journey of building AI assistance systems over 2.5 years, overseeing the shift from helpful assistants to productive agents that make judgments and decisions on behalf of users in legal, tax, compliance, and risk domains. ^[extracted]

## Key Views

Hron articulated several distinctive perspectives on enterprise AI:

- **Agentic AI as dials, not binary** — autonomy, context, memory, and coordination are levers to tune per use case, not on/off switches. ^[extracted]
- **Evals are the hardest thing they do** — even domain experts show 10%+ swings in accuracy when evaluating the same inputs a week apart, making deterministic trust nearly impossible. ^[extracted]
- **Legacy applications as assets** — 100+ years of domain logic in legacy systems are not baggage but unique assets when decomposed as agent tools. ^[extracted]
- **MVPs can be over-indulgent** — the MVP mindset sometimes leads teams to over-indulge in features before establishing trust foundations. ^[extracted]

## AIEF2025 Talk

"From Copilot to Colleague: Trustworthy Agents for High-Stakes" — described Thomson Reuters' evolution from building helpful AI assistance to productive agents that produce output and judgments in environments where the risks of being wrong are not acceptable.
