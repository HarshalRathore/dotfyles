# Brett Victor

HCI researcher and designer known for pushing the boundaries of interactive computing. His principle — "creators need immediate feedback with what they're creating" — directly inspired [[entities/sharif-shameem|Sharif Shameem]]'s early GPT-3 demos where a JSX compiler was placed in the browser for real-time AI-assisted coding. ^[extracted]

## Influence on AI Demos

Shameem cites watching a talk by Brett Victor before building his first GPT-3 code generation demo. The insight that creators need immediate feedback led him to put the JSX compiler in the browser rather than requiring a separate compilation step — creating one of the first interactive natural-language-to-code interfaces. ^[extracted]

## Future of Programming Talk — Goals and Constraints

In his "Future of Programming" talk, Victor argues that programmers should shift from step-by-step procedural thinking to thinking in terms of goals and constraints — guiding programs with higher layers of abstraction rather than hand-coding every instruction. He illustrates this with the historical shift from binary to assembly, where programmers had to trust abstraction layers despite losing direct machine control. ^[extracted]

[[entities/maximillian-piras|Maximillian Piras]] at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 applies this framing to AI UX design: as interfaces become stochastic and dynamic, designers cannot procedurally specify every possible path. They must instead set constraints (via design systems as RL boundaries) and goals (via user stories translated into system prompts), moving from construction to [[concepts/gardening-as-design-paradigm|gardening]]. ^[extracted]
