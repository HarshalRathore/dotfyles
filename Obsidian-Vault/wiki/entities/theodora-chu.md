# Theodora Chu

Theodora Chu is a Product Manager at Anthropic working on the [[concepts/mcp|Model Context Protocol]]. Prior to Anthropic, she was a startup founder in the AI space.

## Role at Anthropic

Theo leads product for MCP, the open-source protocol that enables language models to interact with external tools and data sources. She was a key spokesperson for MCP at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025, where she covered the protocol's origin story, design principles, and roadmap. ^[extracted]

## Background

Theo came to MCP from a startup founder background in the AI space, giving her a builder's perspective on the protocol's design. She is known for being pragmatic and community-oriented, frequently encouraging developers to contribute to the MCP spec and submit PRs directly to the GitHub repository. ^[extracted]

## Personal Interests

Outside of work, Theo enjoys playing poker (and admits she mostly loses money at it) and is a coffee enthusiast, particularly interested in San Francisco's coffee scene. ^[extracted]

## AIEF2025 Talk: MCP: Origins and Requests For Startups

At AIEF2025, Theodora gave a comprehensive talk covering:
- The origin story of MCP (David and Justin's two-engineers-in-a-garage moment)
- Design principles (server simplicity, agent-first architecture)
- Project updates (remote MCPs, auth fixes, Streamable HTTP, elicitation)
- The adoption timeline (Cursor → IDEs → Model providers)
- Future roadmap (elicitation, registry API)
