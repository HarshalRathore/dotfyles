## Background

**Chelcie Taylor** is a venture investor at [[entities/notable-vc|Notable]]. She presented at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 alongside [[entities/dani-grant|Dani Grant]] of [[entities/jam-vc|Jam]] on the AI engineer's guide to raising VC capital. ^[extracted]

## Key Views on Fundraising

Chelcie emphasizes that you do not need revenue or even a product to raise VC capital at the pre-seed and seed stages. ^[extracted] She stresses that early-stage investors are banking on the founder, the team they can build, and their vision for the market. ^[extracted]

She shares that when her fund raised, they were pre-product and her co-founder had not yet left his day job — making everything about potential rather than demonstrated traction. ^[extracted] She argues it is often easier to raise when everything is potential because building a "wow" product first can delay fundraising unnecessarily. ^[extracted]

Chelcie also weighs in on the full-time question: leaving your job to raise VC is firm-dependent, but it serves as a strong signal of conviction to investors who are looking for founders with deep commitment. ^[extracted]

## On Cold Outreach

Chelcie shared examples of cold emails she has received during the talk, including one referencing a Notable blog post that motivated the founder's project. ^[extracted] She notes that cold emails referencing specific content the investor has produced tend to perform better than generic outreach. ^[inferred]
