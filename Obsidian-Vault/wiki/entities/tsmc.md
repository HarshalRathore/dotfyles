# TSMC

**TSMC** (Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company) is the world's leading semiconductor foundry, manufacturing chips for virtually every major tech company.

## Role in AI Chip Supply

TSMC manufactures the advanced chips used in [[entities/nvidia|NVIDIA]]'s GPUs and, through intermediary companies, [[entities/huawei|Huawei]]'s Ascend processors. ^[extracted]

### Huawei Channel

Despite US pressure, TSMC continued manufacturing chips for Huawei through a Chinese cryptocurrency mining company called Sofgo (which pretended to be unaffiliated with Huawei). Approximately 2.9 million chips worth were purchased through this channel before TSMC was forced to stop. ^[extracted]

- The US fined TSMC approximately $1 billion for generating ~$500M in revenue from sales to Huawei through intermediaries — described as a relatively lenient penalty
- TSMC currently has capacity for ~50,000 wafers per month at 7nm

## Geopolitical Significance

TSMC's position makes it a critical node in the [[concepts/china-us-ai-rivalry|China-US AI rivalry]]. The US government has attempted to restrict TSMC's ability to serve Chinese customers, but the effectiveness of these restrictions has been limited by: ^[inferred]

1. The difficulty of verifying end-user identity through shell companies
2. The economic incentive for TSMC to maximize revenue
3. Taiwan's complex geopolitical position
