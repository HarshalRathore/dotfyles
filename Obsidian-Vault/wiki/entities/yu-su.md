# Yu Su

Yu Su is an AI researcher and professor working on natural language processing, agents, and human-AI interaction.

## Current Roles

- **Co-founder** of NeoCognition
- **Professor** affiliated with Ohio State University (NLP research)
- **Sloan Fellow**

## Research Contributions

- **Mind2Web 2** — Co-authored the paper introducing the Agent-as-a-Judge framework for evaluating long-horizon agentic search tasks. The benchmark evaluates 130 realistic web tasks with tree-structured rubrics.
- **Mind2Web** (original) — Earlier benchmark for cross-domain task-oriented web agents
- **SkillWeaver** — Work on agents that self-improve by creating and refining skills
- **Agent-as-a-Judge: Evaluate Agents with Agents** — Research group work on agentic evaluation (Zhuge et al., Meta, Oct 2024); Yu Su's group built on this with Mind2Web 2's formalized framework

## Recent Activity (Jul 2026)

On Jul 5, shared the Mind2Web 2 paper in response to Zhu Liang's (@paradite_) viral question about "agent as judge" for AI evaluations, noting it as a paper ahead of its time. The thread gained significant traction: Zhu Liang's opening tweet received 245 likes and 75K views; Yu Su's reply garnered 44 likes and 77 bookmarks. ^[extracted] Also shared the SkillWeaver paper on self-improving agents in the same conversation.

## Research Collaborations

- **Mingchen Zhuge** — Co-author on the original Agent-as-a-Judge paper (Oct 2024); represents the precursor to Mind2Web 2's Agent-as-a-Judge framework
- **Zhu Liang** (@paradite_) — OSU researcher whose Jul 2026 tweet about "agent as judge" for evals catalyzed a viral Twitter thread where Yu Su shared the Mind2Web 2 and SkillWeaver papers
