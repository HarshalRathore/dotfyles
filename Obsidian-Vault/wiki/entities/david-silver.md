# David Silver

**David Silver** is a Principal Research Scientist at **Google DeepMind** and a pioneer of reinforcement learning. He was the lead researcher on **AlphaGo**, the first program to defeat a world champion Go player, and co-inventor of **AlphaZero**. At the AI Engineer World's Fair 2026, he was described as the archetypal "RO maximalist" — a reference to the view that chain-of-thought reasoning is just additional compute, not fundamentally special. ^[extracted]

## RO Maximalist

During a WF2026 panel discussing whether language is the right representation for reasoning, a speaker noted: "There's a kind of a few discussion, you know, some RO maximalists is like, oh, we don't care about, you know, kind of channel of thought — it's just additional compute." When asked who qualifies in that description, the answer was "David Silver. Ah, okay. Yeah. I mean, they've just left to start their thing." This references Silver's departure from DeepMind to start a new venture (reportedly **Co笔者认为**) that continues his reinforcement learning research agenda. ^[extracted]

The "RO maximalist" philosophy holds that the specific format of reasoning traces (natural language, tokens, continuous vectors) is incidental — what matters is the additional computation they enable. This is in contrast to the view that language is a uniquely effective representation because it ties directly into pretrained intelligence. Silver's position is that chain-of-thought is effective primarily because it provides adaptive compute, not because language itself has special properties. ^[extracted]

## AlphaGo and Reinforcement Learning

Silver led the development of AlphaGo (2016), which defeated Lee Sedol, and later AlphaZero, which mastered Go, chess, and shogi through self-play without human knowledge. His work established the paradigm of RL with self-play as a path to superhuman performance in domains with clear reward signals. At WF2026, this legacy was discussed in the context of whether the same RL-with-verification approach can extend beyond games to coding and knowledge work. ^[inferred]
