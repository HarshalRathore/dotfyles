# Manu Goyal

Manu Goyal is a software engineer at [[entities/braintrust|Braintrust]], where he works on building platforms for AI evaluation and related tooling. ^[extracted]

## Background

Manu's career path was shaped by an early conviction that technology should not be shackled to rule-based systems but should instead "come alive, grow and adapt, and really be a thought partner to mankind." ^[extracted] This philosophy led him from early interest in video games to leetcode preparation, and ultimately to a role in the self-driving car industry. ^[extracted]

In self-driving cars, he learned a critical lesson: model tuning alone (architecture changes, loss function adjustments) is never sufficient to ship to production. The gap between model performance metrics and real-world behavior requires evaluation infrastructure — the insight that now drives his work at Braintrust. ^[extracted]

## AIEF2025 Talk

Manu presented "Why should anyone care about Evals?" at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025, arguing that evals are the foundational practice for building production AI systems. His core thesis: evals are not just unit tests but a laboratory for experiments that lets teams complete 90% of the iteration loop before going to production. ^[extracted]
