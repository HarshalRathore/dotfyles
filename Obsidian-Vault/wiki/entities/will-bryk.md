# Will Bryk

Will Bryk is the founder of [[entities/exa|Exa.ai]], an AI-native web search engine built from the ground up for AI agents rather than human users. He spoke at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 on building a smarter AI agent with Neural RAG.

## Background

Bryk joined Y Combinator in Summer 2021 and raised a couple million dollars. The company spent four years researching and building a fundamentally new approach to search, applying transformer-based next-token-prediction technology to the problem of information retrieval. ^[extracted]

## Philosophy

Bryk's core insight is that traditional search engines were optimized for a fundamentally different creature — slow, flesh-and-blood humans typing simple keywords — while AI agents are a completely different class of user that can process vast amounts of information, use complex queries, and need dense knowledge retrieval rather than curated link lists. ^[extracted]

He frames the problem as a natural extension of the [[concepts/bitter-lesson|Bitter Lesson]]: rather than hard-coding search algorithms based on human behavior, use transformers and scaling to build a search engine that learns to understand queries at a deep semantic level. ^[inferred]
