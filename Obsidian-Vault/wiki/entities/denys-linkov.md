# Denys Linkov

Denys Linkov leads the machine learning team at [[entities/wisedocs|WiseDocs]], a verticalized solutions company focused on AI for document processing. He spoke at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 on structuring a modern AI team, covering the anatomy of AI companies, the value of generalists, and practical hiring trade-offs. ^[extracted]

## Background

Before leading AI at WiseDocs, Denys was hired as the first engineer at a conversational AI / AI agent building platform company with the mandate to build their first machine learning team from scratch. His initial job description was "we want ML" — which he then evolved to "we want AI" after aligning with business teams and leadership. ^[extracted]

## Key Contributions

### AI Team Structure Framework

At AIEF2025, Denys articulated a framework for understanding AI teams that starts with recognizing three types of companies: technology companies, verticalized/services companies, and tech-enabled companies — each with different challenges and different approaches to building AI capability. ^[extracted]

### The Generalist Approach

Denys advocates for hiring generalists rather than specialists when building early AI teams. His experience at the conversational AI company showed that a team of generalists, supported by automation and a custom MLOps platform, could serve hundreds of thousands of concurrent models across multiple domains with real-time training and serving. ^[extracted]

### The "Ampere's Wager" Thought Experiment

He proposed a philosophical wager: would you trade your entire team (with domain knowledge and domain expertise) for five AI researchers from top labs? This challenges the assumption that more AI research talent is always better — suggesting that domain knowledge combined with AI capability often beats pure research talent. ^[inferred]

## Core Beliefs

- **Technology is not the bottleneck**: He believes 90% of the technology needed to solve humanity's problems already exists; the limitation is adoption and usage, not capability. ^[extracted]
- **Generalists over specialists**: For most companies, a generalist ML engineer with domain knowledge delivers more value than a specialist AI researcher. ^[inferred]
- **AI researchers are not always needed**: Hiring AI researchers for model pre-training or fine-tuning is rarely the first step toward delivering AI value. ^[extracted]
