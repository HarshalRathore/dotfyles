# Conviction

Conviction is an AI-native venture capital firm founded by [[entities/sarah-guo|Sarah Guo]] (formerly Greylock) and Mike Vernal (formerly Sequoia). The firm was started roughly two and a half years before ChatGPT launched, on the thesis that AI represents a generational technology shift.

## Operating Model

Unlike traditional VC firms, Conviction takes a concentrated approach — backing a small number of companies while also running a large grant program. This dual approach gives them broad data exposure across the AI startup landscape. ^[extracted]

## The Embed Grant Program

Conviction operates a grant program called Embed specifically for AI startups. Key data points: ^[extracted]

- Receives thousands of applications annually
- Includes user data, revenue data from applicants
- Has seen a 50% year-over-year increase in agent startup applications
- Provides Conviction with a unique vantage point on the AI startup landscape

## Portfolio Companies (mentioned by Guo)

- [[entities/cursor-ai|Cursor]] — AI code editor
- Cognition — AI dev tools
- [[entities/mistral|Mistral]] — Model provider
- Thinking Machines — AI research lab
- [[entities/harvey-ai|Harvey]] — Legal AI

## Investment Thesis

Conviction believes AI is the largest technology revolution to date, with so much change in technology, business models, and product decisions that it creates opportunity for a new venture firm alongside new startups. ^[extracted]
