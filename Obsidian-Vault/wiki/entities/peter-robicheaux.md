Peter Robicheaux is the ML Lead at [[entities/roboflow|Roboflow]], a platform for building and deploying vision models. He spoke at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 on "Vision AI in 2025," presenting a critical analysis of the state of computer vision and the gap between human and machine visual perception.

His talk argued that vision models are fundamentally "not smart" compared to language models, citing the inability of even advanced multimodal models like GPT-4o to perform basic visual tasks like reading a clock. He introduced [[concepts/rf100vl|RF100VL]], a new benchmark designed to measure visual intelligence more rigorously than COCO, and announced [[concepts/rf-detter|RF-DeTter]], a detection transformer leveraging DINOv2 pre-trained backbones.

He also highlighted Roboflow's open-source strategy: making their platform freely available to researchers who then contribute their data back to the community, including medical and biological datasets cited in Nature publications.
