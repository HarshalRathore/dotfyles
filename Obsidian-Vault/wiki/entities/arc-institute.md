# ARC Institute

The ARC Institute is a research organization that trains foundational models for genomics. Their most recent launch is **EVO2**, a model that is superhuman at predicting genomic data across all types of organisms. ^[extracted]

## Collaboration with Goodfire AI

The ARC Institute is working with [[entities/goodfire-ai|Goodfire AI]] on a novel application of [[concepts/mechanistic-interpretability|mechanistic interpretability]]: extracting scientific knowledge from superhuman models. The goal is to identify, in an unsupervised way, what biological concepts EVO2 has learned that humans don't yet know — and extract that information so domain experts can advance their understanding. ^[extracted]

Bissell frames this as one of the most exciting use cases for interpretability: taking models that outperform humans in specific domains and using interpretability techniques to figure out *what principles they are using*, updating human scientific understanding in the process. ^[extracted]

## Disease Biomarker Research

Beyond the ARC Institute collaboration, Goodfire is also working with a major health system on other genomics-based models to identify novel biomarkers of disease — using interpretability to understand why a model predicts a patient's likelihood of rheumatoid arthritis or their likely response to specific treatments. ^[extracted]
