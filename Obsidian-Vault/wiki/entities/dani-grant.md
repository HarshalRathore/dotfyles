## Background

**Dani Grant** is a venture investor at [[entities/jam-vc|Jam]]. She presented at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 alongside [[entities/chelcie-taylor|Chelcie Taylor]] of [[entities/notable-vc|Notable]] on the AI engineer's guide to raising VC capital. ^[extracted]

## Key Views on Fundraising

Dani emphasizes that VCs are actively hunting for deals and reads cold emails as part of their job — framing themselves as salespeople selling capital to entrepreneurs. ^[extracted] She advocates for early, casual relationship-building with investors, including posting content on LinkedIn to generate inbound interest. ^[inferred]

She stresses that at pre-seed and seed stages, investors are backing the founder, the team they can recruit, and their vision — not a polished product. ^[extracted] She shares that her own fund raised pre-product, with her co-founder still employed at his day job, demonstrating that early-stage fundraising can happen on potential alone. ^[extracted]

Dani also champions the "ask for advice, get money" approach: reaching out to investors for advice early creates conversations that often become fundraising opportunities. ^[extracted]

## On Cold Outreach

Dani reads cold emails and responds to ones that are prompted by a warm signal — such as when she has recently posted content on LinkedIn and an entrepreneur reaches out with a relevant idea. ^[extracted] She advises founders to keep cold emails specific and tangible rather than vague, so the recipient knows exactly what to expect from the conversation. ^[extracted] The job of the email, she says, is not to make the sale but to compel a response. ^[extracted]
