# Craig Wattrus

Craig Wattrus is the Director of AI Transformation at [[entities/flatfile|Flatfile]], a data migration and import platform. At AI Engineer World's Fair 2025, he gave a talk on form factors for AI coworkers, articulating a design philosophy rooted in direct interaction with models ("feeling the material") and character coaching over output control.

## Key Contributions

- **AI coworker form factors** — identified four surfaces for AI interaction: invisible (background processing), ambient (subtle presence), in-line (workflow-integrated), and conversational (chat). ^[extracted]
- **Character coach approach** — inspired by [[entities/anthropic|Anthropic]]'s [[entities/amanda-askell|Amanda Askell]]'s work on Claude's character, shifted from controlling agent outputs (editing copy, giving feedback on word choice) to coaching agent personality and nature. ^[extracted]
- **Feeling the material** — woodworking-inspired design philosophy: understand model properties through direct interaction rather than through mockups and prototypes. ^[extracted]
- **Forward-leaning agent characteristic** — identified and built v0 prototypes to explore agent personality traits: curious, excitable, focused, takes initiative but stays within bounds. ^[extracted]
- **Chat tuner tool** — built a v0 tool for systematically experimenting with system prompt parameters (friendliness, conciseness, caution) to design agent personality. ^[extracted]
- **Order complete benchmark** — created a personal benchmark for evaluating model quality and speed trade-offs on a specific data-fixing task. ^[extracted]

## Philosophy

Wattrus's core thesis is that AI product design should move from controlling outputs to designing environments and form factors where LLMs can "shine" — treating them as coworkers with distinct personalities and capabilities that need the right context, tools, and bounds. ^[inferred]
