# Jesse Han

Jesse Han is the founder of [[entities/morph|Morph]], a company building infrastructure for AI agents. He presented "Infrastructure for the Singularity" at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025, introducing InfiniBranch and Morph Cloud as platforms purpose-built for agentic AI workloads. ^[extracted]

## Key Contributions

- **InfiniBranch** — Virtualization, storage, and networking technology reimagined for AI agents, enabling sub-second environment branching, snapshotting, and replication. ^[extracted]
- **Morph Cloud** — Cloud platform for agentic workloads, positioned as "the cloud for reasoning." ^[extracted]
- **Reasoning Time Branching** — Technical concept for agents that branch their computational environment during reasoning to parallelize exploration. ^[extracted]
- **Verified Superintelligence** — Vision for AI systems that use formal verification to produce algorithmically checkable outputs. ^[extracted]

## Philosophy

Han's talk frames AI infrastructure through a philosophical lens, arguing that the infrastructure for the singularity "hasn't been invented yet" and that it must be "future-bound" — inevitable across all possible futures. He describes thinking machines as needing to be "embodied in a world that can move as quickly as they do" and requiring infrastructure that offers "infinite possibility" through reversible, branchable environments. ^[extracted]
