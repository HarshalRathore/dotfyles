# DataQuest

**DataQuest** was an online education startup founded by [[entities/vik-paruchuri|Vik Paruchuri]]. The "data" prefix pattern continued with his later company [[entities/datalab|Datalab]]. ^[extracted]

## Growth and Contraction

DataQuest scaled to 30 people and $4M ARR, bootstrapped during the COVID boom in online education. Post-COVID, when online education "tank[ed]," the company underwent two rounds of layoffs: ^[extracted]

- 30 → 15 employees
- 15 → 7 employees

## The Productivity Paradox

Vik observed that productivity and team happiness actually **increased** a couple of months after both layoff cycles, despite the significant team reduction. This was "obviously awful for the people we had to lay off" but raised a critical question: how could reducing the team so much actually improve productivity? ^[extracted]

This observation became the empirical foundation for Vik's small-team thesis. He developed four hypotheses: ^[extracted]

1. **Specialist silos** — As companies scale, they hire specialists who can't flex across the company to solve key issues
2. **Remote process overhead** — Remote teams require heavy syncing and process that eats into productive time, leading to meeting overload
3. **Senior management burden** — Senior people get tied down managing junior people; a 3-person team cut to 1 actually became more productive by freeing the senior person's time
4. **The golden period pattern** — Companies start aligned and productive, then hire to "fill out the edges" and enter bureaucracy
