# Francois Chollet

Francois Chollet is an AI researcher known for creating the Keras deep learning framework and for his influential 2019 paper "On the Measure of Intelligence." He co-founded the [[entities/arc-prize-foundation|ARC Prize Foundation]] alongside Mike Knup, and created the ARC-AGI benchmark series for measuring artificial general intelligence. ^[extracted]

## Key Contributions

- **"On the Measure of Intelligence" (2019)** — A paper that proposed both a formal definition of intelligence and a benchmark (ARC-AGI-1) to measure it. ^[extracted]
- **Intelligence as skill acquisition efficiency** — Chollet's three-word definition of intelligence captures the ability to learn new skills efficiently, rather than accumulated knowledge or task-specific performance. ^[extracted]
- **ARC-AGI benchmark series** — Created the Abstraction and Reasoning Corpus, which tests AI on novel tasks requiring abstract reasoning without relying on training data or prior knowledge. ^[extracted]
- **ARC Prize Foundation** — Non-profit co-founded to pursue open AGI research and benchmarking. ^[extracted]

## Definition of Intelligence

Chollet's definition — "intelligence is skill acquisition efficiency" — decomposes into two questions: whether a system can learn new things, and how efficiently it can learn them. This definition explicitly distinguishes intelligence from knowledge or memorization: in-distribution performance on seen tasks is not evidence of intelligence. ^[extracted]
