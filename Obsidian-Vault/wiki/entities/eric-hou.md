# Eric Hou

**Eric Hou** is a member of technical staff at [[entities/augment-code|Augment Code]] who delivered the talk "Mentoring the Machine" at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025. ^[extracted]

## Background

Before Augment, Eric spent six years building products and standards for the automotive industry, working on systems where tens of thousands of engineers have contributed and no single person fully understands even 5% of the system. ^[extracted] This experience with large-scale, legacy software shaped his perspective on engineering at production scale. ^[extracted]

## Key Contribution

Eric's AIEF2025 talk "Mentoring the Machine" articulated the core philosophy of how Augment works with AI agents: treating them like junior engineers that need mentoring rather than tools to be commanded. ^[extracted] He demonstrated this through a personal narrative of solving three simultaneous engineering challenges (design system component, staging emergency, new hire mentoring) in roughly half a day of active keyboard time using Augment agents. ^[extracted]

The talk highlighted a gRPC library upgrade that touched 12 services and 20,000 lines of code — work that a normal organization would estimate at three weeks — completed and tested by Augment agents in a single day. ^[extracted]
