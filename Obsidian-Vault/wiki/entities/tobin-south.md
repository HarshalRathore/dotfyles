# Tobin South

Tobin South is a research fellow at Stanford working on safety for AI agents and a member of the team at [[entities/workos|WorkOS]], an enterprise security vendor. He completed his PhD in 2026. His work focuses on providing the authentication, authorization, and security infrastructure that enables AI agents and MCP servers to scale into enterprise production environments.

At AI Engineer 2026, South gave the talk "What Does Enterprise Ready MCP Mean?" — examining the journey from local MCP server demos through authenticated internal deployments to full enterprise-ready MCP serving, covering the security and auth requirements that arise at each stage.

## Known for

- **Enterprise MCP** — Defining what it means to make Model Context Protocol (MCP) production-ready: auth, authorization, SSO, audit logs, DLP, and dynamic client registration management. ^[inferred]
- **AI agent safety research** — Stanford research fellow focused on safety mechanisms for autonomous AI agent workloads.
- **WorkOS security stack** — Building the glue that lets AI labs and AI companies scale their agent deployments into enterprise customers.
