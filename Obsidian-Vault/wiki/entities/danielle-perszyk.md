# Danielle Perszyk

Danielle Perszyk is a cognitive scientist working at the experimental Amazon AGI San Francisco lab. She presented at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 on the concept of "Useful General Intelligence" — AI that makes humans smarter rather than building machines that are smarter than humans. ^[extracted]

## Key Contributions

### Useful General Intelligence
Perszyk proposed UGI as an alternative framing to standard AGI, drawing on [[entities/douglas-engelbart|Douglas Engelbart]]'s vision of computers as tools for augmenting human intelligence rather than replacing it. She argues that general intelligence is inherently social and distributed — it doesn't exist in individuals or models, but emerges through interactions. ^[extracted]

### Techno-Social Co-Evolution
She traces how technologies reshape human cognition in a feedback loop, from language (~6 million years ago) to writing, math, computers, and now AI agents. Each [[concepts/cognitive-technologies|cognitive technology]] builds on the previous one and enables more abstract thought. ^[extracted]

### Agents with Models of Mind
Perszyk argues that for agents to be truly reliable and aligned, they will eventually need models of human minds — the ability to infer what a human wants, not just what they say. ^[extracted]

### Nova Act
She leads the vision for [[entities/nova-act|Nova Act]], Amazon's computer-use agent SDK that grounds agent interactions in a shared visual (browser) environment. ^[extracted]

## Background

As a cognitive scientist, Perszyk brings a neuroscience and evolutionary psychology perspective to AI development. She describes the brain as a "prediction machine" and perception as "controlled hallucination," arguing that hallucinations in AI are features not bugs — the challenge is controlling them. ^[extracted]
