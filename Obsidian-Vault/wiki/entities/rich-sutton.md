# Rich Sutton

AI researcher and reinforcement learning pioneer, known for his influential essay "The Bitter Lesson." The essay argues that general methods that leverage computation have historically outperformed human-engineered approaches across AI domains — and that this pattern will continue. ^[extracted]

## Application to AI UX

[[entities/maximillian-piras|Maximillian Piras]] applies Sutton's Bitter Lesson to product design to derive the [[concepts/bitter-design-lesson|Bitter Design Lesson]]: as long as the basis of competition is inference performance (models are not commoditized), the interface is the commodity that must conform to the next model. This produces the [[concepts/bitter-layout|bitter layout]] — a converged UI pattern optimized for model absorption rather than usability. ^[extracted]
