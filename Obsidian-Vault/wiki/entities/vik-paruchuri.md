# Vik Paruchuri

**Vik Paruchuri** (also known as "Vikas" or "Vik") is CEO of [[entities/datalab|Datalab]], an AI document intelligence company. He is a prominent open-source AI researcher and builder. ^[extracted]

## Background

Vik previously founded [[entities/dataquest|DataQuest]], an online education startup that scaled to 30 people and $4M ARR during the COVID boom. Post-COVID, DataQuest underwent two rounds of layoffs (30→15→7), which Vik observed unexpectedly increased both productivity and team happiness. This experience became the foundation for his thesis on small-team productivity. ^[extracted]

## Datalab

Vik left his AI research job to start Datalab, raised a seed round, and grew revenue 5x since January 2025. Datalab reached seven-figure ARR with customers including tier-one AI labs, universities, Fortune 500 companies, and AI startups like [[entities/gamma|Gamma]]. The team grew from 3 to 4 people (Faraz is the newest hire). ^[extracted]

At Datalab, Vik built and open-sourced [[entities/marker|Marker]] (document processing) and [[entities/surya|Surya OCR]], a state-of-the-art multilingual OCR model. He personally led the end-to-end development of Surya OCR3 alongside research engineer [[entities/tharun|Tharun]] — from customer discovery and paper reading to architecture, data pipeline building, model training, inference code, and customer integration. ^[extracted]

## Philosophy

Vik is a vocal advocate for small AI teams with huge impact. His core thesis: head count does not equal productivity. He argues that reducing team size can increase productivity by eliminating bureaucracy, meeting overload, and the overhead of managing junior staff. He advocates for hiring 15 or fewer generalists, using AI to fill edges, and keeping technology simple. ^[extracted]

He also emphasizes that seniority means maturity, not years of experience — the ability to look at a problem, figure out how to solve it, iterate with customers, and ship without being managed. ^[extracted]

## Hiring Process

At Datalab, the interview process has three steps: (1) a short peer chat to discuss a challenge together, (2) a paid ~10-hour project ($1,000), and (3) a culture fit review. About 40% of interviewed candidates are hired. ^[extracted]

## Personal Notes

Vik stated he "did not get enough sleep" during the Datalab build. He works in person (not remote), believes remote culture makes it harder to distinguish people who ship from people who talk about shipping. He sources candidates through open-source work and Twitter. ^[extracted]
