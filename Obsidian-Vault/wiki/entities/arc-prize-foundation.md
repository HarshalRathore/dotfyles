# ARC Prize Foundation

The ARC Prize Foundation is a non-profit organization with the mission to serve as a "North Star guide towards Open AGI." Founded by [[entities/francois-chollet|Francois Chollet]] and Mike Knup, it is led by president [[entities/greg-kamradt|Greg Kamradt]]. ^[extracted]

## Benchmark Series

The foundation creates and maintains the ARC-AGI (Abstraction and Reasoning Corpus) benchmark series, designed to measure artificial general intelligence through tasks that require abstract reasoning:

- **ARC-AGI-1** — The original benchmark proposed by Chollet in his 2019 paper "On the Measure of Intelligence," featuring single-turn grid transformation tasks. ^[extracted]
- **ARC-AGI-2** — Over 1,000 novel single-turn tasks, each requiring unique skills. No skill is used more than once across tasks, preventing simple memorization. ^[extracted]
- **ARC-AGI-3** — The first interactive reasoning benchmark from the series, moving to game-based environments where agents must explore, infer rules, and complete objectives. Announced at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025. ^[extracted]

## Key Milestones

- **December 2024** — Invited by [[entities/openai|OpenAI]] to co-announce O3 preview results on ARC-AGI via livestream. ^[extracted]
- **400+ human test subjects** — Conducted in-person human testing in San Diego to validate every ARC-AGI task as solvable by people. ^[extracted]
- **Q1 2026 target** — Full launch of ARC-AGI-3 with approximately 120 games. ^[extracted]

## Philosophical Approach

The foundation takes an opinionated approach to AI benchmarking: using humans as the target, creating problems that are feasible for humans but hard for AI. This creates a measurable gap that both quantifies progress and guides research. The benchmarks deliberately eschew language, symbols, and domain-specific knowledge to focus on [[concepts/core-knowledge-priors|core knowledge priors]]. ^[extracted]
