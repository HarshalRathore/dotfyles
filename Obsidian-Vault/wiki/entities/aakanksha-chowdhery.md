# Aakanksha Chowdhery

**Aakanksha Chowdhery** is a machine learning researcher who has been at the center of the LLM revolution from scaling laws through autonomous coding.

## Career

- **Google** (6+ years): Led research for [[concepts/palm-model|PALM]] and was a lead researcher on [[concepts/gemini-model|Gemini]]
- **Reflection.ai** (current): Working on pushing the frontier for autonomous coding with reinforcement learning

## Key Contributions

### PALM and Chain-of-Thought

Aakanksha's work on PALM (a 540-billion-parameter model) demonstrated that chain-of-thought reasoning — asking models to output their reasoning chains before answering — dramatically improves performance on math word problems and generalizes across domains. ^[extracted]

The PALM results showed that the solve rate on middle school math word problems increased substantially when models were prompted to show their reasoning, and this capability emerged only at large scale. ^[extracted]

### Autonomous Coding with RL

Currently at Reflection.ai, Aakanksha is applying reinforcement learning to autonomous coding — pushing models to not just generate code but to iteratively improve it through reasoning and self-evaluation at inference time. ^[extracted]

## Perspective at AIEF2025

In her talk "RL for Autonomous Coding," Aakanksha traced the arc of LLM progress from the 2020 scaling laws paper through chain-of-thought emergence, instruction following, RLHF, and into the current era of inference-time compute scaling — where models improve their outputs by generating multiple samples, performing majority voting, and sequentially revising their answers. ^[extracted]
