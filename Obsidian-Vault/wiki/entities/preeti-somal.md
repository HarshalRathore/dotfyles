# Preeti Somal

Engineer at [[entities/temporal|Temporal]], a platform for reliability and scalability in distributed applications. Presented at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 on the topic of building AI agents that are reliable, observable, and production-ready using Temporal's workflow orchestration platform. ^[extracted]

## Role at Temporal

Part of the engineering team at Temporal, a company whose mascot is a tardigrade (water bear) — chosen as "the most resilient animal known to humankind" to symbolize Temporal's focus on reliability. ^[extracted]

## Conference Presentation

At AI Engineer World's Fair 2025, presented "Scaling AI Agents Without Breaking Reliability" covering:

- The fundamental unreliability of LLM calls in agent applications ^[extracted]
- How agents are complex distributed systems needing orchestration ^[extracted]
- Temporal's workflow abstraction (workflows, signals, queries, activities) for building agent applications ^[extracted]
- The elimination of "plumbing code" (manual retry, error handling, state management) from business logic ^[extracted]
- Real-world customer use cases ([[entities/dust|Dust]], Gorgias) running AI agents on Temporal in production ^[extracted]

## Key Message

Argues that agent reliability is best achieved not by improving individual LLM reliability but by outsourcing the reliability and scalability of the overall distributed system to a platform like Temporal. ^[extracted] The core insight is that "complex systems need orchestration" and Temporal handles the "plumbing code" so developers focus on business logic. ^[extracted]
