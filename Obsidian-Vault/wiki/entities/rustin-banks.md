# Rustin Banks

Rustin Banks is a product manager at Google Labs, leading the product strategy for **Jules**, Google's asynchronous coding agent. He spoke at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 on parallel agent workflows and the future of coding agents.

## Background

Banks is an engineer at heart with a deep love for text-based interfaces dating back to his early computing days:
- Built his first compiler with **Borland C++ 3.1**, delivered on 10 5.25-inch floppy disks ordered from AOL classifieds ^[extracted]
- Hosted a **bulletin board system** out of his parents' closet using salvaged computers ^[extracted]
- Recognized the text-based interface pattern when AI coding tools first emerged, connecting it to his BBS experience ^[extracted]
- Was an early adopter of **ChatGPT 3.5**, noting how slow it was compared to today's standards ^[extracted]

His career trajectory led him to dedicate himself to AI coding, culminating in his current role managing Jules.

## Role at Google Labs

As product manager for Jules, Banks oversees the development and public launch of Google's asynchronous coding agent. Jules was launched "to everyone, everywhere, all at once, for free" at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025. ^[extracted]

## Key Contributions

At AIEF2025, Banks presented:
- The **parallel agent workflow** model — two types of parallelism: multitasking (multiple backlog items at once) and multiple variations (same task, different approaches) ^[extracted]
- A live demo of Jules building features on a conference schedule website, including test generation, calendar integration, accessibility audits, and Lighthouse score improvements ^[extracted]
- The principle that the secret to parallel agent success is a **clear definition of success** — test suites and coverage metrics serve as the arbiter ^[extracted]
