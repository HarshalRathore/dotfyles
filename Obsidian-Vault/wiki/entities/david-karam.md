# David Karam

**David Karam** is a practitioner in AI evaluation and metrics, currently at [[entities/pi-labs|Pi Labs]]. Previously worked at [[entities/google|Google]] on [[concepts/search-engine-relevance|Search]], where he spent over a decade building and evaluating search systems. ^[extracted]

## Expertise

Karam co-presented the workshop "[[references/aief2025-building-metrics-that-actually-work-david-karam-pi-labs|Building Metrics that actually work]]" at [[entities/aief2025|AIEF2025]], drawing on his Google experience with stochastic systems evaluation. His core thesis is that evaluation methodology — not tooling — is the primary lever for building reliable AI systems. ^[extracted]

## Google Search Background

At Google, Karam worked on search systems that were inherently stochastic, making evaluation a central engineering effort rather than an afterthought. ^[extracted] The majority of Google's effort in search went into evaluation and improvement, a pattern he now brings to Pi Labs. ^[extracted]

## AIEF2025: Layering RAG Techniques

Karam presented "Layering every technique in RAG, one query at a time" at AIEF2025, where he introduced a practical framework for improving RAG system quality through a quality engineering loop. He cataloged RAG techniques from in-memory retrieval through cross-encoder re-ranking, emphasizing that teams should start with outcomes and loss analysis before selecting techniques. Key contributions include the complexity-adjusted impact framework, the distinction between keyword-style and natural-language query patterns, and the insight that relevance is a proxy metric that does not equal ranking quality. ^[extracted]
