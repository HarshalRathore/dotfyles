# Jeremy Howard

**Jeremy Howard** is an entrepreneur and AI researcher who founded [[entities/answer-ai|Answer AI]]. He is known for his work in fast.ai and for advocating a radically different approach to company building. ^[extracted]

## Philosophy on Small Teams

Howard's company-building philosophy, which [[entities/vik-paruchuri|Vik Paruchuri]] studied while working with him, centers on: ^[extracted]

1. **Hire fewer than 15 generalists** — People who can work across the full stack and understand all aspects of the company, fill in edges with AI and internal tooling
2. **Use simple, boring technology** — No Kubernetes clusters for a three-person company; prefer straightforward solutions like shell scripts
3. **Invest in block libraries** — Howard has invested in tools like fast HTML and Monster UI as reusable building blocks for company tooling

This philosophy requires: ^[extracted]

- Engineers who talk to customers
- Go-to-market people who actually build
- High trust — people in it for building something together, not politics or personal advancement
- Everyone focusing on customers

## Influence on Datalab

Vik Paruchuri explicitly credits learning Howard's philosophy while working with him at Answer AI. Datalab's operating model — 3-4 person team, generalists, simple tech stack, AI-filled edges — directly reflects Howard's approach. ^[extracted]
