## Andre Karpathy

Andre Karpathy is an AI researcher who has worked at Google, OpenAI, Tesla, xAI, and Eureka Labs (his new educational platform). He is widely recognized as a YouTube educator who produces highly accessible talks explaining how AI and machine learning work for general audiences.

### Key Contributions

He coined the term **Vibe Coding** a few months before the AIEF2025 talk, and the concept has since gained widespread adoption in the developer community. He subscribes to the idea that "the hottest new programming language is English" — a provocative take on the trajectory of AI-assisted development.

He also wrote the **Software 2.0 manifesto** seven years before this talk, predicting a world where machine learning models would write code for us and be better at it than humans. This prediction has largely come to pass.

### Menugen

Karpathy recently created **Menugen** at a hackathon as a vibe coding experiment — a web app that takes photos of restaurant menus and generates image representations of the menu contents. He described building it locally as "an exhilarating and fun escapade" but deploying it as "a bit of a painful slog." The project also served as an implicit critique of the developer experience challenges across major platforms including Replicate, OpenAI, and Vercel.

### GitHub

Zeke Sikelianos (the AIEF2025 speaker) notes that a GitHub repo corresponds to Karpathy's slides, allowing audiences to track down URLs and details mentioned in his talks.

### Karpathy's Rethink of Vibe Coding

Friedman (Qodo) highlighted Karpathy's own rethinking of vibe coding as evidence that the field is maturing. Karpathy has contrasted "code I actually and professionally care about" (maintainable, quality code) with "vibe code" — acknowledging that vibe coding alone doesn't produce production-grade software. This self-critique from the person who coined the term carries significant weight in the developer community. ^[extracted]
