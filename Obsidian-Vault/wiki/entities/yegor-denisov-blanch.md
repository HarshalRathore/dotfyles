# Yegor Denisov-Blanch

**Yegor Denisov-Blanch** is a researcher at [[entities/stanford|Stanford University]] specializing in data-driven decision making and software engineering. He spoke at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 on the largest study ever conducted on AI's impact on developer productivity.

## Background

- At Stanford since 2022
- Focus: data-driven decision making and software engineering
- Previous role: led digital transformation for a large company with thousands of engineers
- Part of a research team including [[entities/professor-kaczynski|Professor Kaczynski]] (Cambridge Analytica whistleblower)

## Stanford AI Productivity Study

Yegor leads one of the largest studies on software engineering productivity:
- 100,000+ software engineers across 600+ companies
- Dozens of millions of commits, billions of lines of code
- Private repository data for accurate team-level measurement
- Time-series and cross-sectional design
- Automated productivity measurement model based on Git analysis

## Key Findings Presented

- AI coding tools produce a 15–20% net productivity boost
- Significant rework costs mask true gains (30–40% raw output increase)
- Ghost engineers: ~10% of software engineers do essentially no work
- Self-reported productivity surveys are poor predictors (~30 percentile point error)
