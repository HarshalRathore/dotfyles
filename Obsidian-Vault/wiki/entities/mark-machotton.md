Mark Machotton is the Enterprise AI Architect at [[entities/los-alamos-national-laboratory|Los Alamos National Laboratory]], where he oversees [[entities/venato-supercomputer|Venato]] and drives the lab's approach to [[concepts/agentic-science|agentic science]] and [[concepts/controlled-classified-ai|classified AI systems]]. He has been with LANL since the Manhattan Project era and has witnessed the laboratory's evolution from running "Los Alamos Chess" on the Maniac I supercomputer in 1956 to deploying agentic AI systems that execute code on HPC assets for nuclear stockpile stewardship.

Machotton's perspective is shaped by LANL's 70-year history of applied statistics and machine learning — predating modern AI by decades. He emphasizes that for national security labs, AI is not a novelty but a continuation of a long tradition of computational science. His talk at AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 covered LANL's approach to agentic AI, the Venato supercomputer partnership with NVIDIA and HPE, and the unique governance challenges of running AI on classified data.

He frames LANL's role as "a safe place to do dangerous things," making the laboratory a trusted partner for frontier AI labs that cannot freely share data or hire talent. This positioning has led to partnerships with OpenAI for chem/bio safety work and the deployment of OpenAI's models on LANL's classified networks via Venato.
