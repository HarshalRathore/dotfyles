# Granola

Granola is an AI note-taking product that entered a crowded market already occupied by Fathom, Otter, Fireflies, and AI-native capabilities from Zoom and Google Meet. Despite this, it achieved significant traction, including approximately $50 million in funding and broad adoption among AI practitioners. ^[extracted]

## Competitive Differentiation

While existing AI note-takers approached the problem as "the product will replace the full job of taking notes," Granola identified an unmet customer need: users wanted help taking better notes themselves, not to have note-taking fully automated. ^[extracted]

## Lego Blocks Assembly

Granola exemplifies the [[concepts/ai-lego-blocks-framework|AI Lego Blocks Framework]]: ^[extracted]

**AI Capabilities (Commodity):**
- DeepGram for transcription
- Anthropic and OpenAI for language processing
- No custom models or unique training infrastructure

**Proprietary Data:**
- Combines user's own handwritten notes with AI-generated transcription
- Context includes both the notes the user takes and the transcription generated
- Enhanced notes build a repository enabling downstream features (chat across meetings, project workspaces)

**Product Functionality:**
- Mac app detects when meetings start
- Accesses system sound for transcription
- Calendar integration for meeting metadata (attendees, titles)

## Sequencing Evolution

Granola has continued sequencing new capabilities: ^[extracted]

- Project and team workspaces
- Downstream actions (CRM integration with HubSpot)
- Experimental company wiki that auto-updates itself
