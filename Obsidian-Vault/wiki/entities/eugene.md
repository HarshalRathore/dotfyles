# Eugene

Eugene is the founder of Federalist AI, a research lab building reliable personalized AGI. He spoke at the AI Engineer World's Fair 2025 as part of the HF0 Residency showcase.

## Background

Eugene built Federalist AI around the conviction that **reliability is revenue**. His core argument: the models are already smart enough for PhD-level math, but production applications need models reliable enough to book airline tickets, sort emails, or file taxes and invoices ^[extracted].

## At Federalist AI

Eugene presented CoreKey 72B — the world's largest model without transformer attention, running on only 8 GPUs with 1000x lower inference cost — and claimed their action R1 agent beats Claude Sonnet, Gemini, and OpenAI on task completion reliability ^[extracted]. He cited the "DoorDash analogy": a 45% success rate is unacceptable for consumer applications ^[extracted].
