# Amir Haghighat

Amir Haghighat is co-founder and CTO of [[entities/baseten|Baseten]], an inference company. He has tracked enterprise AI adoption patterns over hundreds of conversations with CIOs, CTOs, and AI leaders at Fortune 50 companies over two years. He describes his vantage point as selling "very horizontal AI tooling" as opposed to verticalized AI solutions, giving him a broad view of enterprise AI strategy. ^[extracted]

## Enterprise AI Adoption Thesis

Haghighat argues that the true value of AI in enterprise will only be unlocked once enterprises move from buying verticalized AI products (AI for sales, AI for customer service) to actually building with AI themselves. He draws an analogy to the 2000s: if enterprises had only bought Salesforce and never built their own tech, Snowflake, Databricks, and Datadog would not exist. ^[extracted]

He identifies a progression: enterprises start with closed frontier models ([[entities/openai|OpenAI]], [[entities/anthropic|Anthropic]]) via dedicated deployments on Azure or AWS for security and privacy, then later discover "cracks" in the assumption that they can rely on these indefinitely. ^[extracted]

## Key Observations

- **2023**: Enterprises were "toying around" with AI — CIOs described dedicated deployments of GPT-4 dismissively as a way for engineers to experiment. ^[extracted]
- **2024**: 40-50% of 100+ enterprises had something in production, still on closed models. ^[extracted]
- **2025**: "Palpable" shift — cracks appearing in the closed-model assumption, driven by quality, latency, unit economics, and strategic differentiation (destiny). ^[extracted]
