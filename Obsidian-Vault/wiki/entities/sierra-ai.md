# Sierra

Sierra is an AI customer service platform cofounded by Bret Taylor (OpenAI chairman) and [[entities/clay-bavor|Clay Bavor]] (former Google VP of AR/VR and Lens). It is an example of the [[concepts/ai-leapfrog-effect|AI leapfrog effect]] — a traditionally low-tech industry (customer service) adopting AI faster than more tech-forward sectors.

## Traction

- Resolves 70% of customer service queries for its customers ^[extracted]
- Serves major brands including SiriusXM, ADT, Chubbies, and one of the largest US mortgage originators ^[extracted]
- Hundreds of customers; will serve hundreds of millions of consumers in 2025 ^[extracted]

## AgentOS Platform

Sierra provides two coexisting layers for building customer-facing agents: ^[extracted]

- **AgentOS** — A sophisticated toolkit for engineering teams to build complex agents, handling the "under the surface" complexity of production agent systems ^[extracted]
- **No-code tools** — Enable non-technical users (CX teams, brand managers) to build, refine, coach, and update the agent without engineering involvement ^[extracted]

The two layers "seamlessly interoperate" — engineers handle the deep technical work while CX teams manage the agent's behavior and brand voice. ^[extracted]

## Agent Development Lifecycle (ADLC)

Sierra invented the [[concepts/agent-development-lifecycle|Agent Development Lifecycle]], a new SDLC for probabilistic AI software. Key innovations:

- **User simulation testing** — Running hundreds of thousands of simulated conversations with diverse personas before going live ^[extracted]
- **Closed-loop improvement** — Tools for human coaching teams to continuously improve agent performance in production ^[extracted]
- **Capability-boundary detection** — The agent detects when it is "beyond its abilities" and hands off to a human ^[extracted]

## Significance

Sarah Guo uses Sierra as evidence against the "thin wrapper" criticism — if Bret Taylor (OpenAI chairman) is building a customer service AI company, the people closest to the model technology see huge value at the application layer. ^[inferred]

Bavor articulates a broader vision: "every company in the future is going to have an agent, its own branded customer-facing agent" — describing it as "what comes after the website, what comes after the mobile app." ^[extracted]
