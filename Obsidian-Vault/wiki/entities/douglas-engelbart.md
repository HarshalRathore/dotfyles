# Douglas Engelbart

Douglas Engelbart was a computer scientist and inventor, best known for inventing the computer mouse and developing early graphical user interfaces. He is cited by [[entities/danielle-perszyk|Danielle Perszyk]] (Amazon AGI) as representing an alternative vision for AI: "He didn't care so much about thinking machines and solving intelligence. What he cared about was thinking humans and augmenting our intelligence." ^[extracted]

## Augmenting Human Intelligence

Engelbart's 1962 paper "Augmenting Human Intellect" proposed that computers could make humans smarter — a fundamentally different goal from the Dartmouth conference's ambition of building machines that think like humans. Perszyk frames this as a category error in how we think about AGI: ^[extracted]

> "Engelbart actually hinted at this. He said, in a very real sense, as represented by the steady evolution of our augmentation means, the development of artificial intelligence has been going on for centuries." ^[extracted]

## Legacy

- Invented the computer mouse (patented in 1967)
- Developed early hypertext systems (NLS, oNLine System)
- Pioneered collaborative computing (groupware)
- Advanced the concept of intelligence augmentation (IA) as distinct from artificial intelligence (AI)
