# J.C.R. Licklider

J.C.R. Licklider (1915–1990) was an American computer scientist who wrote one of the most influential computing papers, "Man-Computer Symbiosis" (1960). His work envisioned a future where computers would serve as collaborative partners to human intellect.

## "Man-Computer Symbiosis" (1960)

The paper envisioned an assistant that knows everything you're working on, has perfect context, and can help with anything immediately. Licklider wrote this at a time when the epitome of computing technology was vacuum tube computers and punch cards — programs took hours or days to run. ^[extracted]

## Modern Relevance

[[entities/sharif-shameem|Sharif Shameem]] argues that we have largely failed to fulfill Licklider's vision. Modern tools like ChatGPT still require starting a "new chat" with no memory of previous conversations. He frames fulfilling this vision as a moral obligation for today's AI engineers, given the extraordinary tools (like Claude Opus 4) at our disposal compared to what Licklider had. ^[extracted]
